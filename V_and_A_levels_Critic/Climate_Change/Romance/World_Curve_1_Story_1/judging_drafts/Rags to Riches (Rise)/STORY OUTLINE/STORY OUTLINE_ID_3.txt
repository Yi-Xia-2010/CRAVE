## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
A mid-sized coastal metropolis, ten to twenty years from now, tilts between floodplain and retrofit. Repeated storm surges have re-stitched districts into islands of raised walkways and patched pilings; municipal budgets groan and private developers promise rapid fixes. The political center has become a battleground between pragmatic top-down engineering and grassroots eco-architectural practice. Religion and class recede into the background here; the dominant public faith is in technical solutions and legacy wealth, while hope lives in DIY co-ops and living roofs.

The Harborfront District—my neighborhood—smells of kelp and diesel; communities have organized around shared pumps, sandbag brigades, and seed exchanges. Elliot’s Green Spire studio and City Hall’s council chambers are antagonistic poles: one of moss-filtered optimism and reclaimed glass, the other of polished protocols and ledgered compromises. Climate science, led by figures like Dr. Noor Patel, supplies data that both constrains and inspires, while developers like Luca Marin translate risk into glossy renderings.

The atmosphere is low-saturation cinematic: weathered metal, living moss, wet reflections, soft, salted light. Politics revolve around funding schemes, redevelopment deals, and the moral calculus of who stays and who is moved. Intimacy grows from technical labor and shared risk: romance and resilience are braided, each political choice shaping private commitments and communal futures.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Rios) race to secure community-led adaptive defenses before the next storm season. My scars—loss of my brother, institutional betrayal—have made me fiercely self-reliant. Elliot Chen, inventive and plant-obsessed, offers a living seawall prototype that could prove the neighborhood’s capacity for grassroots resilience. Irene Voss, the council leader, touts a developer-funded project promising quick protection and jobs but threatens displacement. Sami organizes bold direct actions; Dr. Noor brings data to hearings; Luca sells the allure of scale.

The narrative is a climb: starting from skeptical restraint and bureaucratic frustration, I must decide how to balance coalition-building, tactical resistance, and technical compromise. Branch points push me toward collaboration with Elliot, confrontation with Irene, or grassroots decentralization. Each decision yields distinct outcomes: negotiated hybrid victory, partial success with costs, political defeat, or a morally complex federated survival. Romance with Elliot deepens through shared design and risk, but whether it resolves depends on my willingness to trust and to let the community shape technical choices. The story follows a Rags-to-Riches emotional arc: progress and increasing hope unfold across routes, with the highest emotional intensity arriving at each route’s climactic turning point.

Notes on Navigation and Emotional Curve:
- The story uses a tree-like branching structure with split hubs at Chapters 3, 5, 8, and 12. Each chapter (IDs 1–15) has a unique parent except Chapter 1. All branches from Chapter 1 to any Ending have at most six chapters.
- Valence and Arousal are assigned to ensure each path follows a rising emotional arc: starting from negative or neutral ground, gaining momentum through collaboration, pressure, or quiet federation, and culminating in scenes of high emotional intensity and decision.
- Visual descriptions strictly reference provided character designs and location aesthetics (wet reflections, waxed canvas jacket, Elliot’s smudged drafting glasses, Irene’s trench coat, Sami’s neon lifevest, Dr. Noor’s folio).
- POV remains first-person throughout chapter summaries: "I" observations, inner decision-making, tactile detail, and emotional interiority drive the narrative.
