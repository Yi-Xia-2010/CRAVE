## Visual Novel Title
Tides Between Us

---

## World View
Puerto Alba is a small coastal town in the near future (2030s), carved into a mid-latitude shore where sea-level rise and ferocious seasonal storms have rewritten daily life. Municipal authority is pragmatic but strained; local councils scramble to balance heritage fishing livelihoods and incoming proposals from ethical-tech collectives and profit-driven investors. The culture blends old maritime lore — told at markets and family tables — with a brittle modernity: sensor-equipped seawalls, elevated walkways, and salvage-built labs. Religion is local and syncretic: a tangle of saintly votives in fishing huts and ritual respect for tidal cycles. Class lines are subtle: long-time fishers and market vendors, municipal administrators, and a new class of tech workers and sympathetic engineers. Geography is vivid and constraining — the marketboard, a salt-stained lab by the estuary, a cliffside promenade for storm-watching, a municipal council hall, a nascent green-prototype shore, and an abandoned lighthouse where private conversations erupt into confessions. Atmosphere is painterly coastal realism: wind-worn textures, salt-streaked colors, fogged light and cinematic contrasts. Politics are urgent: short-term survival versus community stewardship; bargains with outside investors versus grassroots resistance. On every street, the sea's encroachment is both literal and symbolic, forcing choices that test love, loyalty, and authority.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Serrano, a local organizer and former marine-biology student. Puerto Alba faces an urgent survival question: accept a rapid, investor-backed structural solution championed by Council leader Iris Varela or build a slower, community-centered hybrid of marsh restoration and modular defenses proposed by Elias Park and his ethical-tech colleagues. The narrative is an Oedipus arc (Fall → Rise → Fall): I fall into crisis as storms and political discord reveal painful losses and misjudgments; I briefly help broker hope through collaboration with Elias and local knowledge, achieving temporary wins; but the final act collapses into hard costs and moral sacrifice, producing endings that vary by choice—ranging from a fragile communal victory, to bittersweet survival, to ruin and loss, to a slow stewardship that asks a personal sacrifice. Romance with Elias grows through shared fieldwork and repair nights — his salt-flecked hair, amber-brown eyes, and gentle engineering optimism contrast with Iris's steel-blue, calculating intensity. Secondary figures — Mateo's steady hands, Abuela Rosa's tidal lore, Sofía's activist fire, and Nima's technical realism — color the stakes. Every branch obeys the same emotional curve: we fall, taste a rise of promise, and descend again; what changes is what is lost and what I must carry.
