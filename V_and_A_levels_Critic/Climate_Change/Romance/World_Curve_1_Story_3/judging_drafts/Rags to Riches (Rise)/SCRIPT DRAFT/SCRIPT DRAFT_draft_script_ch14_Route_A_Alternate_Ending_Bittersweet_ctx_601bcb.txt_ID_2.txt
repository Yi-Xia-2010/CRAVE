You come to the lighthouse because it is a place that remembers storms and also remembers people. The lamp room holds air that tastes like history and wet wool. The barometer is warm from your palm; you set it on the sill and watch the needle hold its own small truth.

Your nights blur into a rhythm: meetings, mediations, press calls, late-hour visits to looming porches.
