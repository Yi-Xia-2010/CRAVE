## Visual Novel Title
Tidewrights of Maris Bay

---

## World View
Near-future Maris Bay is a small coastal town squeezed between sea and stubborn memory. Once a sleepy fishing enclave, it now corrals seasonal superstorms, rising tides, and a steady trickle of climate migrants. Power and capital are scarce; pragmatic technologies — solar textiles, modular flood barriers, rooftop reedbeds, and hand-built desalination rigs — are layered atop vernacular wooden cottages. The political landscape is a battleground between large private investment and community-led ecological repair. Irene Vale champions a high-profile harbor redevelopment to bring jobs and engineered stability; Maya Alvarez and grassroots organizers champion nature-based adaptation that preserves saltmarshes and cultural continuity. Local institutions are thin: a municipal council led by Mayor Lionel Park, a scientist mentor in Dr. Sora Kim, memory-keepers like Captain Reyes, and mobilizers like Tala Ruiz. The culture is stitched from reciprocity and memory — seed exchanges, rooftop nurseries, late-night council debates — but is frayed by economic urgency. Geography is intimate: the Boardwalk, the Verdant Rooftop Nursery, the Saltmarsh Labyrinth, the Glass Harbor site, and the Stormcliff Lighthouse. The atmosphere is watercolor-wet: salt stains, moss, rust, and low sun, where every choice about land, love, and survival is both practical and moral.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I tell the story in the first person as Maya Alvarez, a local ecological designer who returns to the Bay determined to protect her town. The narrative is a rise: from anxiety and constrained resources toward increasing agency, community success, and personal connection. Early chapters establish the stakes: a planned Glass Harbor redevelopment led by Irene Vale, desperate economic pressure, and the fragile marshes that buffer storms. I must choose how to act — support Evan Kaito’s grassroots prototypes, negotiate with Irene through Mayor Park, or escalate with direct action. Each major choice branches into a short, focused route building toward a six-step arc: gathering evidence, mobilizing neighbors, public confrontation, technical trials, and a climax at the Council House, Saltmarsh, or Harbor site. Along the way, romance with Evan deepens or frays depending on my willingness to accept help and compromise. Outcomes vary (happy, bittersweet, sad, or a complex “true” ending), but emotionally the story rises: each path strengthens me, the community gains more capacity, and the town’s fate is decided through pragmatic repair, moral reckoning, or painful loss.
