You expected the harbor to be a place of sentence and standoff—Jonah avoiding eye contact, the cooperative split into those who trusted you and those who could not. For a time, he kept his distance. That absence was a pressure you carried like a stone. But the town moves on its own momentum, and wounds that could be tended are tended.

Jonah: (He speaks when the night is thinning into a cold, honest gray; his hands move competent and small as he rethreads a section of net.) "We lost good wood. We lost rope that knows a thousand turns. It hurts."
Maia Rivera: (You answer by handing him a new spool, the scent of tar and sea on it.) "We can make rope that remembers the old turns. It will be different. We'll learn the new ones."
Jonah: (He looks up, sea-glass eyes narrowed—soft, guarded.) "Different is not always bad. Just—don't ask me to pretend nothing was taken." (He tests a knot, fingers working with a practiced economy.) "And don't ask me for more than I'm willing to give."

The conversation stretches into the cold, each line an instrument in a duet of repair. You test apologies; he returns small pragmatic counters that are not rejections but boundaries. The harbor offers work as an olive branch: nets to be mended, hulls scraped, an entire small fleet to be coaxed back into seaworthiness.

Jonah: "This is where I belong, Maia. I'm with the bay first. If we can find ways to keep her healthy without losing the people who read her, I'll be here."
Maia Rivera: (You listen, counting breaths like beads. The ache in your chest doesn't vanish; it rearranges.) "Then be here. Help us design the co-op oversight. Teach me the knots I don't know."

Time has bulged and folded itself into a series of small victories and stubborn setbacks. Night fell and the town kept moving. The memorial for the boathouse went as Tomas led it—stories spoken under a canopy of taped lights, wood shavings scattered like confetti for something sacred. You keep a catalogue of wrongs in your chest—lists of compromised shoals, altered fishings, promises that bent under pressure. Guilt is a constant weather pattern.
