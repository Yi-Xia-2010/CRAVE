## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Marisol

---

## World View
Marisol Bay is a small coastal town in the near-future (circa 2040) where sea-level rise, stronger storms, and shifting fisheries have tightened every personal choice with political consequence. The town balances nostalgia and anxious urgency: fog-laced mornings, golden twilights stained with haze, boardwalk meetings under buzzing fluorescents, and scaffolding hugging sand-drenched edges. Power is distributed between a cautious mayor, a proud fishing cooperative, grassroots organizers, and incoming climate-tech firms with deep pockets. Culture prizes shared labor, maritime rituals, and intergenerational craft passed down along docks and rooftops. Religion and ritual are secularized into memorials for past storms; civic meetings and petitions take the place of altars. Class hierarchies emerge through access to capital: small-scale fishers and restoration volunteers risk livelihood while developers and project managers promise infrastructure and jobs. Geography is intimate—muddy wetlands, a lived-in harbor, a flooded historic quarter, and a rooftop garden that keeps memory and rosemary alive. Political conflict centers on whether to pursue community-led adaptation, accept corporate-scale engineered defenses, or find a hybrid that preserves autonomy. Atmosphere: salt and grit, earnest faces, and the persistent rattle of tide against human will.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I play Maia Rivera, a coastal ecologist driven by guilt and care, trying to shepherd Marisol Bay through an existential choice: local stewardship, corporate engineering, or principled resistance that risks short-term aid. The narrative follows a Man-in-a-Hole emotional arc: initial equilibrium slides into a crisis (a town hall showdown and a deep betrayal or miscalculation), I hit a personal and communal low—relationships fray and a storm threat or political compromise deepens stakes—then I work to climb back, building partnerships, making hard moral choices, and healing intimate wounds. Love is the engine: Jonah Kade embodies rooted, tradition-first stewardship; Elias Wren offers systemic technological solutions and career-grade charisma; supporting figures like Mayor Rosa, Ava, Tomas, Lupe, and Dana press and prod my decisions. Throughout, visuals keep coastal realism: Maia’s salt-streaked chestnut hair and battered Moleskine, Jonah’s wind-tossed sandy blond hair and fisher’s ring, Elias’s slicked black hair and AR lapel pin. Branching choices force me to weigh local sovereignty against large-scale safety, and each route (community-led, corporate partnership, whistleblowing truth, and fractured compromise) resolves with different romantic, political, and ethical textures while always adhering to the Fall→Rise emotional curve: we fall into crisis, hit a low, then claw toward recovery.
