Elias: (lowering his voice) "You know I admire it. I do. But admiration and survival are different currencies. I—" (he rubs his thumb along the scar on his knuckle) "—I don't want them to take you apart in public."

You: "They already have." (The words come out thinner than you expected.) "They're rewriting my whole record. They're saying I betrayed my profession."

Elias: "Maybe you did. Or maybe you betrayed the comfortable lie that was going to cost us everything later." (He searches your face.) "Either way, I'm here."

You: (long exhale) "That matters."

Mayor Ana: "Transparency is painful," (she says, voice firm but frayed) "but it is necessary. We will investigate. We will involve independent counsel."

You: (in the crowd, when a reporter's microphone nudges you) "We were offered a choice that asked some neighborhoods to bear the cost of a showcase. The town deserves to know who is choosing for whom."

You: "I expected resistance. I did not expect the kind of personal attacks that aim to drown the facts in noise."

[Scene: Tidewatch Pier | Evening — Protest and Protection]

[Music: Chaotic strings, cymbals; voices overlap]
[Sound: Shouts, chants, a single, sharp whistle cut through the noise]

The town erupts into a thousand conflicting movements. Some demonstrators demand resignations and accountability for corrupt deals. Others accuse you of sabotage because they fear economic collapse if investors pull out. Small businesses that had planned on grants watch bank accounts freeze. The co-op's seed fund stalls. The smell of wet cardboard and coffee is everywhere.

Samir stands at the edge of the crowd with his camera, capturing faces: relief, fury, fear — documentary evidence of a community being forced to choose in public. Later, footage goes viral — your exchange with Maren, the mayor's strained smile, Elias' furious whisper to you. The online mobs pick at every seam.

A legal notice is delivered to your door by courier at dusk — a suit alleging breach of contract and defamation, with an immediate injunction request. For a moment, you float, as if looking down onto the scene from above. Your name in the headline is a kind of acid.
