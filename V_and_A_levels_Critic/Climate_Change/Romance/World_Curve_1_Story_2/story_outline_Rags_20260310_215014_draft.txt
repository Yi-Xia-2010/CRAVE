## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Hearts

---

## World View
Mariselle, 2038: a coastal archipelago region where the sea has grown theatrical and uninvited. Once-thriving boardwalks and narrow harbor streets are now stitched with tide barriers, shabby hoists for salvaged windows, and community gardens planted atop repaired dunes. Politics are a careful ledger of funding bids, public hearings and press conferences; technocrats and developers wield engineering diagrams and glossy projections, while grassroots organizers carry handmade banners and salt-stiffened flyers. The municipal government is a patchwork of Development Authority contracts and emergency ordinances; the town’s religion is pragmatic stewardship—rituals of repair, shared storm watches, and memorials for losses. Class divides run along waterfront property lines and access to retrofit subsidization; migrant fisher families and co-op volunteers keep local foodways alive while private capital, represented by developers, pushes verticalized housing and gated sea walls. Geography is a ribbon of boardwalks, flooded alleys, reclaimed wetlands and a low-slung research institute half-buried in dunes. Atmosphere: humid, salt-scraped, with sudden toxic-yellow sunsets and cinematic fog. Everyday tech is weatherworn—solar canopies patched with tarps, desal plants humming, barometric sensors grafted onto fitness-watches. The central tension: balancing immediate human attachment to place and culture with the cold calculus of engineering solutions.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Ari Navarro, a coastal planner returned home to Mariselle after loss and exile. The narrative arcs upward: beginning in grief and institutional failure, you rebuild agency, alliances, and love. The external stakes are rising tides, wavering municipal votes, and a looming developer-backed mega-project; the internal crucible is your guilt over a sibling lost to a flood and an aversion to intimacy that risks keeping you alone. Choices force a tension between grassroots activism (Sora), technocratic cooperation (Lina), and the corrosive lure of private capital (Ephraim). Across divergent routes you negotiate trust, policy compromises, and acts of public repair—each path reframes what “saving the town” means and what kind of relationship you can sustain. The emotional arc is Rags to Riches: incremental gains, community wins, and romantic blossoming as control and hope return. Endings range from a warm grassroots partnership (Happy), to a bittersweet technocratic compromise (Bittersweet), to a painful loss (Sad), to a realistic hybrid governance model that redefines love and duty (True). The story is first-person, intimate, and tactile—salt on your boots, the chipped enamel pin at your collar, Sora’s wind-tugged bandana, Lina’s precise platinum bob under rainlight.
