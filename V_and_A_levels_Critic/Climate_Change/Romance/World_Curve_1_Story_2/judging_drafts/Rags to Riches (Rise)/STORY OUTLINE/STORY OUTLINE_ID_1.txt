## Visual Novel Title
Mariselle Rising

---

## World View
Mariselle is a near-future coastal region in 2038 that feels like a place caught between tides—literal and political. Once-thriving fishing piers and seaside promenades are scarred by 'supertides' and seasonal storms; buildings wear salt like a second skin. The region is governed by a layered municipal-regional system in which the Development Authority, Municipal Council, and community cooperatives jockey for funding and authority. Class divisions map to shoreline lines: long-time fishers and co-op members cling to endangered neighborhoods while developers and investors push inland vertical housing and gated desal hubs. Religion and ritual are present but diffuse—a coastal folk spirituality of offerings to tides and communal memorials rather than doctrinal faith. Culture centers on resiliency craftsmanship: wetland gardening, tide knowledge, and seed libraries are political acts as well as livelihoods. Technology is pragmatic—solar canopies, tide barriers, community desal plants—but weathered, often improvisational. Conflicts rotate around mitigation vs adaptation, grassroots pressure vs technocratic compromise. Geography is of salt, wind, reclaimed marshes and a boardwalk that remembers happier summers. Atmospherics are heavy: humid, gray, sudden toxic-yellow sunsets. The overall atmosphere is intimate and urgent; romance and civic duty are inseparable, and every personal choice has environmental consequence.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Ari Navarro, a coastal engineer returned to Mariselle after personal loss. You want to build community-led adaptive systems—living shorelines, retrofits, evacuations—that both protect lives and preserve local culture. At the municipal level, Lina Moreau, precise and urbane, pushes large-scale engineered fixes backed by private capital (Ephraim Blake). Sora Watanabe, a fiery organizer, insists on direct action and community sovereignty. The story is a Rags-to-Riches (Rise) romance: over several possible routes you grow in trust, agency, and political effectiveness. The root conflict—how to balance radical activism, technocratic scale, and community stewardship—is reflected in intimate choices: whom you trust, whom you compromise with, and how you manage grief and responsibility. Branching decisions determine whether you fall in love with Sora, align with Lina pragmatically, pursue an independent third-way, or face loss. Each ending covers one of the canonical outcomes (Happy, Bittersweet, Sad, True). Emotionally the arc rises: early uncertainty and cautious rebuilding progress to confident, collaborative action and hard-won intimacy or principled realism. Visual novel pacing emphasizes tactile coastal details: salt-stained jackets, a chipped enamel seagull pin, Lina's platinum bob, Sora's paint-splattered jacket—every image a moral weight.
