## Visual Novel Title
Tidebridge: Low Tide Promises

---

## World View
Tidebridge is a near-future coastal town (circa 2038) perched on the ragged edge of an estuary being reshaped by rising seas and storm intensification. Weathered wooden piers and leaning fishing shacks line Tidebridge Harbor; salt-streaked streets hang onto community gardens and hand-built defenses. The town is a patchwork of shared labor and private interests: a local restoration collective led by elders and fieldworkers; a municipal council balancing civic duty with external investment; and an outside firm offering rapid, engineered protection with strings attached. Culture here is small-town intimacy mixed with activist urgency—neighbors garden together in sand-scarred lots, repair netting and buoy housings, and meet in the warm amber interior of Etta’s greenhouse to plan. Religion and formal hierarchy are secondary to kinship and history; informal elders like Etta Hargrove carry weight. Geography funnels conflict: Saltmarsh Flats, tidal pools and eelgrass beds are both livelihood and battleground; Wave Innovation Lab and corporate-funded pilings sit uneasily beside handmade coir logs. The aesthetic is painterly anime realism—muted sea-glass greens, storm-gray skies, sun-faded ochres, textured grime offset by luminous biotech LEDs. Tension between community-led ecological restoration and profit-driven technocratic mitigation shapes the town’s politics and personal lives; climate pressures act as crucible for love, loyalty, and moral choices.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Ari Tanaka, Tidebridge native, restoration organizer, and reluctant hero. The story opens with cautious hope: living-shoreline projects are taking root and Mateo Alvarez, an engineer and soft-hearted love interest, returns to support hybrid technological measures. Elio Sato, an efficient corporate technocrat, promises fast, hard protections that would stabilize the town but demand buyouts and centralized control. As storms grow in frequency, choices compress: collaborate with Mateo and accept strategic funding; hold the line with purely grassroots restoration; or compromise with municipal and corporate plans to secure immediate relief. Each route deepens conflict and erodes trust: projects fail or succeed imperfectly, loved ones get hurt or leave, and betrayal—intentional or bureaucratic—brings moral rupture. The arc is tragedy: each decision leads to escalating losses—failed deployments, flooded properties, coerced relocations, compromised principles—until the nadir of the estuary’s social fabric. Romance with Mateo provides intimacy and moral tension; sometimes it survives strain, sometimes it doesn’t. Endings vary in shade—bittersweet survival, fractured community, corporate containment, or an unresolved ongoing struggle—but all descend from hope to loss, reflecting that adaptation can save structures but not always the town’s complete soul.
