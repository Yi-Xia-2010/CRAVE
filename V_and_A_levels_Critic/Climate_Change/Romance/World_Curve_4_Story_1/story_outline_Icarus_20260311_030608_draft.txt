## Visual Novel Title
Tides Between Us

---

## World View
Solhaven is a small coastal town in 2035, perched between temperate sea and reclaimed marsh. The town’s economy is a patchwork of fishing, small-scale aquaculture, seasonal tourism, and grassroots green enterprises. Politically, local council elections are intense and personal; civic life happens at town hall meetings, at the Saltwren Commons, and on fishermen’s decks. A technocratic regional development cooperative, fronted by Celeste Park, pushes for capital-driven floodwalls and a dike-lined promenade to attract investment. Community organizers — led by locals like Mara Lin and Rae Carter — favor nature-based buffers, living shorelines, and equitable relocation. Dr. Amina Bhatt runs Tidewatch Lab and provides scientific mediation between advocates. Class tensions and practical survival needs sit beside cultural reverence for place; many households have family histories of rebuilding after storms. Geography alternates low-lying marsh and eroding sandstone bluffs; seasonal storms and king tides reshuffle the shoreline and livelihoods. Religion is diffuse — ritualized town gatherings, lighthouse vigils, and memorials for storm victims carry communal ritual weight. The atmosphere is melancholic but resolute: grief for lost ground and hope baked into hands that plant, sandbag, and map. Visuals are semi-realistic anime with seafoam, rust, and slate tones; salt, compost, and wet wood textures are constant.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Mara Lin, a pragmatic urban ecologist who runs the Saltwren Commons reclamation project. Solhaven faces a turning point: Celeste Park’s well-funded promenade and floodwall promises immediate jobs and investor attention; Elias Navarro proposes an engineered-hybrid plan that aims to blend infrastructure with wetland buffers. Over a rapidly accelerating stretch of civic momentum, you and Elias form an uneasy alliance — and a fragile romance — as you negotiate technical details, public hearings, protests, and personal compromises. The narrative follows an Icarus curve: early momentum and success (a rapid rise) — culminating in a celebrated pilot implementation and your emotional closeness — followed by a catastrophic crash when a powerful storm exposes structural limits, political betrayals, or irreversible trade-offs depending on choices. Branching decisions alter whether compromise succeeds, whether development steamrolls local will, whether the town pays heavy losses, and whether love survives. In some endings you find partnership and durable adaptive plans; in others you lose trust, lovers, or parts of the town. The story explores whether love can persist across different time horizons and how community stewardship and engineered fixes can or cannot be reconciled.
