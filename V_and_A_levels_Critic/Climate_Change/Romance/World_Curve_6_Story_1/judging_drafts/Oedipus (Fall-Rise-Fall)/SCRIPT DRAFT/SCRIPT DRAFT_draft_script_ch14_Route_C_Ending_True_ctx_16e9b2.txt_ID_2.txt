Maya Corvin: "Compromise is fine until it eats the thing we were trying to save. We have a history of 'best intentions' turning into permits for someone else's profit."

You cross the barricade into a place where sweat meets steel. Work that would have been overnight now stretches — more expensive, more visible.

Camila 'Kai' Navarro: (a half-smile) "You make it sound easy, Maya. Agreements on paper never match the weather."

Maya Corvin: "Then we make agreements that hold up when storms come. We make them live documents — audited, transparent, enforceable."

Camila 'Kai' Navarro: "You asked for municipal teeth. You got them. I'm constrained, but I'm not powerless. We both know the stakes. Protecting your wetland corridors is not charity — it's engineering that costs time."

Maya Corvin: "Zero wasn't an option. We know that. But Sima — what are the trade-offs we didn't quantify?"

Dr. Sima Raza: "Some cultural sites will see increased salinity and altered access patterns. There are biotic response lags we can't compress. And funding cycles mean monitoring could be understaffed in lean years unless the city guarantees positions."
