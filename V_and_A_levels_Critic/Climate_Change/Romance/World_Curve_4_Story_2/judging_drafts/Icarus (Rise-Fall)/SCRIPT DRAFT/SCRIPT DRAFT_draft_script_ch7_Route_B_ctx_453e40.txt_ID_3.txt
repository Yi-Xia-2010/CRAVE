[Scene: Approaching the Glass Pier | Night]
[Visual: The Glass Pier rises like a shard—LED lines like surgical lamps. Floodlights wash the scaffolding white. VossCorp banners flap, too new and too bright to belong here. Security clusters in tactical vests at the edges, the color of polished storm-clouds.]
[Sound: The chants bump against the wall of corporate light; a rising flurry of camera shutters, the crisp click of PR teams arranging the scene. A van hums and coughs as a legal team step out, folders clutched like talismans.]
[Music: A staccato electronic note threads the percussion—an intrusive, mechanized counterpoint.]

You can see Calder's people before you see him—polished shoes on uneven pier planks, badges, the glint of carefully calibrated concern. A suited spokesperson steps forward, smile practiced, hands open in the universal pose of de-escalation.

VossCorp Spokesperson: "We appreciate the community's passion. Mr. Voss has directed that we open a dialogue and ensure any concerns are addressed. We're prepared to meet..."

The sentence is soft, precise—designed to frame you and your neighbors as reasonable parties in a process they manage. The words smell like containment: antiseptic, rehearsed.

You: "Dialogue only works if it's real. Tenure. Resident-led governance of the pilot. Legal guarantees now, not next quarter."

VossCorp Spokesperson: (The smile thins by a fraction; a PRed pause.) "We can discuss those items in good faith. For the record: we propose an independent audit and a managed, phased engagement. Safety is our priority."

Jun steps forward, recorder raised like a banner. His tone is calm but sharp.

Jun: "Who will fund this independent audit? Who chooses the auditors? Where is the clause that guarantees resident tenure? These are not abstractions—these are legal safeguards."

VossCorp Legal Counsel: (Stepping into the light, voice clipped.) "We are discussing terms. Loud public displays complicate the negotiation environment. We ask you to disperse to facilitate a smoother process."

Maya's jaw clenches; her fingers tighten on the ledger like a talisman.

Maya Lin: "We are not a complication. We are the people any process must answer to. You do not get to call us noise and expect cooperation."

You can feel the crowd press forward—forward, not violent but insistent. A child shouts something about keeping their house. Someone rings a kettle drum with ferocious clarity. The air tastes metallic and bright, like the moment before rain.

Ori: (leaning in, voice low) "They want the optics. If you let them control the optics, they control the story. You know this."

You: "I know. But optics are the river and we are the paddle. We steer."

The PR spokesperson tilts their head, eyes calculating. Behind them, a legal counsel fingers a folder embossed with the Voss crest.

VossCorp Spokesperson: "We propose a public forum next week, with media present, moderated by an independent panel. That will give space for voices—both community and corporate—to be heard."

Jun: "Who chooses the panel?"

VossCorp Spokesperson: (beat) "We can discuss the selection protocol."

For a heartbeat the world narrows to the cadence of that sentence—'We can discuss.' It is a sandbar phrase: it seems to promise land while moving the tide out of reach.

You feel the building tension coalesce into a bright, hot focus. The march is a living thing behind you; you, perched at its edge, have to name whether this offer is a foothold or a trap.

[Sound: A drone's soft whir overhead; someone in the crowd starts a chant that folds into the noise: "No relocation! No erasure! Tenure now!"]
