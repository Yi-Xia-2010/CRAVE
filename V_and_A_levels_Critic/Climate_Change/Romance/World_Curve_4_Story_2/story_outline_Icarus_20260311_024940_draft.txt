## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Harborlight is a near-future coastal metropolis remade by water. Rising seas and harsher storms have braided city life into intertidal districts where stilts, pontoons and floating markets sit beneath the distant glass silhouette of corporate towers. Power is contested: VossCorp—polished, streamlined, promising engineered certainty—sits across the channel from Saltway Quarter, a patchwork neighborhood of wooden tenements, mossed boardwalks and communal tinkers. The city hums with salvage-workshops, living seawalls and solar sails. Political power centers on redevelopment mandates, insurance-driven relocations, and investor-backed “resilience” projects that promise safety through scale. Grassroots networks, led by community elders and maker-collectives, counter with low-cost, ecologically integrated designs that keep people rooted. Religion and civic ritual are less formal than ritualized resilience: tide-watch vigils, seed-sharing, and memorials for lost porches. Class lines lace the shoreline—Saltway’s salt-streaked banners and filament lanterns against VossArc’s clinical LEDs—and the moral friction between technocratic certainty and communal care is the city’s slow, salt-scented argument. Weather is an active presence—fog that hides rows of planters, sudden squalls that rearrange docks, golden calms that let the city breathe. The story is intimate, staged in kitchens, ferry-labs and construction scaffolds, where personal love and civic duty collide under an unforgiving sky.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Ava Maren, a pragmatic idealist born and bred in Saltway. The story follows my attempt to secure a community-led living seawall and keep my neighbors in place while a charismatic corporate force, Calder Voss of VossCorp, offers grand-scale engineered redevelopment. I partner with Ori Navarro, a charming maker with paint-splattered overalls, whose improvisational beauty challenges and attracts me. The narrative is organized as a branching tree that rises—hopeful community success, public validation of grassroots design—then falls—political compromise, corporate pressure, and the costs of survival. At three primary decision nodes I choose: negotiate with VossCorp, escalate public exposure, or prototype quietly with Ori. Each choice pushes the arc toward a brief peak (a public success or breakthrough) and then a catastrophic collapse—legal backlashes, forced relocations, storm-fueled failures, or political betrayals. The emotional core is the romance between my damp auburn bob and sea-glass eyes, and Ori’s warm amber gaze—our tender choices become the hinge of community fate. Endings range from partial triumph with personal costs, to systemic exposé and reform, to forced displacement and grief; every outcome honors Harborlight’s textures, the people who live there, and the moral compromises adaptation demands.

- Arousal is arranged so that along any playable route (start → split → branch climax → ending) tension increases toward the climax and reaches high intensity at the fall.
- Visuals adhere strictly to provided character designs and locations: Ava’s auburn bob, sea-glass eyes and bracelet, Ori’s curly black hair and patchwork coat, Calder’s silver-black hair and signet ring, and the named Harborlight locales.
- Branching nodes: Chapter 3 (major route split), Chapter 5 (Route A split), Chapter 8 (Route B split), Chapter 11 (Route C split). Additional route chapters (13–15) are reachable from split outcomes as described, maintaining every path’s depth ≤ 6 chapters from Chapter 1 to an ending.
