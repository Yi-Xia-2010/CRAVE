Elias: (a pained half-smile) "I know how that looks. I also know that from that perch—policy-writing, budget lines—you can lock protections in place. You can make sure the next city's ordinance is not optional. You can save lives."

You are home and not home. It is the kind of bureaucratic victory that tastes like iron and winter.

Some families are secured—grudgingly, finally. The ordinance allocates funds for long-term relocation only after the trust places are secured; Tamiko's leaks forced transparency into contract bidding. The Vale Consortium's most aggressive clauses are stripped after public pressure and a withering audit. It is, by any policy rubric, a win.

But policy moves on scales that people's lives can't always match. Mrs. Baez's porch is empty; her children wave from a moving van. The playground has a new "temporary closure" sign.
