## Visual Novel Title
Tidelines of Home

---

## World View
Verdante is a mid-sized coastal city in the late 2030s where seasonal King Tides have become a civic emergency. The municipal center contrasts with eroding traditional harbors: glass towers bearing renderings of engineered shorelines sit opposite Mariner's Row, a patchwork of wooden homes, sandbag lines, and ringed porches. Political power is shared uneasily between municipal planners, private infrastructure firms, and grassroots neighborhood collectives. The Vale Consortium, led by Cassian Vale, pushes decisive, profit-driven redevelopment—seawalls and relicensing of waterfront parcels—framed as necessary to "save the city." Municipal planners like Elias Moreno champion scalable technical fixes but sometimes underestimate social displacement. Neighborhood activists (Tamiko, Lina) and community-rooted professionals (Dr. Arun Patel, Mira) argue for participatory, ecological adaptation: living shorelines, phased upgrades, and legal protections for long-term residents. Culture in Verdante mixes maritime memory with civic urgency: public meetings in City Hall Plaza, community markets afloat at the Tidal Market, and rooftop experiments at Skyfield Conservatory. Religion is less overt than local rituals—annual tide-watch vigils and shared preservation of family heirloom scarves and boats. Geography centers an exposed harbor, scattered piers (Beacon Station), and low-lying Mariner's Row—places where policy becomes flesh and where love and duty collide against an encroaching sea.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mira Santos: community organizer, urban ecologist by training, and child of Mariner's Row. The story opens as Verdante's municipal response reaches a boiling point—Cassian Vale's Consortium proposes an expedited seawall and redevelopment that would protect large parts of the city but forcibly rezone and displace my neighborhood. Elias Moreno, the man I’ve been quietly falling for, is leading a municipal plan that promises broad protection but relies on top-down delivery timelines that risk social erasure. My work with Lina, Tamiko, and Dr. Arun pits community knowledge against political momentum. Across multiple branching choices, I must decide how to fight: negotiate a hybrid plan with Elias, sabotage or expose consortium corruption, or hold fast to grassroots resistance. The arc is tragic: decisions, misjudgments, and compromises cascade into losses. Some routes end with a collaborative but costly victory; others with heartbreak and displacement; worst-case outcomes have Cassian's plan prevail and the neighborhood sanitized. The romance between Mira and Elias is tested by value divergence; our relationship’s fate depends on whether we can align means and ends or whether love becomes collateral damage in a city racing to shore up its future.
