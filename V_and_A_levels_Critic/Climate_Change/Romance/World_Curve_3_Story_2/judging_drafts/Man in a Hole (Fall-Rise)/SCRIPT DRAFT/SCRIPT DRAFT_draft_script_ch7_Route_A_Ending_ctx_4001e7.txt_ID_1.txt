The workshop becomes a crucible. Some volunteers bend toward Elena's donations as lifelines and hope; others whisper that the cultural loss will be irreversible. Maya arrives late, camera swinging, tears on her cheeks, furious and brimming with footage she can upload. Kaito: "They've cordoned the marsh. They told us last night they are moving the ceremonial line inland, behind the scaffolding. They told Luka that it would be safer."

Kaito: "They will let people stand there and light candles, but the sound will be different. The marsh will be under the other side of a wall."

The realization forms like a bruise. The barrier that rises tonight will work — Elena's models say so — but it will also be a wall between the town and the marsh's living edge. It will be a familiar scaffold with an unfamiliar face.

The barrier is finished by morning. Elena gives a short speech about resilience, the cameras capture her smile framed in triumph. The storm season prediction improves.
