## Visual Novel Title
Saltline Rising

---

## World View
Saltline is a near-future coastal town clinging to a crescent bay where tides have begun rewriting streets and rituals. Once a sleepy fishing village, Saltline now balances patchwork marsh reclamation, floating farms, and experimental cliffside wind turbines. Power sits between local cooperatives — workshops that repurpose salvage into resilient infrastructure — and centralized agencies promising engineered barriers and automated retrofits. The local culture prizes communal rituals (nighttime boat-song gatherings, shrine-like tide offerings, annual cliffside seedings) and practical craftsmanship: boatbuilders, lantern-makers, community gardeners. Politics circle around resource allocation: grassroots adaptation versus corporate/state-led mitigation. Religion is subtle — respect for sea and weather encoded in folklore and town ceremonies rather than formal institutions. Class tension arises when funding proposals require relocation or commodify coastal commons. Geography matters: salt-silver beaches, sunken promenades, terraced cliff gardens, a converted boathouse that doubles as Tidewise Lab & Greenhouse, and a storm-ready community hall. Atmosphere is bittersweet — wind-swept and hopeful — lit by amber greenhouse lamps and cold storm lighting. The conflict tests intimacy and responsibility: choices for love mirror choices for the planet. No new main characters are introduced; the story centers on Marin Alvarez, Kaito Sakamura, and Elena Moro within Saltline's fragile ecology.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Marin Alvarez) return home to Saltline carrying technical knowledge and the weight of a past project that displaced marshland life. The town faces accelerated sea-rise and an offer from Elena — a polished, corporate-minded engineer — to build a massive coastal barrier and retrofit core infrastructure. Kaito, my childhood friend and maker, champions community-led, craft-based resilience. The story arcs into a Man-in-a-Hole pattern: the narrative falls into crisis as disaster and political pressure expose my past mistakes and fracture trust; I sink emotionally and professionally; then I confront moral choices and rebuild relationships and plans. Branching decisions force me to choose alliances (craft cooperatives, corporate engineering, hybrid governance), prioritize ecosystems or human safety, and negotiate or resist centralized power. Multiple endings reflect those choices: cooperative hybrid success (happy), forced but effective barrier with relocation (bittersweet), corporate consolidation that sacrifices commons (sad), and an emergent federated stewardship built on moral coherence (true). Romance with Kaito weaves through these stakes: staying or leaving, protecting people or ecosystems, and trusting in vulnerable partnership.

Notes on Structure and Emotional Curve:
- The story follows a Fall → Rise ("Man in a Hole") arc. Chapters 1–4 emphasize the fall into crisis; chapters after the bottom (chapters 5 onward) depict recovery and rebuilding, with valence moving from V-VeryNegative at the bottom to V-VeryPositive in hopeful endings.
- Arousal increases monotonically along playable paths, peaking at the climactic decision and resolution chapters (endings have A-VeryHigh).
- Branching occurs in four major hubs: Chapter 3 (initial split), Chapter 5 (Route A split), Chapter 8 (Route B split), and Chapter 11 (Route C split). Every route from Chapter 1 to any Ending is no more than six chapters long.
- All characters remain as provided, with visual traits and clothing referenced in each chapter to maintain visual consistency and anchoring.
- The narrative remains strictly first-person, immersing the player in Marin Alvarez’s perspective: emotionally grounded, scientifically literate, and ethically conflicted as she negotiates love, responsibility, and community resilience.
