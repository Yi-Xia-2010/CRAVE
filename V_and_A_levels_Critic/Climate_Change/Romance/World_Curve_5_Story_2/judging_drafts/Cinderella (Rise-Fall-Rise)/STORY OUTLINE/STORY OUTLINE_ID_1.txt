## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Marisma

---

## World View
Marisma Bay is a near-future coastal town reconfiguring itself around rising tides and brittle weather. The political center is split between pragmatic municipal offices and private restoration firms vying for contracts; Councilor Nguyen mediates in public hearings while corporate lobbyists slip through glass corridors. Culture is hybrid: older fishing families, reparative community gardeners, young activists, and technicians who translate old lore into sensors and models. Religion is diffuse—small rituals to the sea, remembrances for lost houses—informal but powerful in shaping local priorities. Class lines track elevation: low-lying neighborhoods are poorer, with blue-collar livelihoods tied to the docks; the city’s higher ground hosts polished firms promising techno-solutions. Geography is salt-soaked: reclaimed lots, fog-lined docks, a battered sea wall, an algae-slim harbor, and a greenhouse lab called Tidepark. Conflict centers on whether Marisma's future will be steered by corporate-scaled geoengineering, municipal planning compromises, or grassroots, place-based restoration. The atmosphere is tactile and weathered—salt-streaked streets, damp clothing, and watercolor skies—where love and local memory sit beside models, blueprints, and hard decisions about who stays and who must move.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aiko Navarro, an ecological engineer returned home to Marisma Bay to build a community-led marsh restoration plan after losing your childhood home to a storm. The narrative frames climate as a living, moral force—its slow pressure making each choice feel intimate and urgent. The story follows a Cinderella (Rise-Fall-Rise) emotional arc: early momentum builds as you rally neighbors and a promising municipal partnership (Rise), a sudden institutional or personal betrayal (or a compromise you regret) causes collapse and loss (Fall), and finally you salvage a durable victory by combining community knowledge with selective technology and personal sacrifice (Rise). Romance threads with Mateo Ríos (the conciliatory planner) complicate decisions: aligning with him can amplify municipal leverage but risks diluting community voice; pushing him too hard fractures intimate trust. Dr. Selene Park—charismatic corporate restoration director—offers fast solutions that promise measurable protection but risk ecological and social displacement. Secondary allies (Ivy, Old Man Rohan, Tala) represent local stakes and knowledge. Branching choices determine which alliances you prioritize: community-first co-design, cautious municipal compromise with Mateo, or entering a program with Dr. Park. Endings range from hopeful community success to bittersweet adaptation, each viewed through first-person intimacy and tactile coastal detail.
