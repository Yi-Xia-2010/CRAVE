## Visual Novel Title
Tideward Hearts

---

## World View
A near-future coastal metropolis (circa 2042) balances reclaimed wetlands, rooftop farms, and razor-edge corporate islands. Seasonal superstorms, high tides, and rolling blackouts reframe public life: wooden platforms, patched concrete, salt-stained metal, and moss-laced architecture coexist with HelioCorp’s glass atriums and curated vertical gardens. Power is fragmented — community microgrids hum beside corporate-controlled grids; law and policy sway between municipal councils and private contractors. Culture prizes improvisation, shared labor, and oral memory; cuisine and barter still bind neighborhoods. Religion is diffuse — ritualized remembrance of lost shorelines and lighthouse vigils; Sora’s stories anchor the past. Class fault lines run between those whose livelihoods depend on the shore and technocrats promising rapid rescue. HelioCorp, led by Dr. Rowan Hale, sells large-scale geoengineering as salvation; grassroots organizers like Elias push distributed resilience and mutual aid. The atmosphere is melancholic but tenacious: watercolor light, salt haze, and banners fluttering along tideward promenades. This city’s conflicts are ethical and intimate—choices about infrastructure become choices about trust, love, and who decides who stays. You are Aiko Mori: a former urban climatologist anchored to a neighborhood that refuses to be erased.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You (Aiko Mori) return from institutional exile to lead community resilience after a catastrophic flood. Early chapters rise with hope as neighborhood victories, rooftop farms, and Elias’s warmth reawaken your purpose. At a pivotal town meeting (the Common Route decision), three paths open: commit fully to community organizing with Elias; test a partnership with HelioCorp and Dr. Rowan Hale; or collaborate with investigative journalist Rafi to expose hidden contracts. Each branch follows the Cinderella arc: an initial rise to hope (common optimism), a steep fall when systems betray or catastrophe looms, and a final rise where you secure a different kind of victory — whether community-led survival, pragmatic compromise with regulated tech, systemic reform after exposure, or a tragic sacrifice that sparks reform. Romance with Elias is threaded through choices: it deepens in grassroots routes, strains under compromise, and fractures under conflicting ethics. Secondary anchors — Mina’s community kitchen, Sora’s oral histories, and Rafi’s recordings — shape the moral texture. Each ending reflects trade-offs between distributed power, technocratic intervention, personal love, and public duty.
