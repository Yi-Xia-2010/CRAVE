## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Near-future coastal metropolis, circa 2042, rests on a knife-edge between salvage and invention. Once-bulwarked harbors now fold into reclaimed wetlands and layered rooftop farms; segmented sea walls and improvised levees stand alongside gleaming corporate glass. The political landscape is a tight three-way tension: grassroots collectives and mutual-aid networks that stitch neighborhoods together; HelioCorp and other techno-capital interests promising rapid, centralized fixes; and a cautious city council negotiating between public safety and private investment. Culture is pragmatic and ritualized—sea-speech, tide-maps, harvest festivals—woven with the new austerity of rationed power and rolling blackouts. Religion and belief have blended: lighthouse rituals and oral histories sit beside data-driven modeling worship. Class lines run along waterfront gradients: those with resources occupy fortified mid-rises and tech retreats; the outskirt neighborhoods—where fishermen, gardeners, and makers survive—rely on shared infrastructure and memory. Geography is tactile: salt-streaked concrete, mossed steel, rooftop soil, and wet, luminous skies. The atmosphere is melancholic but tenacious: people balance grief for what was lost with stubborn care—planting, patching, arguing at the Old Beacon over late-night tea. That tension—between centralized technology and neighborly resilience—drives every choice and relationship in the narrative.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aiko Mori, a former urban climatologist turned neighborhood resilience coordinator. The story opens with you coordinating patchwork defenses and food runs across the Tideward Promenade and Verdant Terrace, struggling with scarce supplies and the seductive promises of Dr. Rowan Hale’s HelioCorp. A charismatic organizer, Elias Chen, rekindles a romantic trust as you fight for direct, community-led solutions. A moral and emotional crossroads arises when HelioCorp offers infrastructure and swift protection in exchange for significant centralized control. The narrative follows a Cinderella arc: an initial steady rise (building community wins and the nascent romance), a sudden fall (a critical failure—storm, betrayal, or a heartbreaking sacrifice), and a final rise toward lasting, systemic change (through local organizing, savvy use of tech, or wrenching personal cost). Branching choices force you to choose collaborators, reveal corporate secrets, and decide when to sacrifice personal relationships for the broader good. Each path explores ethical trade-offs: distributed resilience, technocratic salvation, public accountability, or tragic martyrdom. Visuals emphasize watercolor-anime textures: rain-slick concrete, salt-stained boots, rooftop green mosaics, and HelioCorp’s cool glass atrium.
