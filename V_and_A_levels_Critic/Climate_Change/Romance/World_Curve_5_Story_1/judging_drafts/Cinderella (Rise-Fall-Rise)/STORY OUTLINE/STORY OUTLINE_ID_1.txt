## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Gardens

---

## World View
Near-future coastal city (circa 2042) built around a harbor reshaped by rising seas and stronger storms. Blocks of reclaimed brick and rusted steel sit cheek-by-jowl with newly planted green roofs and community glasshouses. Public life oscillates between salvage and regeneration: informal markets and boat-slung deliveries mix with corporate banners promising engineered certainty. Political power is brokered through public-private partnerships; developers like Jonah Hale command resources and persuasive presentations, while grassroots networks — roof gardens, cooperatives, and the Green Roof Network — stitch social and ecological resilience together with little funding but abundant care.

The city's culture reveres memory and practical ingenuity: salvaging, seed-saving, mutual aid and small ceremonies at Twilight Festival Plaza coexist with metrics-driven meetings at the Coastal Resilience Lab. Religion or formal doctrine is peripheral; ritual is civic (planting, mending nets). Social class follows closeness to the water: harbor neighborhoods are denser, community-tied, and more vulnerable; inland planning and investors hold power. Geography is a patchwork: raised terraces, seasonal submerged boardwalks, a glassed community greenhouse, and a controversial seawall construction site framing the political fault lines. Atmosphere: textured eco-grit and cinematic pastel storms, human warmth under threat.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Ayla Moreno, a practical idealist who has spent years knitting the harbor’s green rooftop network and community greenhouse into lifelines for food and dignity. When Jonah Hale’s consortium proposes a sweeping seawall and modular levee system promising security, the city fractures. Elias Rook — a climate engineer I have begun to trust and feel for — offers a hybrid path: targeted engineering integrated with expanded green infrastructure. Kira and neighborhood activists demand outright resistance. I must choose: hold the line and oppose top-down projects, partner with Elias to co-design a hybrid plan, or take a risky private consultation with Jonah to mitigate losses.

Across multiple branches the emotional arc follows a Cinderella pattern: we climb toward a hopeful breakthrough, a sudden collapse threatens everything, and then we scramble and choose paths that can lead to compromise, loss, or an imperfect but lasting recovery. Each route explores romance strained by politics and sacrifice, where the true victory is a resilient cityscape and an honest, evolving bond with Elias — or painful separation that reinforces community resolve. Endings vary between mutual co-creation, bittersweet sacrifice, hard-loss, and a realistic, ongoing collaboration that honors both love and civic duty.
