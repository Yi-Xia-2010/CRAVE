## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Esperanza

---

## World View
Esperanza Bay, circa 2042, is a coastal city braided with old docks, kelp farms, and gleaming corporate seawalls. Climate-driven superstorms and chronic high tides have remade daily life: insurance lines, volunteer brigades, and public debates over which neighborhoods will be protected and which will be “managed.” Political power sits with a municipal government beset by investors and charismatic contractors; economic decisions are framed as emergency triage, while grassroots neighbors improvise living infrastructure—kelp nurseries, floating gardens, and patched-up community pumps. Class lines map to elevation: the economic core gets hard, privatized defenses; low-lying districts like East Strand shoulder piecemeal solutions. Cultural life is maritime and communal—fishwives at dawn, elders repairing nets, festival processions to the harbor. Religion and ritual mix practical tide-reading with memorials for lost porches and storefronts. The city’s geography is a series of thresholds: promenades that drown then clear, warehouses turned resilience labs, and abandoned piers where regeneration is practiced. Atmosphere: salt-humid air, water-glass light at dusk, and a persistent sense that every conversation could tip policy. Visual style is soft painterly realism—pastel coastal hues, filmic golden-hour lighting, and anime-influenced character expressions that linger over small gestures.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Evans, a coastal resilience planner born in East Strand, returned to build a living seawall that protects people and preserves place. The story follows an Oedipus curve: an initial Fall (the crisis of displacement and political compromise), a Rise (a fragile, collaborative triumph when community design and Noah’s modular tech demonstrate impact), and a final Fall (an unavoidable political or climatic reversal that undoes or reshapes that success). Across multiple branches, my romantic entanglement with Noah Ríos and my clashes with Arman Kade are the emotional engine. Choices determine whether we steer toward public collaboration, private compromise, or fragmented survival—but every path must reconcile the moral cost of trade-offs. Each route compresses the arc into six chapters from start to ending: we fall into crisis, rally and innovate, taste victory, then confront the structural pressures that force a painful decline—be that displacement, bittersweet evacuation, corporate capture, or a mixed, morally complex compromise. Romance anchors stakes: the relationship with Noah alternately strengthens, frays, or realigns depending on the compromises each choice requires. The city’s fate is never a simple win; endings range from happy-but-temporary refuge to hard-won but incomplete realism.
