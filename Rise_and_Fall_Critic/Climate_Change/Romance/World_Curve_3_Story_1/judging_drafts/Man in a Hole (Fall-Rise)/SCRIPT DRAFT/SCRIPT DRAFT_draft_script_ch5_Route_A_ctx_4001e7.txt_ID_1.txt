Inspector: "You've made admirable progress. These tests are encouraging. However, without provisional permits for on-site testing, we'll need to formalize the work before we can certify it."
You: "We need to move faster than municipal timelines allow. These aren’t experiments in a lab; they're defenses on a shoreline that moves."
Inspector: (softening, almost apologetic) "I understand urgency, Ms. Alvarez. I also have to file a record. There's liability to consider."

The exchange is a small crucible—policy and panic at the same table. You feel the friction between the speed you need and the steps the town requires.
