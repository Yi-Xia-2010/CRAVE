Kai: "Exactly. Screw-perfect engineering. Learn-in-public saves lives."

Tom: "I set up double shifts. Wait till you see the jigs—we're making them idiot-proof."

You and Kai walk to the very end of the repaired length—the boards younger here, the nails bright and proud. You sit with your legs dangling, the sea swallowing the sound of your shoes. The lights ripple across the damp planks, turning everything into softened strokes of gold.

Kai moves with the kind of ease born of tool-use; his fingers trace a line in the wood beside you, smearing a dark spot where grease met grain. He pushes his beanie back and turns to you, eyes reflecting the harbor lamps.

Kai: "You ever think about how different we'd be if the pier hadn't splintered that first winter?"

You: "Sometimes. I imagine a version where we ignore the warnings and keep buying time. But I don't miss that other version."

Kai: "No. You wouldn't. You'd be quieter. You'd still be kind, but your hands would probably be more polite about getting dirty."
