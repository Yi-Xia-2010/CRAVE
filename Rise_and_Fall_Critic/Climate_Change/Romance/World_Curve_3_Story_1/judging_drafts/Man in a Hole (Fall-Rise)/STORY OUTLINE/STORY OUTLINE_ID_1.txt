## Visual Novel Title
Tides We Keep

---

## World View
Mariner's Reach is a mid-sized coastal town in 2035 straddling memory and adaptation. Once a shipwright and fishing hub, it now wrestles with higher tides, erratic storms, and thinning municipal coffers. Weathered clapboard storefronts and patched hulls share blocks with solar canopies and rooftop garden plots. Power is local and patchwork: community-run microgrids, volunteer retrofit teams, and a municipal planning office trying to stitch grant cycles into durable infrastructure. Socially, the town is layered — blue-collar repair shops, cooperative gardeners, cautious small-business owners, and a municipal bureaucracy squeezed by region-wide funding constraints. Culture clings to festival piers and communal fish fries even as politics force trade-offs: which streets to elevate, which leaseholders to buy out, how to keep the harbor economy alive.

Religion and ritual are subtle: the pier blessing, memorial plaques for lost seawalls, seed exchanges — civic liturgies that hold memory. Class is local and visceral: boatshops and marinas rubbing against planners' spreadsheets. Geography compresses conflict; a single rising tide threatens both a family repair shop and a municipal budget line. Atmosphere is melancholic but soft: salt-streaked facades at dusk, greenhouse humidity under string lights, and blowing tarps during storm warnings. Visuals are soft neo-realist painterliness — muted seafoam greens, rusted ochres, and small bright accents of new plant life.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Iris Alvarez, home in Mariner's Reach, pushing community-led adaptation projects to preserve the town I grew up in. A severe season of storms and a stalled municipal grant shove me into a crisis: the rooftop pilot irrigation systems fail, key funding is delayed, and a storm damages the pier where Kai and I used to race kites. That fall pulls me into the "hole": guilt, strained alliances, and the sense that every choice costs something I love.

From that low point I must choose a path: join Kai's grassroots open-source retrofit movement; negotiate with Marin, the municipal planner, to secure durable funding; or hold to DIY community-only tactics and refuse institutional compromise. Each route deepens certain losses but reveals different recoveries: with Kai I cultivate grassroots scale and renewed affection through inventive communal resilience; with Marin I learn political patience and build durable infrastructures at the cost of immediate comforts; with independent organizing I reweave community ties while sacrificing some old landmarks. Across all routes I fall into crisis and — through difficult compromise, candid conversations, and risky work — climb out into a recovered, changed commitment to both love and civic care. Endings vary: joyful partnership and regional influence, bittersweet trade-offs and preserved relationships, sad fractures with new personal resolve, and a "true" outcome where coalition-building yields systemic change. The emotional arc is Man in a Hole: Fall into crisis, struggle, then rise through hard-won recovery.
