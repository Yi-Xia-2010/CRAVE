## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Green Glass

---

## World View
The year is 2038 along a coastal metropolitan stretch where rising seas and intense storm seasons have remade life. Neighborhoods like La Marisma hang between land and water — raised walkways, patched seawalls, rooftop farms, and communal markets. Corporate towers such as Aegis Tower loom like cold beacons, offering sleek, tech-forward fixes: automated floodgates, desalination microgrids, and algorithmic evacuation plans. Political power is fractured: local mayors juggle constituents and lobbyists; grassroots collectives patch levees and swap seeds; university labs publish models that oscillate between hope and doom.

Culture is tactile and improvised — salvage art, seed exchanges, bartered labor — with rituals that tether people to place. Religion and civic life fold into stewardship and mutual aid: elders teach seed-saving, kids learn pump maintenance. Class lines map onto elevation: higher-income districts can afford sealed towers and private barriers; barrios like La Marisma rely on community ingenuity and shared labor.

The visual style is soft cyberpunk neon-emerald: animated watercolor textures, eco-noir lighting. Scenes in La Marisma glow with warm, sunwashed terracotta and moss green; Aegis Tower is clinical blue-white. The atmosphere is humid and bittersweet — every dawn market can be shadowed by a flood warning. The core conflict threads climate response and intimacy: choosing between fast, centralized tech and slow, communal resilience while love complicates every vote, negotiation, and midnight repair.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Asha Rivera) return to La Marisma after years of study, committed to community-built resilience: living seawalls, rooftop agriculture, and decentralized networks. The city faces a critical funding window and looming superstorm season. At a pivotal public hearing I must choose an alliance: back grassroots direct action with Elias Hart, negotiate tech-for-support with Hana Kim (now with Aegis-linked projects), seek political compromise with Mayor Lila, or blend approaches. Each major decision opens a distinct route of collaboration and strategy, and each route contains a mid-route tactical split (trusting tough organizing, technical safeguards, or institutional bargains). Across all branches, my strengths — amber-brown eyes that scan blueprints, a weathered notebook, and stubborn empathy — guide a steady ascent: gaining influence, securing resources, and building durable systems. Love interest dynamics shift depending on alignment: with Elias, romance grows amid hands-on repair and late-night strategy; with Hana, intimacy is forged in labs and policy rooms; with both, a delicate balance of heart and logic emerges. The arc is Rags to Riches: each path accumulates capacity and legitimacy, different kinds of success (community sovereignty, technological scale, political policy, or a replicable hybrid), never a collapse. Endings vary in tone — joyful partnership, bittersweet tradeoffs, hard-won true model — but all depict growth: more lives protected, more cultural continuity preserved, and my personal reconciliation with the costs of leadership.
