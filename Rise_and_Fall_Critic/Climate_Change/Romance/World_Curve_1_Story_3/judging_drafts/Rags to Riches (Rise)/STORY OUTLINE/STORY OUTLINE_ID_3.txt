## Visual Novel Title
Tides of La Marisma

---

## World View
The year is 2038. The coastal metropolis has been remade by salt and storm: seawalls stitched from living mangroves butt up against reflective corporate spires, rooftops bristle with hydroponic terraces, and boardwalks thread through rewilded estuaries. Political power is split between city councils trying to hold fragile coalitions and a handful of well-funded green‑tech corporations promising large-scale, efficient solutions. Culture is syncretic — market vendors and community builders preserve rituals of place even as new, tech‑mediated rituals emerge among the affluent towers. Religion is less organized and more practical: stewardship and ritualized repair bind neighborhoods. Class is visible in materials and light: warm, tactile fabrics and potted gardens in La Marisma; clinical, glass-and-steel design at Aegis Tower. Conflicts center on how to allocate scarce resources and whose expertise counts: grassroots resilience or technocratic scaleups. Geography compresses into vertical layers — flooded streets below, market domes above, corporate towers on the skyline. The atmosphere is humid and anticipatory: neon-emerald reflections on puddles, the scent of salt and citrus, the distant rumble of an incoming swell. Everyday life mixes improvisation with high-stakes negotiation; survival and love are entangled.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Asha Rivera: a pragmatic idealist who returned to La Marisma after losing home and found purpose in community-led resilience. The narrative arcs upward: each emotional and political obstacle sharpens my skills, alliances, and moral clarity. Early choices set me toward three main strategies: deepen grassroots mobilization with Elias; negotiate a hybrid compromise with Hana; or pursue city-scale policy change with Mayor Ortega and Dr. Jun Park. Across branching routes, every decision escalates responsibility and resource access but requires different trade-offs: personal intimacy vs. professional compromise; doctrinal purity vs. scalable compromise; sentimental place‑keeping vs. strategic relocation. Each path culminates in an achievement — protection for neighborhoods through living seawalls, targeted tech funding for adaptive infrastructure, a hard-won policy blueprint, or a replicable hybrid model — and in each ending I grow into a leader who preserves community dignity. Romance evolves alongside policy: affection, trust, and heartbreak catalyze alliances rather than negate forward motion. The tone is hopeful and emergent: loss becomes fuel for collective ingenuity; my stubborn empathy becomes governance.
