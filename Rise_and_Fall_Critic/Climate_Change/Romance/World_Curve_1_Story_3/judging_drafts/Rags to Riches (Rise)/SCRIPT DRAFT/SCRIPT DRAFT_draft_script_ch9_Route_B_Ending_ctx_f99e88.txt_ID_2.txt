By sundown, water runs through new channels into familiar places. A child splashes at a makeshift fountain while a vendor fills jugs with measured care. Elias: "Fair point."

Sofia finds you between stalls, pressing a small wrapped tamale into your hands. Her hands are flour-streaked and sure.

Elias: "Whatever comes next, we fix it together."
You: (eyes on the market) "Together."
