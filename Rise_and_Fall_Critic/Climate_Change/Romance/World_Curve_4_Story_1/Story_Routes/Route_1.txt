You will continue. You will organize, teach, press against the ledger where it can be pressed. You will keep making room for people to find one another again.
It is not triumphant. It is not clean. It is the exact, aching place where survival meets memory.
You tuck your hands into your jacket, feel the locket warm against your sternum, and take a step toward the terrace where there will be people to teach tomorrow, seeds to sort, and a long list of grievances to keep in memory.
