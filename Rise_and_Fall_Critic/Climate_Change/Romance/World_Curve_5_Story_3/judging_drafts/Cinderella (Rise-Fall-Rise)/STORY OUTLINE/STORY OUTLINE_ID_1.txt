## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Hearts

---

## World View
A subtropical archipelago town clings to reclaimed marsh and low cliffs, a patchwork of repaired seawalls, floating gardens, and half-submerged bazaars. The town is small, economically fragile, and culturally rich: fishers, artisans, engineers, activists, and researchers inhabit intertwined social networks. Technology is decentralized — solar textiles, small autonomous boats, community-run labs — but resources are scarce and decisions are political. Local governance is pragmatic and pressured: Mayor Isla Cortez must balance emergency budgets, insurance pressures, and regional mandates. Dr. Kavir Singh leads the Tidal Research Lab, a modest glass-and-weathered-steel building that is at once scientific anchor and communal hearth. The key conflict is ideological and practical: Mateo Hale, a municipal engineer, pushes adaptable modular infrastructure (levees, stormwalls, deployable walkways) for immediate life-saving resilience; Sora Lin, a charismatic organizer and cultural steward, argues for community-led ecological restoration (mangrove and seagrass reclamation) that requires sacrifice and long-term stewardship. Aria Navarro, the protagonist, lives between these worlds: trained scientist, daughter of a fishermother, carrying a battered diver's watch and water-stained notebook. The atmosphere alternates between golden calm after storms and bruise-colored electrical skies before them. The emotional pressure is intimate: love, guilt, trust, and decision-making under climate urgency.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Aria Navarro) return to my hometown after studies in marine ecology to lead a restorative shoreline plan. Early success—uniting lab data, sensor networks, and neighborhood knowledge—creates momentum: temporary project funding, pilot mangrove beds, and municipal interest. I become romantically entangled with Mateo Hale, the steady engineer proposing rapid adaptive infrastructure, and Sora Lin, the fevered organizer rallying cultural stewardship. At a town council moment, I must choose alignment: commit to Mateo’s engineering, Sora’s restoration, or broker a hybrid. Each choice sets me on a path where things rise to a hopeful peak (the town rallies, work deploys), then a sudden catastrophic setback (a storm breaching defenses, political betrayal, or funding collapse) forces painful trade-offs. In the final act, I marshal scientific rigor, community ritual, and small technological ingenuity to rebuild with wiser strategy—achieving durable resilience and a matured personal relationship (a committed partnership, a resilient long-distance compromise, or a principled separation). Across branches, the emotional curve follows Cinderella: rise → fall → rise. The endings vary (Happy, Bittersweet, Sad, True) depending on tactical choices, but all center on moral clarity about community, love, and the ethics of adaptation.

I step off the ferry with the water-stiff scent braided into my hair and the diver's watch cool against my wrist.
