## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
A near-future subtropical archipelago clings to its margins: low cliffs and reclaimed marshes stitched together with patched boardwalks, solar textiles, and community labs. Rising seas and ferocious, unpredictable storms are the new weather pattern; the tideline eats at markets and foundations, and each season rearranges who belongs where. Technology is small-scale and decentralized—autonomous skiffs, open-source sensors, textile photovoltaics, and tinkerers’ innovations—yet municipal power is necessary to deploy larger works. Socially, everyone knows each other's lineage and failures; fishermen, engineers, organizers, elders, and lab techs negotiate survival and memory under humid, salt-scented skies.

Politically, the town exists between local councils and regional disaster bureaus. Mayor Isla Cortez must balance budgets and votes; the Tidal Lab—run by Dr. Kavir Singh—translates data into policy, while community organizers like Sora push ritual, stewardship, and direct action. Class tensions are subtle: artisanal households, small-time fishers, municipal workers, and scientific personnel overlap in the same neighborhoods. Religion and ritual are less doctrinal than practical—ceremonies to bless the mangroves, wakes for lost boats, neighborhood potlucks after storms—anchoring people when decisions are technical. The atmosphere alternates between golden calm after a squall and bruise-colored electrical skies before one. Visual style is soft anime watercolor with realistic coastal textures: muted pastels, neon salvage, sea-glass colors, and expressive silhouettes against painterly light.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aria Navarro, a marine ecologist trying to reconcile formal science with lived local knowledge while steering a community through climate-driven crisis. The emotional arc follows a Cinderella (Rise-Fall-Rise) pattern: a steady rise as Aria rallies support and prototypes a regenerative shoreline plan that brings her social and professional momentum; a sudden fall when a violent storm and political pressure create a catastrophic setback that threatens the town and her personal life; and a final rise where, through bold choices, coalition-building, and sacrifice, Aria engineers a lasting solution that centers both ecological restoration and adaptive infrastructure—while also resolving, in different ways across routes, her romantic life with Mateo (engineering pragmatist) or Sora (community organizer).

At the town meeting the stakes crystallize: choose rapid-engineering saves, grassroots restoration, or a mediated hybrid. Each choice steers a unique path of alliances, crises, and reconciliations—the endings vary from negotiated hybrid success, to bittersweet restoration with some loss, to moral "true" sacrifice that cements community control—yet every path maintains the Rise-Fall-Rise curve: gaining hope, suffering a sharp loss, then building to permanent victory.
