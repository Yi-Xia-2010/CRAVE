## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Solace

---

## World View
Solace Bay is a near-future coastal city reshaped by incremental sea-level rise and increasingly violent storms. Its geography folds a working harbor into low-lying neighborhoods, reclaimed-material architecture, rooftop farms, and tidal wetlands that edge into urban blocks. Power sits uneasily between municipal planning offices and grassroots collectives: City Hall channels budgets and permits, while organizers and fisherfolk mobilize at promenades and rooftop gardens. Religion is muted; communal ritual is expressed through local festivals, market mornings, and rites around the tides. Culture is pragmatic and sentimental—people repair what they can, barter skills, and layer old textiles over new composites. Class divides have hardened around elevation: waterfront businesses and municipal elites occupy elevated, fortified parcels, while long-standing neighborhoods shoulder most flood risk. Conflicts center on how to adapt—engineered seawalls and hydraulic works pitted against wetland restoration and managed retreat. The atmosphere is painterly and tactile: warm-gloom light, salt-streaked surfaces, textured eco-grunge details, and a constant tension between the smell of wet soil and diesel. Everyday life mixes sunlit market mornings with storm-washed nights; the city feels alive, precarious, and morally complicated.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Mara Lin) return to Solace Bay determined to scale community-centered adaptation projects after losing part of my childhood neighborhood. When a pivotal public hearing forces a choice between technocratic seawalls, radical community-managed retreat, or a coalition approach, my decision splinters my relationships with Elias Kade (municipal engineer) and Nova Duarte (grassroots firebrand). The story follows a Man-in-a-Hole arc: I fall as plans unravel, protests erupt, and a storm/administrative crisis drags me into the lowest point—blamed, exhausted, and personally bereft. From that low, I reorganize: I learn where my stubborn hope damaged others, accept help from Samir and Professor Anika, and rebuild strategies that combine engineering, wetlands, legal pressure, and compassion. Different branches explore different balances: a technocratic path tests whether love can persist when I trade some ideals for scale; a grassroots path demands sacrifice and hard truth; a coalition path tries to fuse approaches. Each route ends with recovery of a kind—some happier, some bittersweet, some painfully honest—emphasizing that resilience is messy, gradual, and shaped by whom you trust.
