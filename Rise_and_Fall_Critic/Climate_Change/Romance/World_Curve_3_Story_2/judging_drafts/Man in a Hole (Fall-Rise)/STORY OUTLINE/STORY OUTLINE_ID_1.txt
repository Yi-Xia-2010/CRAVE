## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Solace

---

## World View
Solace Bay is a near-future coastal city slowly remade by sea and storm. Rising tides have shifted the urban rhythm: market mornings at the Promenade trade salted citrus and braided fishing rope for batteries and sandbags; rooftop gardens harvest rainwater; municipal planning meetings—once ceremonial—now decide who moves and who stays. The city’s political center is a glass-lined City Hall dominated by flood-model displays; the Maris Institute runs the scientific watch on currents, while grassroots encampments and activist brigades reclaim tarpaulin-strewn lots to plant tidal marshes. Culture is pragmatic and riven: generations who remember a low-lying neighborhood that vanished hold tight to place, while technocrats argue for steel and concrete to buy time. Religion and ritual survive in small altars to sea gods and daily horizon-watch gatherings. Class frictions appear as reconstruction contracts and relocation packages become leverage. Geography compresses: a long jagged skyline faces a sea that is both resource and threat; neighborhoods slope from preserved historic lanes into marshy fringes. Atmosphere is muted—sunlit markets, storm-washed nights, and a painterly coastal palette. The central conflict is intimate: can love endure when choices split community preservation from large-scale safety? No new leaders are introduced; the city's fate turns on Mara and the two people she either fights with or loves.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
Mara Lin, a practical idealist shaped by loss, returns to defend Solace Bay—caught between Elias Kade, a municipal pragmatist who favors engineered defenses, and Nova Duarte, a fierce organizer pushing radical, community-led retreat and restoration. The narrative follows a Man-in-a-Hole emotional arc: a descent into crisis as storms intensify and plans collide; a period of struggle and moral testing at the low point when a failed compromise costs part of a neighborhood; followed by a climb toward recovery as alliances form, hard choices are accepted, and durable solutions emerge. The story branches at key moral crossroads: whether Mara allies with Elias’s technocratic projects, Nova’s activist strategies, or attempts a hybrid that risks alienation from both. Each branch contains split decisions that alter the balance between immediate safety and long-term resilience, shaping both the city's fate and Mara’s romantic outcomes—ranging from triumphant cooperative partnerships to bittersweet separations and nuanced “true” endings that accept loss and slow progress. Throughout, visual detail (compass pendant, ink-stained trousers, Elias’s engraved keychain, Nova’s sea-glass beads) anchors first-person intimacy and the urgency of climate-driven choices.
