## Visual Novel Title
Tides Between Us

---

## World View
Solace Bay is a near-future coastal city re-learning how to live with the sea. Incremental sea-level rise and more violent storms have turned urban planning into an urgent moral exercise: reclaimed-material architecture sits alongside activist encampments; rooftop farms sprout on stepped terraces; municipal engineers roll out steel barriers while community gardeners coax tidal marshes back to life. Politically, the city tilts between technocratic stability (city planners, municipal contracts, and corporate interests) and grassroots sovereignty (organizers, neighborhood co-ops, and movement-run mutual aid). Class lines map roughly to elevation: better-funded districts cluster on higher mid-city ridges; low-lying neighborhoods are culturally rich but structurally vulnerable. Religion and ritual have adapted — small sea-blessing ceremonies mix with data-driven risk briefings. Geography is intimate: a narrow harbor, a long promenade, terraces rising toward municipal glass towers, and the islands of marsh slowly reclaiming the reclaimed. Atmosphere: painterly coastal realism — warm-gloom light, salt-streaked surfaces, textured materials, eco-grunge details. The emotional engine is climate-as-relationship: every policy is personal, every shoreline decision a story of care, loss, and the compromises love must survive.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Lin: a practical idealist whose childhood neighborhood was partly lost to a storm. The story begins with a sudden escalation — new tide projections and a storm warning expose the gaps between technocratic seawalls and community-led wetlands. The "man in a hole" emotional arc takes me down: public hearings, old survivor guilt, and a confrontation that forces a choice between aligning with Elias (municipal engineering), Nova (radical community adaptation), or forging a hybrid coalition. After the fall into crisis across the opening chapters, every route moves upward: recovery, negotiation, and rebuilding in different forms. Branch A follows an accelerated engineered path with Elias; Branch B follows radical, people-first restoration with Nova; Branch C attempts to stitch both camps into a durable coalition; Branch D offers hybrid experiments and professional scaling. Each split presents trade-offs in romance, ethics, and resilience. Endings are varied but all follow recovery — happy protection through engineered/ecological fusion, bittersweet victories requiring managed retreat, or "true" slow wins rooted in messy compromise and long-term partnership.

Notes on Structure and Emotional Curve:

- There are multiple split points: Chapter 3 (major strategic choice), and one split in each route (Chapter 5, Chapter 8, Chapter 11). Additional branching endpoints are distributed to keep every route from Chapter 1 to its final chapter at or below the estimated depth of 6 chapters.
- Every chapter is written in first-person, uses only provided main and secondary characters, and repeatedly references visual traits (hair, eyes, clothing, accessories) and locations from the world setting.
- Each chapter includes declared Valence and Arousal and is tagged [Fall] or [Rise] to conform to the target emotional curve.
