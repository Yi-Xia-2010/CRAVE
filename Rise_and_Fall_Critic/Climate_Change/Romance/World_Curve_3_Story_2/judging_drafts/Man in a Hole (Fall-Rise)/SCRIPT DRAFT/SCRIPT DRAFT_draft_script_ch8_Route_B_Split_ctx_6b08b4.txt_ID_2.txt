You are processed and fingerprinted under a light that feels clinical enough to strip decisions down to inked ridges. On the television, Mayor Heloise Chen's spokesperson speaks in a tone as smooth as oil.

You: (thinking) The phrasing tastes like gravel. 'A later date' becomes a hole where voices are supposed to be. The sea is already planning the schedule.

Professor Anika Bhat: "I know. But legal ruin and public chastisement will strip you of the very platform you need to argue for restoration. We have to pick our fights—choose where we make them matter."

Her words are not a cure; they are a scalpel. They cut and leave you raw. You realize "strategy" is not a moral lamp but a set of choices with collateral.
