## Visual Novel Title
Brineheart Rising

---

## World View
Near-future archipelago cityscape where the sea has become both threat and livelihood. The urban fabric stitches floating farms, raised boardwalks, and repurposed industrial relics into neighborhoods that ride seasonal surges. Political power is split between a redevelopment consortium pushing large-scale engineering and investment-led towers, and a mosaic of grassroots co-ops and small enterprises practicing regenerative design. Religion is diffuse—local ritual and tidal folklore persist alongside pragmatic science. Class lines are tied to ownership of land and access to capital: extractive developers promise jobs and hard defenses, while co-ops offer slower, less certain resilience rooted in communal knowledge. Geography alternates between sun-bleached piers and fogged wetlands; storms leave luminous aftermaths on wrought metal and moss. Tension is constant: councillors in polished pavilions negotiate with salt-streaked organizers; labs patch leaks while gardens thrive on barges tied to old piers. The mood is tactile and intimate—wet wood, oxidized metal, salt on skin—hopeful but fragile, where every policy choice shapes who stays and who leaves.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aria Solen, a pragmatic idealist leading the Tidehaven restoration project to protect the Brineheart Wetland Corridor. The story is a Cinderella arc: an initial climb as small wins consolidate community trust; a sudden political and personal collapse when a redevelopment proposal and an unforeseen betrayal undermine the project and personal relationships; and finally a recovery that forges durable, systemic change. Across branching paths, your decisions map onto different strategies—formal negotiation with Caden Holt, grassroots resistance with Eli Navarro, or truth-seeking exposure through political channels—which alter the texture of success: negotiated hybrid governance, hard-won but costly survival, outright displacement, or principled rupture that forces slow systemic reform. Romance with Eli evolves amid these choices: closeness grows through shared labor and risk, frays under strain, or reconvenes in different forms. Each route keeps the emotional curve: rise to a peak, fall into crisis, and a final rise toward a new equilibrium—though the personal and communal costs differ.
