## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Care

---

## World View
Near-future archipelago city-state strung along a rising sea. The municipal center balances on reclaimed causeways while outer neighborhoods cling to eroding piers and reed-stitched marshlands. Politically, a redevelopment consortium led by technocratic planners promises capital and stability through tall, stilted architecture and engineered seawalls; grassroots co-ops and community scientists advocate living shorelines, buoyant micro-economies, and distributed resilience. Culture mixes maritime folkways and pragmatic repair: oral tidal knowledge, communal fish smokehouses, and repurposed shipyards. Religion is private and syncretic; ritual offerings to tide and storm survive in markets and elder practices rather than formal institutions. Class lines run along land tenure and access to capital—co-ops and floating entrepreneurs face pressure from investors. Geography alternates between salt-saturated wetlands, fog-dimpi­ed boardwalks, and antiseptic council chambers whose glass contracts the light of storms. The atmosphere is tactile: damp wood, moss, oxidized metal, and soft painterly lighting after rain. Conflict centers on whether to prioritize short-term development that draws investment or to defend community-based restoration that protects culture and livelihoods. The overall tone is quietly urgent and intimate—environmental stakes are mirrored in interpersonal repair, and choices about shoreline design are choices about love, trust, and moral courage.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I—Aria Solen—return to my island to lead restoration of the Brineheart Wetland Corridor, seeking to protect my town and atone for a sibling lost to a surge. I balance scientific rigor from Tidehaven Research Lab with grassroots tactics alongside Eli, whose floating greenhouse Helix shelters livelihoods. Caden, the redevelopment director, offers scale and capital but threatens displacement. Across branching decisions I navigate alliances, public pressure, moral disclosures, and personal vulnerability.

The emotional curve follows Cinderella (Rise → Fall → Rise): in the Common Route I build momentum—community buy-in, pilot successes, and a small political opening (Rise). Each branch takes that momentum into a temporary peak where a high-profile win seems possible (Rise/peak), then a sudden collapse—political betrayal, an infrastructural failure, or forced compromise (Fall). In the final act each path finds recovery (Rise) through different means: negotiated hybrid governance (Happy), exposing corruption with cost and partial salvage (Bittersweet), personal integrity with systemic reckoning (True), or bittersweet personal growth after loss (Sad reframed as inner rise). Romance with Eli mirrors ecological repair; restoration of place parallels the repair of intimacy and trust.
