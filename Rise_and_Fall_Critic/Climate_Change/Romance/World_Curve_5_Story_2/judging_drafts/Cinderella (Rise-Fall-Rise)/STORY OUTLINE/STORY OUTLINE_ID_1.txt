## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Brineheart

---

## World View
Near-future archipelago city built from stitched islands and reclaimed piers, where sea-level rise and intensified storms have reshaped daily life. Political power orbits redevelopment consortia promising visible infrastructure and capital, while grassroots co-ops and restoration teams pursue living, adaptive strategies. The city’s class fault lines run between investors and displaced small enterprises; culture values maritime folklore and communal craft, and religion or ritual is expressed through seasonal tide rites rather than formal institutions. Geography alternates narrow causeways, floating markets, salt-marsh corridors, and low-slung research boathouses. The atmosphere—moist salt air, luminous post-storm skies, and the smell of peat and smoke—frames both grief and hope. Technology is pragmatic and constrained: patched solar sails, sensor buoys, and repurposed industrial controls. Conflict centers on whether to erect rapid, centralized defenses (seawalls, high towers) that promise economic influx but displace local life, or to invest in distributed, community-led living shorelines that protect culture at slower political cost. Social rituals, oral tidal knowledge and small-scale enterprise are the cultural anchors threatened by market-driven redevelopment. The mood is tactile and bittersweet: painterly realism—seafoam, slate, rust—where moss and oxidized metal meet hand-stitched resilience.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aria Solen, a pragmatic idealist leading restoration of the Brineheart Wetland Corridor. The narrative follows a Cinderella emotional arc: an initial rise as early successes consolidate hope; a sudden fall when political betrayal or an ecological shock undermines progress; and a final rise in which moral choices, community ingenuity, and personal repair yield a durable outcome—though not always the same flavor of victory. Across branching routes you balance technical restoration (seed mixes, check dams, tidal channels) with intimate choices about trust and love with Eli Navarro, the floating-garden builder; confront Caden Holt, the polished redevelopment director; and negotiate with Lina Voss and local elders like Old Man Rafi. Decisions steer the project toward cooperative hybrid solutions, public exposure of redevelopment malfeasance, compromise with painful trade-offs, or quiet resignation and renewed outward service. Romance unfolds as restoration: choosing openness with Eli mirrors choices to open or close the corridor to risk. Each branch preserves the Rise→Fall→Rise curve but offers different moral and emotional payoffs—ranging from a community-governed win to exile-for-truth or pragmatic survival with real losses.
