Internal: Measurable. You have always measured nets and pumps in smaller units — crank turns, stitch counts, the time it takes to fix a leak. This is the same instinct applied to the scale of your town. You feel the strange, rising hope that your technical mind can translate into Soren's charts.

You: "Then we make the measures include living systems, not just cubic meters moved or dollars allocated. Kelp beds, marsh acreage, and a maintenance labor algorithm — things that keep being tended. Soren watches you, as if assessing an unexpected variable that might make the model better rather than riskier. For a brief moment you see something unmasked in his expression — not softness, exactly, but the look of someone confronted with a problem he enjoys solving with more data.

Soren Voss: "Ambitious. I like that. If we can show funders how ecological investments reduce premium costs and increase social capital, they'll buy in. Thirty percent of coastal hardening replaced by nature-based solutions in phase one—doable, with offset monitoring."

Soren Voss: "Include your workshop as a formal node in the RFP. We'll write it into the operational plan."
