Soren's hand taps the data-bracelet at his wrist, bringing a zoning model into sharper relief: a ribbon of raised streets, clusters of living shorelines, proposed housing terraces.

Soren Voss: (strategic) "I can lock in funding and contractors now. I can also accept oversight if you and the Mayor define what that oversight looks like. What I worry about is scope-creep — too many vetoes, too much delay. I lose the political capital to get this built."

You: "Then the question is political capital for whom, and for what end?" Your words are blunt because things that matter rarely get saved by prettiness. Elias: (hopeful) "And the Board could include clause-based triggers — the pilot metric structure I mentioned. It's not a veto in the abstract; it's an evidence-based pause to correct course."

Jun: "Charter it with apprenticeship quotas and local-first contracting. If you build with us, build with our hands."

Riv: "And dashboards. Every quarter, people see the numbers. Everyone can watch the town's lifelines."
