## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Hearthbend is a near-future temperate coastal town balanced precariously between old livelihoods and engineered adaptation. Salt-scarred piers and patchwork sea defenses sit beside newly poured concrete promenades; community-run kelp nurseries rub shoulders with corporate-branded tide monitors. Local governance is a fraught negotiation of memory and expediency: Mayor Ansel Reed shepherds funding and permits, Seren Blake steers regional capital toward rapid, large-scale protection, and grassroots leaders like Etta Muir anchor cultural continuity. Science—embodied by Ilias Navarro and Dr. Hana Sato—offers tools and limits; journalism by Rafi Soto shapes public feeling. Geography compresses stakes into narrow margins: harborfront markets, mangrove-ruined shipyards, rooftop gardens, and a glass Skyhub that literally overlooks the town’s choices. Religion and explicit ideology are muted; instead, practices—fishing rites, shared seed banks, and community repair nights—carry moral weight. Social classes overlap: fisher families, tradespeople, municipal technicians, and corporate planners collide in daily commerce and council chambers. The tone is tactile and immediate—salt crust, wet wood, damp fabric—infusing romance with the political. Every choice about love is also a choice about the coast: preservation vs. rapid engineering, small-scale stewardship vs. top-down relocation.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Kestrel, a planner who has built my life around keeping Hearthbend whole. The story begins with an inexorable fall: a violent, unexpected surge that reopens old losses and pushes the town to the brink. From that nadir I push, collaborating with Ilias to restore kelp and eelgrass and argue for community-first adaptation, while Seren offers rapid, large-scale engineering and political leverage. The middle (rise) is a fragile success—hybrid pilots, repaired networks, and budding intimacy with Ilias give me hope. But every solution contains trade-offs: grants mean strings, seawalls mean displaced fishing plots, and even restoration needs sacrificial decisions. The final arc returns to ruin as a sudden policy swing, corporate pressure, or an engineering failure reverses gains. Depending on route choices, I either lose the town’s cultural fabric, accept painful relocation and an ambiguous partnership with Seren, or help seed a regional cooperative that scales but costs intimate things. Emotionally, each path follows Fall → Rise → Fall: initial disaster and guilt; temporary victories and personal closeness; and ultimate collapse or loss that forces acceptance. Romance is woven into these choices—love is enacted through stewardship, compromise, or painful separation.
