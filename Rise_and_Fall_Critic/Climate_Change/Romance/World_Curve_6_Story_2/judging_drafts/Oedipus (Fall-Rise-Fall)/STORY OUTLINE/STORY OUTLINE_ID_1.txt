## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Hearts

---

## World View
Hearthbend is a near-future temperate coastal town perched on a narrowing shore where the sea has been edging inland for decades. The town’s economy blends small-boat fishing, artisanal boatworks, and emergent green crafts; politics are local, porous, and perpetually negotiating with regional adaptation agencies and private infrastructure firms. Climate impacts — recurring storm surges, chronic salt intrusion, shifting fish runs — are the engine of daily life and civic debate. Social structures are dense and familiar: kin networks, neighborhood guilds, community gardens, and an oral memory held by elders like Etta Muir. Institutions exist (a mayor, research labs) but are stretched thin, alternating between grassroots improvisation and top-down proposals. Religion is not dominant; instead, there is an ethic of stewardship and a vernacular ritual around the sea: offerings of kelp, repaired nets, and rooftop gardens that double as sanctuaries. Geography emphasizes the seam where water meets human things: harborfronts, retrofitted rooftops, an engineered sea wall scarred by banners, and a repurposed shipyard overgrown with mangrove. The atmosphere is tactile — salt crust, wet wood, damp cloth — and visually rendered in soft watercolor coastal realism with cinematic light: muted pastels, neon civic accents, and weather effects that feel like characters themselves.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Maya Kestrel, Hearthbend-born planner and protector. The story begins with an accelerating crisis: another set of storms and an aggressive regional proposal for rapid, engineered adaptation that would reconfigure livelihoods and the town’s cultural geography. The emotional arc is Oedipus (Fall → Rise → Fall): first a descent as threats and betrayals surface; then a fragile, skillful upturn where Maya, Ilias, and parts of the town fashion promising, community-led solutions; finally a tragic downturn when choices that once seemed salvational ripple outward, culminating in systemic losses that cannot be reversed. Branching choices steer Maya toward different modes of compromise and partnership: leaning into community restoration and Ilias’s science, collaborating with Seren’s large-scale engineering, or navigating hybrid networks that aim to scale grassroots resilience. Every branch contains a temporary recovery — a tangible success, an intimate romance, or a policy win — followed by an unavoidable, devastating outcome that tests loyalty, love, and the moral cost of survival. The endings vary in personal attachments and civic legacies: from estrangement and cultural erasure to painful compromises that preserve fragments, but in every route the final fall reframes the meaning of “saving” Hearthbend.

Notes on Structure & Arc Compliance
- Narrative curve adhered to: every route begins with the protagonist’s Fall (Chapters 1–3), proceeds to a Rise (route-specific Chapters 4–5, 7–8, 10–11), and culminates in a Fall (endings: Chapters 6, 9, 12, 13, 14, 15). Each path from Chapter 1 to an ending is at most six chapters long.
- Branch points: Chapter 3 (major fork), Chapter 5 (Route A fork), Chapter 8 (Route B fork), Chapter 11 (Route C fork) — satisfying at least three distinct split points.
- All chapters use first-person POV as Maya Kestrel; visuals repeatedly reference Maya’s hair, clothing, accessories (notebook, coral pendant, compass charms) and consistent visual descriptions of Ilias, Seren, and secondary characters.
- No new main characters are introduced; secondary characters appear in supporting roles only.
- The endings reflect the Oedipus-style Fall-Rise-Fall: temporary successes followed by unavoidable social/ecological losses that reframe personal and communal relationships and the romance arcs (Ilias/Seren) depending on choices.
