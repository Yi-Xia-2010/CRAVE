## Visual Novel Title
Saltwick Rising

---

## World View
Saltwick is a small coastal town in the 2040s reshaped by the sea: harbor sheds lean under salt crust, patched dunes keep time with new seawalls, and trailer-style labs sit on raised platforms. Politics is tense between community stewardship and rapid modernization; the municipal council negotiates with outside developers while volunteer squads stitch temporary defenses after every storm. Technology is uneven — efficient hydroponics and local microgrids sit beside rusted diesel generators. Culture values lineage (old boatwrights and fisher elders) and care work (community kitchens and shared labor days). Religion and ritual remain local and practical: tide-blessing gatherings at the lighthouse, feast-days to honor harvests, and the cultural memory held by elders like Oba Kofi. Class divisions are subtle but sharp: multigenerational residents, returning experts like me (Alys Maren), and entrepreneurial outsiders embodied by Mayor Celine Harrow. Geography is intimate: a crescent harbor, the Glass Marsh, a cliff-top lighthouse greenhouse, a lab on pilings, and battered north ridges where sand and makeshift seawalls contend with incoming storms. Atmosphere is melancholic but verdant — saltwind, the smell of kelp, and the uneasy optimism of townspeople who keep fixing things because leaving is not an option.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I return to Saltwick resolved to restore seagrass beds and shorelines to protect the town. Early momentum — volunteer crews, pilot seagrass mats, and visible local engineering — produces a rapid ascent in community trust, funding, and romantic warmth with Ivo. The town experiences a brief triumphant peak: seedlings take, harbor repair projects finish, and a ribbon-cutting sense of safety settles over Saltwick. Then, as if on cue, hubris, compromised deals, and a fierce storm expose structural and moral faults. Across branching routes I must choose alliances and compromises — with Ivo’s immediate pragmatic defenses, with Celine’s rapid funding, or with Dr. Leyna’s patient restoration science — each choice leads to a distinct version of the same arc: a bright rise to achievement followed by a catastrophic fall. Some endings emphasize moral victory at a cost; others emphasize displacement, heartbreak, or the revelation of corruption. The emotional core is always first-person: I must weigh love against duty, community trust against expediency, and my own fear of betrayal against the town’s need to survive.

- Major split points: Chapter 3 (town strategy), Chapter 5 (Route A: contractor choice), Chapter 8 (Route B: exposure vs completion), Chapter 11 (Route C: hybrid vs strict restoration).
- Entry Conditions explicitly link: Chapters 4, 7, and 10 begin from Chapter 3 Options A/B/C respectively. Branch endpoints remain within the allowed Estimated Depth: any path from Chapter 1 to an ending visits no more than 6 chapters (for example: 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 = 6 chapters).
- Visual consistency: all character designs and location descriptions strictly use provided appearances: my dark chestnut hair, gray-green eyes, signature olive jacket and compass; Ivo's canvas cap and hazel eyes; Celine's silver bob and navy tailoring; Dr. Leyna, Oba Kofi, Maya, Ronan; settings like Glass Marsh, Muri Lab, Old Lighthouse Greenhouse, Harrow Hall, and North Beach Ridge.
- POV: All chapter narratives are first-person as Alys Maren, centering internal conflict between love and duty against climate stakes.
