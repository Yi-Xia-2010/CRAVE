When the gray of evening thickens, you walk to the water and drop a pressed strip of seaweed into the tide. It disappears into the hush of after — a small offering. You think of all the times you have refused help, and of the ways that refusal has multiplied hurt. You think of the ledger and how unkindly it lays things out: entries with names, dates, decisions.

Elias sends a message later that night: one line, then nothing. You do not know whether he will leave Corvena for good or whether his silence is a new kind of survival. You close your eyes and imagine him, alone in a small room, running the numbers like a priest counting rosary beads.
