## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Ash and Salt

---

## World View
Corvena Bay is a near-future coastal city reshaped by water: neighborhoods stitched together with reclaimed timber, rusted flood gates, and community gardens that double as storm cisterns. Political power is centralized in a city council that balances dwindling budgets against rising tides; planners promote engineered barriers and managed retreat while grassroots cooperatives push regenerative, low-tech solutions. Religion is diffuse — ritual tied to seasons and sea; cultural memory is kept by elders in communal ceremonies. Class fractures have widened: property owners and developers lobby for large-scale engineering to protect economic cores, while fishing and dock communities risk displacement. Geography is stitched: salt-weathered boardwalks, makeshift Tideworks labs, rooftop sky-farms, and a low Basin garden where mangroves are coaxed back to life. The atmosphere is humid and febrile — salt-gray skies, streaked sunlight after storms, film grain of moisture on every surface. Everyone lives by a ledger of choices, small moral calculus measured in lives sheltered or surrendered. Romance, kinship, and policy are entangled: adaptation decisions become intimate tests of loyalty and conscience, and the human cost of infrastructure is always visible in the worn faces and patched clothing of those who remain.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Kestrel, a neighborhood organizer trying to protect my community as Corvena Bay buckles under accelerating storms and political triage. At the core is a triangle: me (community-first, practical), Elias Navin (a patient marine scientist with regenerative designs), and Jun Park (a decisive municipal planner pushing city-scale engineered solutions and managed retreat). The story follows a downward arc of tragedy: miscalculations, compromises, and the cruel timing of a storm create cascading losses. Early chapters depict organizing, meetings at the Strand, and fragile alliances formed in Tideworks Lab and the Salted Rooftop Garden. A council decision splits my trust — I must choose whom to align with: grassroots resistance, a scientific pilot with Elias, or Jun’s enforced plan that trades neighborhoods for the city’s core. Each decision yields different failures: a failed pilot that washes away hope and someone I love; a political compromise that mandates relocation and fractures neighborhoods; or a hardline enforcement that causes avoidable casualties. Across branches the stakes escalate emotionally and physically: shelters overflow, infrastructure fails, and the moral weight of choices crushes relationships. The endings land in loss: violent storm, forced displacement, broken partnerships, or a hollow victory that costs too much — all consistent with the Fall arc.

Jun’s emissary sent a city memo overnight proposing prioritized floodwalls for the core and "de-risking" of marginal blocks. My stomach tightens. I promise Rosa I’ll fight for fair protections, but I also know our resources are thin. I keep my compass pendant tucked under my sweater. Every choice feels like balancing someone’s rooftop against another family’s chimney. The air tastes like metal; the whole town waits to see what cracks first.

## ROUTE C (Refuse both; lead independent community resistance)
