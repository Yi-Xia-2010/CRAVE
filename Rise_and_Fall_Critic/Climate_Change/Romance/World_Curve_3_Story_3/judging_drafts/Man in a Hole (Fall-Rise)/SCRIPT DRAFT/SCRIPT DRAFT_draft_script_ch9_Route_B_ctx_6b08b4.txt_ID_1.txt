The room is quiet enough that you can hear the old heater sigh. Somewhere outside, waves kiss the shore with an indifferent rhythm that is no longer only threat — it is part of the world you are learning to work with.

You: "If this is the first sunrise of a long mending, then we are the people holding the seams."

The committee formalizes the oversight charter. Names are read. Signatures dry into the margins of the document like small promises. You: (your internal voice) The red thread at your wrist slackens but does not fall away. It is tied, now, not only to a private promise but knotted into the communal work you have shaped. The guilt that once felt like a stone has become a stone you can set into the foundation.

You walk outside with Noah at your side and Elias a few paces ahead, hands tucked into pockets, the sea folding itself along the shore. The town looks like it has been injured and is learning to stitch itself. It's not a picture you framed as victory, but it is, unmistakably, growth.

The town breathes. You breathe with it.
