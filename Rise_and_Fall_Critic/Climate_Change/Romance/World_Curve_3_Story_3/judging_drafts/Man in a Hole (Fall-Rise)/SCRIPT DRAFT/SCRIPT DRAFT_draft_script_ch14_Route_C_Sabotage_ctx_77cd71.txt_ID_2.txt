It is a cage and a gift at once. Accountability from the outside will be painful. But it also buys runway — long enough for the pilot to demonstrate that community-led projects can be both effective and responsible.

Time softens edges and reveals new textures. Mayor Lillian Cho: (at the podium, voice steadier than you expected) "The town will not be fixed overnight. But tonight we start an accountable, community-led approach that respects both the past and the need for safety."

Narration:
You watch the mayor speak and think about glaciers again — but here, the glacier is moving because we shoveled the ice a little, not because she ordered it. Gussets of compromise hold up a larger humanity. The legal clamps didn't choke the life out of the town; they forced you to be legible and to invite others into the ledger.

Finn: (pulling you aside, practical) "You did right by the people who couldn't wait for reports. You also fixed it so the town can live through the next storm. I'm proud."

Pride from Finn tastes like salt and something sunlighted. You let it in.
