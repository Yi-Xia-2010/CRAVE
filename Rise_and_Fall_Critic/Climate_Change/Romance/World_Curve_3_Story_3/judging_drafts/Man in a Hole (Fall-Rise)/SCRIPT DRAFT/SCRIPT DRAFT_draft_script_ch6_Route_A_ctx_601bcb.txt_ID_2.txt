[Scene: Rooftop Sanctuary | Midmorning]
[Visual: Neighbors gathered in a semicircle around a whiteboard propped on a crate; sketches of modular barriers are pinned with clothespins]
[Music: Lighter strings; a heartbeat of optimism]

You: "And maintenance isn't just technical." (you look at the faces gathered — young people with paint-stained jackets, older folks with careful hands, kids asleep on a lap) "It's human. Dr. Hana Park: (a small smile) "That's the thing the models can't measure: the social redundancy. It's why your proposal landed on my desk at all."

Narration:
Rosa's café is the next stop, but the roof feels like confession — a place where plans come from breathing in the same air as the plans' eventual beneficiaries. The whiteboard is scrawled with times and names; someone has drawn a sun with a worried face next to the cistern schedule.

The bargaining is a strange, necessary intimacy: you trading a suspicion for a set of safeguards, naming non-negotiables until you both have a map you can follow. Noah's offer is a rope with a clasp — secure if you check the gear.

Narration:
You find a quiet moment at the edge of the sanctuary and touch the dented compass at your throat. The red thread is still there, frayed but intact. You tie a new knot in it, not as a burden but as a marking — a working knot, a plan to be checked and retied when needed.

Narration:
You do not pretend the future will be smooth. You do not frame this victory as a final triumphant banner. It is a beginning and a renewal: conditional grant funding, community oversight, technical expertise, and a network that can now scale practices beyond piecemeal survival. Love — with Elias, with your family, with the strange, stubborn town of Saltbridge — deepens because it is shared labor, because tenderness is now the medium of repair, not an escape from it.

It feels like an open door.
