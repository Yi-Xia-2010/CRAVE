## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Havenport

---

## World View
Havenport is a near-future coastal town clinging to the shoreline in 2040, where salt and circuitry meet. The town's economy still smells of fish oil and tar, but solar arrays, modular storm barriers, and floating terraces push a new skyline against gaffed masts. Politically, local councils and regional developers tug at authority: grassroots collectives argue for participatory stewardship while corporate-backed projects promise rapid, engineered protection. Culture hangs on rituals — an annual tide-mending, the boatmaker's knot ceremony — that risk dissolving under relocation plans. Class tension emerges between long-time fishers and newly arrived technicians and investors; religion is pragmatic ritual more than dogma, centered on sea-tales and community oaths. Geography compresses memory and danger: the boardwalk and old boathouse, the Greenwave terraces, a clinical Coastal Research Hub, and a glassy Tideguard Corporate Pavilion jut into the exposed edges. The atmosphere feels melancholic and resilient: painterly light, salt-streaked façades, and neon tech surges after storms. The core conflict is intimate — love and loyalty versus large-scale solutions and trade-offs — lived through small acts (saved seedlings, a demolished dock) that reveal policy consequences. There is no easy salvation, only choices that echo through neighborhoods and relationships.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Marin Sato, born of Havenport weather and boathouse sweat. My aim is to guide my town toward community-led adaptation that honors memory and sustains living shoreline systems. The story arcs downwards: each choice we make becomes a narrowing tide that erodes options and relationships. Early hope — shared plans with Ariana Voss and the restoration collective — collides with Cassian Rhee's sleek, decisive redevelopment. I must choose confrontation, negotiation, or pilot innovation; each path seems to promise protection but ends up demanding compromises that exact human cost. Branches explore different strategies: public resistance, quiet bargaining, and experimental ecological engineering. In every route, losses accumulate: partial victories that hollow neighborhoods, betrayed trust, demolished boathouses, or the sacrifice of intimacy for civic duty. Romance with Ariana is present in every branch but strained by ethical trade-offs and failures. Secondary figures — Ivy, Elder Tomas, and Lina — intensify stakes: their loyalties, reports, and warnings shift public will. The tragedy curve is strict: emotions fall and collapse; endings settle into sorrowful outcomes — displacement, fractured love, and governance born of pain. Even the "true" compromise is bittersweet: resilience secured, but at the cost of innocence and intimacy, an ongoing burden rather than a tidy victory.
