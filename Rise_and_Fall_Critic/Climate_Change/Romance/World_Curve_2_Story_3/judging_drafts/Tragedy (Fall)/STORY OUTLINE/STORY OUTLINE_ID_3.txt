## Visual Novel Title
Tidefall: Letters from Havenport

---

## World View
Havenport is a near-future coastal town where fishing piers meet experimental seawalls and rooftop nurseries. By 2040, rising tides and increasingly violent storms have forced a brittle hybrid of old practices and emergent tech: reclaimed terraces perch over flood-prone alleys; volunteers in neon vests braid kelp lines beside corporate engineers in water-resistant blazers. Local governance is fragmented—neighborhood councils, a grassroots restoration collective, and an external infrastructure firm led by Tideguard. Economic pressure, cultural memory, and climate urgency collide: elders keep boatmaking lore while developers promise immediate protection through hard engineering and private capital. Religion and ritual persist in small ways—harbor blessings, annual tide-watch memorials—but civic life is dominated by pragmatic debates about relocation, access, and who pays for resilience. Geography compresses debate into thin strips of shoreline; the boardwalk, the Greenwave garden, the Coastal Research Hub, and the Tideguard Pavilion are staging grounds for friction. Atmosphere is melancholic yet stubbornly hopeful: salt-worn faces, patched tarps, and neon field gear glint beneath storm clouds. Everywhere, choices ask whether to preserve place or prioritize survival, whether to love someone who represents a different solution, and what counts as betrayal when stakes are global and immediate.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I—Marin Sato—return to Havenport to stitch community-led adaptation into a town frayed by storms. I carry a scuffed brass compass, a stained field notebook with pressed leaves, and the weight of a boathouse that was once my family's anchor. Ariana Voss arrives as a radiant outsider with living-seawall proposals; Cassian Rhee arrives with corporate-backed rapid defenses. Across multiple branching paths, I navigate the conflict between love and duty: whether to ally with Ariana’s experimental, community-centered engineering, negotiate with Cassian’s top-down stability, or attempt a fragile compromise. Each decision pushes me deeper into conflict and sacrifice. Because the narrative arc is tragedy (Fall), the trajectory is a steady downward spiral: initial optimism buckles under escalating losses—failed projects, fractured trust, the demolition of places I love, and relationships strained by impossible trade-offs. Branches diverge into different flavors of setback—public humiliation, legal defeat, or personal exile—yet all routes culminate in grim reckonings where I must choose what to save and who I become after loss. Romance intensifies stakes: choosing love affects community outcomes; choosing community strains love. Endings vary in scale and scar tissue but share a painful decline that forces hard truth: resilience requires hard choices, and none are without casualty.
