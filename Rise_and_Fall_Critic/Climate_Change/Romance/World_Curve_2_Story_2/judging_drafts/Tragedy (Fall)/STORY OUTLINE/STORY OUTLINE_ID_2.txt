## Visual Novel Title
Harborfall Tides

---

## World View
Harborfall is a narrow coastal town in the 2040s, a frontline community where salt, fog, and retrofitted technology overlap. Municipal flood berms and raised promenades sit beside crumbling fishing coves; corporate plazas of engineered certainty press against the rooftop greenhouses of grassroots resilience. Politically, Harborfall is a battleground between municipal pragmatism—represented by Mayor Elena Rossi and her tight budget calculus—and private capital, embodied by LuxCorp's CEO Lucia "Lux" Moreno. Culture leans toward communal memory: fishermen like Tomas Bianchi hold traditional ecological knowledge in their hands, while younger engineers and scientists such as Kaito Sato bring data-driven optimism tempered by guilt. Religion and ritual take muted forms—lighthouse vigils, seasonal planting—that anchor identity even as rising seas rewrite property lines. Class hierarchy is subtle but real: longline fishers and diner owners rent the aging harborfront; LuxCorp brings contracts and high-paying technicians; grant-funded researchers float between both worlds. Geography compresses consequence—mangrove estuaries, a storm-broken lighthouse, the promenade and a rooftop greenhouse are all within earshot of the same tide. Atmospherically, Harborfall smells of brine and oil, feels fog-lit and film-grained, and hums with the tension of urgent choices about technology, sacrifice, and love.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Inoue) attempt to steer Harborfall through an escalating climate crisis while navigating fragile, consequential relationships. The story follows a descending tragic arc: each attempt to control fate—through confrontation, compromise, or technical fixes—yields new failures or costs. The communal heart of restoration collides with LuxCorp’s promise of immediate protection, and Kaito's return complicates trust between technical possibility and moral responsibility. I move from steady stewardship into increasingly fraught gambits: public confrontations, experimental deployments of sensors and prototypes, and politically explosive decisions at town meetings. Personal stakes escalate—old grief about my brother resurfaces, my bond with Kaito strains under competing ethics, and Lux's polished certainty forces choices that fracture community trust. Split decisions lead to different forms of loss—overt disaster, eroded tradition, or the corrosive isolation of principled refusal. Across all branches the mood falls: failed safeguards, ill-timed storms, betrayed partnerships, and civic fractures accumulate into a nadir where Harborfall is reshaped and my relationships are scarred or severed. Romance weaves through every collapse, never a panacea—love becomes another vector for loss, compromise, and quiet regret.
