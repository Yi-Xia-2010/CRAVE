## Visual Novel Title
Harborfall Rising Tide

---

## World View
Harborfall is a near-future coastal town caught at the hinge between ancestral tides and engineered certainty. The political structure is municipal—Mayor Elena Rossi presides over a small council that answers to regional funders and wary voters. Outside investors, represented by Lux Moreno's firm, pressure the town with capital and promises; grassroots groups, led by volunteers and elders such as Tomas Bianchi, push for slow, ecological approaches. Culture here is maritime and communal: fishers remember names of currents, rooftop greenhouses double as meeting halls, the diner is a civic pulse. Religion is diffuse—rituals are small, sea-blessed gestures and memorials for storm losses. Class tension threads through laborers, municipal staff, and visiting technocrats; corporate teal meets salt-scarred prom wood. Geography is a network of raised berms, mangrove estuaries, a deteriorating lighthouse, and a promenade where tide lines register memory. Conflict centers on whether to accept LuxCorp’s large-scale engineered sea wall and automated flood control (fast, funded, top-down) or to commit to community-led nature-based restoration—mangrove replanting, living berms, redistributed zones (slow, authentic, sacrificial). The atmosphere is weathered nostalgia: fog, salt-streaked paint, and constant small repairs, with neon corporate signage sometimes bleeding into the scene like an intrusion.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I narrate as Maya Inoue, Harborfall native and coastal restoration coordinator. The story is a tragedy in a descending arc: each choice, however well-intentioned, deepens loss. Early chapters set stakes—rising seas, old debts, and the complicated return of Kaito Sato and the polished arrival of Lux Moreno. A pivotal split forces me to choose how to negotiate the town's path. Branches explore different alliances: committing to collaboration with Kaito's small-scale engineered proposals plus nature-based work; leaning toward LuxCorp's sweeping wall and funding; or doubling down on grassroots resistance. Each route contains further splits—compromises, tactical errors, or betrayals—that cascade into worse outcomes: funding diverted, an ill-timed pilot failure, or a storm that exposes hubris. Secondary characters—Hana at the diner, Tomas on his boat, Dr. Noor advising on trade-offs, Mayor Rossi balancing budgets—appear as witnesses and catalysts. The emotional curve is a steady fall: losses accumulate (property, livelihoods, trust), relationships fray or rupture, and my survivor's guilt intensifies into blame. Endings vary but converge on tragic nadirs: displacement, fractured love, or hard-won but Pyrrhic solutions. Even "best" outcomes come at grievous cost—my resolve remains, but Harborfall is altered irreparably.

Notes on Structure and Emotional Curve:
- Every chapter is in first-person POV as Maya Inoue and adheres to the Fall (Tragedy) arc: each scene accumulates loss, compromised choices, or cornered ethics leading to a nadir.
- There are three major split points: Chapter 3 (primary split A/B/C), Chapter 5 (Route A split), Chapter 8 (Route B split), and Chapter 11 (Route C split)—meeting the requirement for at least three splits.
- All chapters are tagged [Fall]; valence is negative, arousal ranges to reflect crisis intensity.
- Visual details consistently reference character appearances (Maya's teal hair streak and dive watch; Kaito's sea-green eyes and field gear; Lux's platinum ponytail and mirrored goggles) and locations (Promenade, Rooftop Greenhouse, Kaito's Field Lab, LuxCorp Plaza, Mangrove Estuary).
- Route depth constraint: every path from Chapter 1 to an Ending comprises no more than six chapters in sequence (for example: 1→2→3→4→5→6; or 1→2→3→7→8→9; or 1→2→3→10→11→12; alternate endings at 13–15 are reachable as the 6th node where applicable).
- No new main characters are introduced; secondary characters are used as provided.
