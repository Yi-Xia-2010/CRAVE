## Visual Novel Title
Tide of Small Failures

---

## World View
Harborfall is a near-future coastal town perched between memory and a changing sea. Once a fishing village, it has become a frontline of climate adaptation in the 2040s: retrofitted piers, raised berms, rooftop gardens and municipal flood berms stitched into the town fabric. Political life is brittle—local councils juggle limited budgets, investor promises, and the lived needs of fishers and elders. Corporate actors bring polished solutions and money; grassroots networks counter with slow, place-specific ecological restoration. Culture is a mixture of maritime labor pride, communal dinners at Hana’s diner, and an anxious hope that something can be kept of the town’s soul. Religion and ritual survive as practical talismans—wire-crossed tide charts, lighthouse bells, and memorials for storm losses. Geography is intimate: the promenade, mangrove estuary, storm-broken lighthouse, a rooftop greenhouse, Kaito’s field lab in a boathouse, and LuxCorp’s raised, reflective plaza. Class lines are visible in whose houses are already elevated and who sleeps in converted storage near the quay. Ecology is a character: salt-streaked paint, mossed rails, tide-lines on buildings that mark both history and warning. Conflict pulses between short-term engineered fixes and slower, community-rooted restoration; romance is tangled with responsibility.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Inoue) navigate a downward spiral of decisions in Harborfall: each choice meant to protect the town catalyzes new harms. The story is a tragedy: initial compromises, misjudged trusts, and small errors compound into civic fracture, infrastructural failure, and intimate loss. Three initial strategies are available from a single civic crossroads: negotiate with LuxCorp for funding and a sea wall; back Kaito's community-centered technological pilots; or mobilize a citizen-led referendum that rejects big players. Across each branch the emotional tone steadily worsens—promises turn hollow, prototypes fail in storms, social cohesion frays, and survivors shoulder grief. Romantic threads with Kaito strain as I prioritize community outcomes; Lux's charisma becomes a double-edged sword. Secondary figures (Hana, Tomas, Dr. Noor, Mayor Elena) react visibly—some betray or distance me. Every decision attempts a practical fix but leads to unintended consequences: displacement, investor-driven escalation, prototype-triggered disaster, or the quiet burnout of stubborn resistance. Endings all descend into loss: a failed corporate deployment causing worse flooding; a compromised protection that scatters tradition; a fractured community where I leave; or a bitter, principled victory that costs cherished lives and relationships. The curve never lifts; my choices produce new failures until the nadir.
