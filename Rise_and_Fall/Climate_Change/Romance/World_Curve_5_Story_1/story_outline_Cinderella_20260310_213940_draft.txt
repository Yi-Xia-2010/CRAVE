## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promise

---

## World View
Salthaven is a near-future coastal city where the sea has begun to reclaim its edges. Weathered wooden piers and clapboard shops cling to raised concrete blocks and newly installed stilts; cranes and arcology scaffolds silhouette against a sky that constantly smells faintly of salt and storm. The town lives by a brittle code of collective memory and practical repair: elders remember festivals on dry sand, activists remember flooded cellars, planners remember a zoning map stained by stormwater. Politics are local and urgent — community councils, developer liaisons, and nonprofit researchers argue under the same fog, each with different models of "saving" Salthaven. Religion and ritual exist side-by-side with data: memorial plaques for lost houses, seeding ceremonies for salt-tolerant plants, and monthly town gatherings on the Civic Rooftop Garden that double as storm drills. Class lines are visible in who can afford raised foundations or engineered buyouts; everyone breathes the same briny air, but not everyone has a dry upstairs room.

The atmosphere is bittersweet and pragmatic — sun through storm clouds, salt-bleached signage, and low, persistent hope. Culture revolves around places: the creaking Boardwalk, the warm, misted Tidal Conservatory, and the sterile Arcology Development Site that promises security but risks erasing the town’s texture. Conservation, activism, and techno-engineering are competing languages for survival; each choice reshapes neighborhoods and relationships.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Aria Marin, returning planner and reluctant hometown negotiator pulled between passionate organizer Kai Solano and pragmatic engineer Noah Vega. The emotional arc follows a Cinderella-style rise, catastrophic fall, and eventual, hard-won rise: early momentum toward community-led resilience (personal and political) reaches a hopeful peak, a crisis precipitated by an escalation of tactics and corporate pressure sends everything into a painful collapse, and finally a difficult synthesis is forged where relationships and plans are remade into something more durable.

Across branching routes, your choices steer whether you align with direct action, institutional negotiation, or experimental compromise. Each path hits the same structural beats — an initial rise in influence and optimism, a fall marked by betrayal or loss, and a final recovery that is emotionally tempered but forward-looking. Romance with Kai blooms or frays depending on whether you match his moral intensity; Noah’s steel-blue gaze can become either ally or emblem of compromise. Secondary characters — Maya, Elder Tomas, and Dr. Linh — anchor personal stakes and provide alternative lenses on memory, science, and the cost of progress. Endings range from communal hybrid solutions to bitter concessions to corporate triumphs; a rare "true" ending arises when you coax all three protagonists into hard ethical collaboration.
