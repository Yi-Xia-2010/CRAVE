## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Lumen Bay, a small coastal town in the 2030s, is defined by salt, reclaimed marsh, and the collision of grassroots resilience with corporate technocracy. The bay’s geography has been reshaped by rising seas: wooden boardwalks patched against high tides, community rain gardens perched beside concrete flood barriers, and a gleaming AquaWorks Research Station on stilts. Politically, the town is split: local councils and volunteer networks demand community-first ecological repair while a powerful marine-tech firm—led by Dahlia Kestrel—proposes a rapid, engineered sea barrier, The Arc. Culture is maritime and intergenerational; fishing lineage and oral knowledge carry weight, even as new jobs and modern promises tempt the town. Religion and ritual center on memorials for lives lost to storms and seasonal tide rites; class tensions rise where development promises income but threatens tradition. Conflict threads through everyday life: the smell of wet wood and compost, the hum of pumps at AquaWorks, and the flash of teal corporate branding against the marsh’s deep greens. The atmosphere is painterly semi-realism—moody, salt-bleached, with warm close-ups in tender moments and harsh, neon-tinged lighting around corporate structures. The story’s engine is the ethical trade-off between fast technocratic safety and slow, community-rooted restoration.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I play Maya Reyes, a marine ecologist and local organizer determined to restore the Marsh of Tides through community-led work. When Dahlia Kestrel arrives with The Arc—a slick, large-scale sea barrier promising rapid protection—Lumen Bay polarizes. I navigate meetings, fieldwork, and an uneasy attraction with Elias Kwan, a third-generation fisher who embodies the town’s traditions. Through a sequence of choices I broker (or reject) partnerships, negotiate design compromises, and mobilize neighbors. Each route accelerates into an apparent victory—a surge of funding, rapid construction, media praise, and a sense that the bay will be saved. The Icarus arc unfolds: our successes climb fast and intoxicatingly, but hubris, unforeseen environmental force, and ethical blind spots precipitate a catastrophic collapse—a storm exposes structural flaws, or social trust fractures. Across branches the crash differs in scale and consequence: from a painful but reconstructible setback (bittersweet or true) to a devastating failure that scatters lives (sad). Romance with Elias threads through these decisions, strained or deepened by what we choose. Every ending closes with the catastrophic fall, followed by varied seeds of resolve: community rethinking, relocation, or a tempered partnership built from hard lessons.
