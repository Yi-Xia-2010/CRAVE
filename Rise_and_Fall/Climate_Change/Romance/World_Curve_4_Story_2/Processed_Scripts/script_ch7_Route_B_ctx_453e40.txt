You kneel by a newly planted trench, press mud against a plug, and imagine lines on a map becoming ridges on the land. The first time you feel the tide pull and the planted roots hold, something in your chest unclenches.

For a few weeks the rise is real. You sleep in a bed that feels lighter because people are awake and doing. You allow yourself to imagine the marsh as a defense built from memory and muscle rather than a screen of steel.
