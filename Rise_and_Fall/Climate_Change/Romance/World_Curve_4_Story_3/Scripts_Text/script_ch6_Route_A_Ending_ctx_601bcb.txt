Where the engineered berms met hard surfaces, whole lengths have sheared out and left scoured channels. A camera flashes. You do not flinch. The press has already painted a story: "Unforeseen Failure" runs under a photo of a collapsed terrace. The mayor's name appears in a sidebar. The city's glass heart is very loud now.

Resident: "You said it would keep us! You promised—"

Maya folds her arms, jaw tight, and then turns to you with a look that is equal parts fury and practical steadiness.

Maya: "This is what happens when big firms control what we can't touch. People get priced out and then flooded out."

Jonah Reyes: "They called for sabotage on the radio. NovaSeis says someone tampered with the array."

You feel the cage of contracts closing: proprietary locks, legal pauses, a public relations campaign that reframes failure as criminal sabotage. Elias's spokespeople repeat the line like a prayer—no fault, only malice.
