It is illegal, yes—no permits, no stamped plans—but it is honest, and for a few hours their faces look tired and fierce in the headlamps, not like criminals. They are trusted neighbors: a woman whose son you taught to read tides, a retired deckhand who keeps time by the rhythm of his lungs.
