Mara Voss: "That's why we're building from the ground up. Seedlines, oyster nurseries, paid shifts… We can create income without waiting for the developer to decide our fate."

Elias "Eli" Calder: "And what if the developer walks in with sealed bids and a contractor who pays in promises and bagged cash? What if people can't wait for the long arc of restoration?"

Mara Voss: "I know. I'm trying to build both. It's messy. It's slow. But it's something we control."

Elias "Eli" Calder: "Sometimes what we control isn't enough. Sometimes the town needs a jolt."

Elias "Eli" Calder: "Help me sketch some raised docks that let fishers keep going even when tides rise. We stitch it to your mats. Small jobs on both sides."

Mara Voss: "Then we'll stand," you say, and the vow sits between you like a weight you both recognize and fear.
