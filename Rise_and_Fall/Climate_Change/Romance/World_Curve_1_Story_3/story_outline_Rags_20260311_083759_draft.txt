## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound: Seeds of the Promenade

---

## World View
Port Lyra is a near-future coastal metropolis remade by the ocean's insistence. Rising seas and fiercer storms have redrawn property lines and livelihoods: low-lying districts float on patched pontoons, reclaimed wetlands nestle against engineered seawalls, rooftop farms and market greenhouses crown retrofitted warehouses. Politically, the city is a tangle of corporate consultancies, city council factions, and grassroots coalitions. Lucia Montrose represents swift, large-scale remediation through corporate-managed seawalls and redevelopment; Councilor Mateo Qu negotiates policy under media scrutiny; community organizers and wetland keepers like Eda Nal advocate for living, nature-led adaptation. Cultural life is biophilic and inventive — markets exchange salvage, rooftop greenhouses hum with barter, and emergent rituals honor tidal calendars. Religion and ritual are less institutional than local: elders invoke ancestral shoreline knowledge; currents of practical ecology inform community identity. Class hierarchies follow elevation: higher ground correlates with political influence and safety, lower-lying quarters host hybrid economies and DIY resilience. Conflict centers on whether to prioritize engineered rapid protection for the economic core or to invest in wetlands and community-led defenses that preserve biodiversity and dignity. The city feels urgent but intimate — salt-wet air, intermittent acid-rain advisories, the political hum of public hearings, and lovers who weigh heart against design.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Solenne, a wetland keeper and community organizer whose home, the Drowned Garden, is threatened by a city-scale adaptation plan. The story is a first-person arc of steady progress ([Rise]) in which every choice increases agency, resources, or emotional reconciliation. Early scenes ground the conflict: Elias Harrow, the engineer I love, is contracted with Lucia Montrose’s consultancy to design coastal defenses. At a City Hall hearing (the common split), I must decide whether to pursue radical direct action, negotiated partnership with engineers, or legal/political appeals. Each route yields a different kind of success rather than success vs. failure: a community-driven hybrid pilot (Happy), a pragmatic compromise that secures protections and funding while preserving some wetlands (Bittersweet), and a policy-savvy, iterative plan that embeds living elements into engineering via a negotiated pilot and continued co-management (True). Along the way I deepen trust with Elias through collaborative design, shrewd organizing with Rani and Eda Nal, and tactical political moves with Councilor Mateo Qu. Branching choices emphasize different strengths: charismatic mobilization, technical co-design, or legal/policy strategy. The arc culminates in uplift: wetlands preserved or integrated, livelihoods stabilized, and a matured romance formed on a foundation of mutual respect and shared craft.
