Process does not stop a porch from failing.

[Narration — Denouement]
The town will endure in the ways towns do: patched, rearranged, grieving. Some families left quietly after buyouts—faces that used to wave now gone. Some livelihoods stabilized; fishermen relearned rhythms. The pier's lights flick on and off as people count small recoveries and larger losses. The monitoring board drafts new clauses. The legal machine grinds slowly. Enforcement is bureaucratic and late; it is a poor makeshift justice.

You have a person who will stay. You have a town that is safer in some ways and more fragile in others. You have the ledger you cannot wipe clean and the board you cannot force to move faster than process allows. You live with the memory of what the wall held and what it pushed away.
