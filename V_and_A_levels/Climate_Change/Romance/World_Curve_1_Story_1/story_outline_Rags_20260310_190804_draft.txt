## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Near-future coastal city and its fringing tidal marshes have been reshaped by steady sea-level rise and harsher storm seasons. The municipal center is a pragmatic mix of reclaimed land, cranes, and new corporate projects promising security; neighborhoods cling to patched boardwalks and rooftop gardens. Power is distributed between civic institutions, community coalitions and private reclamation firms. Culture is practical and place-attached: small fisheries, market stalls, and neighborhood-led restoration crews coexist with lobbyists and engineering proposals. Religion and class do not dominate the plot, but class tension and property stakes are real — long-time residents face displacement as investors propose costly hard infrastructure. Geography alternates between sacred, fragile marsh ribbonlands and the city’s clinical Duval Reclamation Tower. Atmosphere is salt-streaked and work-worn: fog, backlit rain, wind-bent reeds, patched boots, and heartfelt meetings in the town hall. Conflict centers on two responses to climate change: decentralized, community-led living-shore restoration versus top-down reclamation and seawalls driven by profit. Romance threads through the debate, binding personal trust and compromise to policy choices. Visual style is realistic anime-influenced: muted seafoam, rust and slate palette, cinematic fog and salt-splattered light.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Maya Reyes, a field ecologist returned to fight for the tidal marsh that once sustained your family. The story follows a "Rags to Riches" rise: you begin exhausted and beleaguered, face institutional pressure and moral friction, then gather allies, refine adaptive designs and win incremental but meaningful victories. Romance with Elias Stone—an inventive engineer with a conflicted past—develops as you negotiate trust between your community and technical solutions. Camille Duval, champion of a large reclamation tower, offers a seductive promise of security, forcing you to choose between uncompromising grassroots stewardship and pragmatic compromise. Through split decisions you steer protests, legal maneuvers, and engineering trials. Each path culminates in one of four broad outcomes (Happy, Bittersweet, Sad, True) reflecting the costs of change. Regardless of outcome, the arc emphasizes growth: Maya learns to share burdens, translate guilt into collective strategy, and forge a durable ethic that makes the marsh more resilient — and, if fate allows, builds a life with Elias.
