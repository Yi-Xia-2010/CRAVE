## Visual Novel Title
Tide Lines of Maris Bay

---

## World View
Maris Bay is a near-future coastal town (2035–2040) carved into a rocky inlet and a stretched boardwalk, where salt-thick air and wild saltwort fight with neon scaffolding from outside capital. Local culture is maritime and procedural — three generations of fishers, pier-side cooperatives, rooftop gardens and communal storm shelters. Economic pressure from investors and the promise of immediate protection collide with community memory: Old Tomas’ stories, Rosa’s organizing, and Mayor Ana Beltran’s precarious balance of budgets and ballots. TideLine Ventures (Aria Chen’s firm) offers engineered seawalls, automated floodgates and contracts stamped in reflective silicone; the town answers with braided rope, community gardens, and Dr. Lian Obasi’s salt-tolerant marsh restorations. Power is negotiated through town halls and pier-side arguments rather than outright law. Religion is sporadic—folk rituals to the sea and ceremonial offerings at the Old Pier—but pragmatic hope and local reciprocity rule daily life. Geography is intimate: broken boardwalks and reclaimed greenhouses, storms that arrive fast and leave briny traces. Atmosphere is textured — wet reflections, muted coastal palette, neon intrusions — a place where practical love and political choices shape what the coastline will become.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Marin Solé, returning coastal ecologist, who must shepherd a community-led restoration for Maris Bay while navigating a charged romance with Kaito Navarro and the top-down offers from Aria Chen of TideLine Ventures. The narrative follows a Cinderella (Rise → Fall → Rise) emotional arc: an initial build of hope as Marin and the town find momentum, a sudden catastrophic setback—an intense storm and political betrayal—that collapses the temporary victory, and a final, hard-won recovery through moral clarity and collaborative action. The branching structure forces you to choose alliances and tactics: cooperate with TideLine for quick, partial protection; insist on community ownership at the risk of funding; or pursue legal and public exposure of hidden contracts. Each choice shapes your relationship with Kaito (romance, friendship, or estrangement), the fate of fishing access, and whether the shoreline is privatized or kept common. Emotional stakes are civic and intimate: every technical decision loops back to who sits at the pier in the morning and whether Marin can let others share the burden.
