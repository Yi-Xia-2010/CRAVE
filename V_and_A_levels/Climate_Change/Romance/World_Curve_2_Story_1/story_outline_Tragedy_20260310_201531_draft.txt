## Visual Novel Title
Marrow Bay: Tides of Promise

---

## World View
Marrow Bay is a near-future coastal town pinned between a swollen sea and a slipping economy. Temperate marshlands stretch in ribboned reeds; the boardwalk villages cling to weathered pilings and patched tarps. Political power is split between the municipal council, local organizers who run the Saltworks reclamation projects, and an external redevelopment firm led by Cassandra Whitlock. The firm promises engineered seawalls, raised promenades, and private investment, exchanging private property claims and relocation for perceived long-term stability. A culture of reciprocity and oral memory persists in family lines and aunt-and-nephew networks; repair, reuse, and barter keep the town alive. Religion is syncretic—folk rituals around tides coexist with civic meetings and technical briefings. Class stratification is subtle: lifelong residents and fishers hold community capital and memory; developers wield financial capital and bureaucratic leverage; younger activists oscillate between pragmatism and idealism. Geography telescopes between marsh, boardwalk, lighthouse outcrop, municipal glass-walled council chamber, and a patched rooftop greenhouse. Atmosphere: salt-scented, wind-roughened, mournful but stubbornly communal. Conflicts revolve around whether to pursue community-led adaptation—small renewable prototypes, tide gardens and raised platforms—or allow Cass’ large-scale, market-driven redevelopment. Romance weaves into policy: choosing a partner can mean choosing a path.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Mira Santos, a coastal ecologist turned resident activist. Marrow Bay is at a crossroads: Cassandra Whitlock’s redevelopment proposal offers engineered safety at the cost of displacement and private control; Elias Park’s small-scale renewable prototypes offer humane resilience but lack funds and legal teeth. The narrative is a tragic fall: early hope devolves into political compromise, ethical compromise, and painful loss. Across branching choices you must negotiate public hearings, community builds, intimate confessions, and moral exposures. Romance with Elias deepens under salt-slick light—his calm amber eyes and greased utility jacket contrast with Cass’ sharp gray suits and corporate ease. Choices test whether you preserve community memory and autonomy or accept protective stability that erases neighborhood life. Branch paths lead to different endings—moral exposure with personal sacrifice, partial preservation with displacement and grief, outright defeat and exile, or a principled but isolating truth that fractures romance. The emotional curve falls steadily: small hopes and positive energy at the start decay into anxiety, then despair as the town is divided and the tide of politics overwhelms grassroots resilience.
