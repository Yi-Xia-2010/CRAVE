## Visual Novel Title
Saltwood Tides

---

## World View
Saltwood is a near-future coastal town clinging to eroding cliffs and the rhythms of the sea. By the mid-2030s, advanced environmental sensors and augmented monitors are common, but political will and funding lag; private firms promise fast, engineered fixes while communities press for nature-based resilience. Saltwood’s social structure is tight-knit: fishers, restoration volunteers, municipal staff, and elders form the backbone of daily life. The Mayor mediates between grant timelines and re-election pressures; community rituals — the dawn tally at the quay, Old Man Cala’s evening stories, the weekly repair of the community hall — keep identity alive. There is tension between preserving livelihoods and accepting costly, top-down technological solutions. Geography is intimate and precarious: wind-swept cliffs, an exposed tidal marsh with a cracked boardwalk, a warm tide research station on stilts, and a cold corporate construction site that arrives like an intruder at night. Culture is practical and sentimental — patched oilskins and hand-drawn maps beside augmented data streams — and religion is light, mostly rituals of remembrance for past floods. Class friction centers on control over adaptation: corporate capital versus community governance. The atmosphere is salt-stung, tender, urgent; love and responsibility are tested under rising water and the glare of floodlights.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I play Maya Serrin, back in Saltwood to lead the municipal restoration program. The story opens with the town confronting an immediate flooding emergency and an incoming corporate proposal led by Dr. Elena Park for a large engineered seawall. Aiden Koa — my love interest, a third-generation fisher — distrusts high-tech fixes that displace fishers and reshape the coast. The narrative follows an Oedipus (Fall → Rise → Fall) emotional curve: first a fall into crisis and personal failure, then a brief but intense rise as we enact promising community-led measures or hybrid strategies, and finally a fall when compromises, timing, or political pressure lead to irreversible loss. Branching centers on whether I rally the town to fully embrace living-dune, nature-first defenses; accept a pragmatic hybrid with Elena’s firm; or try a politically palatable compromise. Across routes, my relationship with Aiden and the community is tested; Elena is sometimes antagonist, sometimes reluctant ally. Endings vary: community-first survival with romantic reconciliation; corporate seawall success that severs traditions and relationships; a bittersweet partial preservation with heavy losses; or a “true” ending where I step back to scale the model regionally, trading immediate romance for larger stewardship.
