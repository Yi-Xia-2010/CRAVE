## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Near-future Lira Bay sits on the razor between memory and modern urgency: a curved harbor of stilted pastel houses, battered piers, and a tidal marsh that used to be town insurance and cultural story. The political structure is pragmatic and fraught: a municipality led by Mayor Tomas Greer, pressured by regional policy director Evelyn Hart and corporate backers who favor hard infrastructure, while local grassroots organizers (Lina Park) and community elders (Rita Soler) lobby for ecological, low-impact responses. Class lines run through livelihoods — third-generation fishers like Arin Kai depend on the sea; working-class families rent or own the clapboard houses near the marsh; scientists like Maya Soler return from university labs to negotiate between data and neighbors.

Culture blends close-knit maritime rituals with efficient emergency planning. Religion is diffuse local reverence for the bay’s rhythms and seasonal rites kept by elders. The town’s economy centers on fishing, small tourism, and newly proposed redevelopment projects; conflicts emerge between immediate economic security and long-term ecological resilience. Geography is coastal and low-lying: the harbor, The Flats marsh, a municipal seawall construction zone, and a rooftop solar terrace. Atmosphere is salted air and low cloud, cinematic late afternoons, with cinematic painterly visuals highlighting wet surfaces and reeds swaying in wind. Love, stewardship, and policy collide: every intimate choice is shadowed by the next storm.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I, Maya Soler, return to Lira Bay to restore the tidal marsh that once protected our town. The story follows an Icarus (Rise-Fall) emotional arc: a rapid ascent of hope and early success as community energy, science, and a fragile romance with Arin converge; then a sharp, devastating fall when political pressure, economic urgency, and a catastrophic event unravel those gains. The Common Route (Chapters 1–3) builds momentum: personal stakes, community alliances, and a pivotal public decision. From there, three major routes unfold depending on my choice: a community-led hybrid adaptation (Route A), alignment with Evelyn's seawall and redevelopment (Route B), or an investigative route exposing secret deals (Route C). Each route contains a mid-route split that forces further moral trade-offs — preserve ecological integrity at personal cost, compromise for livelihoods, or pursue public truth that destabilizes the town. Each path climaxes in a rapid, emotionally intense collapse (storm, political sabotage, or revealed corruption) that leaves Lira Bay and relationships altered. Endings vary across bittersweet, tragic, or morally complicated outcomes, but the arc always follows the rise to a peak of confidence followed by catastrophic collapse.
