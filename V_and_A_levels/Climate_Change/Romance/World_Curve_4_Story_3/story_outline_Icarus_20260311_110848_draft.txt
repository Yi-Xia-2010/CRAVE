## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Near-future Marabel is a coastal town eroded and remade by rising seas and investment. Once a ring of fishing households and timbered porches, the town now balances hand-built pilings and corporate resilience infrastructure. Political power is fragmented: Mayor Rosa Alvarez mediates between community boards and outside funders; the Beacon—a corporate resilience hub—looms as a promise of safety and a threat to local autonomy. Class divisions are subtle but sharp: long-time fishers, boatbuilders, and gardeners practice low-cost adaptive techniques; consultants and investors bring capital, sensor networks, and conditioned contracts. Culture centers on communal ritual—raised garden markets, boatyard labor, and tidewatch evenings—tied to memory of lost boats and storms. Religion is pragmatic communalism: small altars at docks, memorial buoys, civic meetings held in the boatyard. Geography: reclaimed marshes, the Promenade of rotting boards, the Tidewatch Lab’s concrete calm, the Beacon's glass halo. Conflicts revolve around land use, data control, and identity—do you defend the physical town, or sell access to safety? Atmospherically, Marabel is salt-scented, weather-worn, painterly muted palettes—rust, moss, rain—where romance and policy overlap at the waterline.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I return to Marabel, the town I left to study coastal systems, and I find it at a hinge. The resilience consortium led by Lila Park brings funding and a high-tech Beacon that promises safety—but at a cost. Noah, my old friend and town planner, is trying to broker deals that keep community voice; Mayor Rosa is pressured; Eli and Marta ground the community’s living memory. I (Asha Moreno) propose a community-led marsh restoration paired with tactical defenses. For a time, success seems likely: the Beacon helps install sensors under community oversight, volunteers restore marsh, public trust grows, and Noah and I grow closer. At the peak everything feels possible—plans, funding, and a tender honesty with Noah. Then a compound catastrophe tests alliances: a storm breaches a test wall while a data leak allows private asset claims; Lila doubles down, the Beacon locks down access, and political pressure forces painful choices. Branches explore compromise, resistance, and consequence: one route yields a negotiated hybrid preserving most homes (lessons learned, a fragile but joyful partnership); another trades community land for immediate safety (bittersweet sacrifice); another pursues risky natural restoration with uncertain outcomes (true ending of moral integrity); and another surrenders control to corporate fixes, leaving the town intact but hollow. Across every route, romance is a crucible—love deepens, frays, or reshapes around what we choose to save.
