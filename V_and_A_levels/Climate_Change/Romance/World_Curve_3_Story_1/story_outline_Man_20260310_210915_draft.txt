## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Asterport: a near-future coastal city where salt and circuitry meet. Once a sleepy harbor, the city now endures extended storm seasons and the daily negotiation between handmade resilience and corporate-engineered safety. Neighborhoods stitch reclaimed rooftop gardens and tidal marsh restoration into a fabric of community survival. The municipal apparatus, led by technocratic elites, pushes large-scale flood barriers and land reconfiguration that promise fast security and investor returns—but at the cost of low-lying, historic districts. Culture is mixed: fisherfolk lineages, climate migrants, grassroots ecological collectives, and polished urban planners rub against each other under an atmosphere of humid salt air and frequent orange sunsets split by storm-light. Geography is dominated by the Flats—fragile tidal marshes—crowded quays and a drowned promenade with an aging lighthouse. Class hierarchies are subtle but sharp: those who own high ground and can move, and those who stay because this is the only life they know. Religion and ritual are local—blessings before plantings and memorials at lost blocks. Politics pivot on adaptation strategy: community-led ecology-first restoration vs. engineered displacement. The air smells of brine, diesel, and wood smoke; visually, weathered watercolor and soft anime realism combine with gritty textures and neon accents.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Mara Lin, a marine ecologist and community organizer who must navigate urgent municipal decisions about Asterport’s future while a fragile romance with Ibrahim “Ibe” Rafiq deepens. The city fractures around a proposed megaproject led by Eloise Varela: a gleaming engineered barrier and land regrading scheme that would protect higher ground but displace low-lying neighborhoods. You, guided by Rosa and supported by Kai and others, lead grassroots tidal marsh restorations and rooftop resilience projects. As storms intensify, the stakes become personal—loved neighborhoods, lives, and your relationship with Ibe (a pragmatic engineer who wants durable solutions) are all tested. The arc is Man in a Hole (Fall → Rise): the story falls into crisis as a storm-catalyzed policy leak and a failed early protest produce a devastating surge, and you hit a low point. From there you grapple with moral tradeoffs, expose corrupt deals, negotiate compromises, or, in some routes, accept painful relocations. Romance threads—tender, strained, sometimes broken—reflect who you protect and how you heal. Multiple endings range from triumphant community victory and a lasting partnership with Ibe, through bittersweet compromises, to lonely duty after personal sacrifice or the sterile safety of enforced displacement.
