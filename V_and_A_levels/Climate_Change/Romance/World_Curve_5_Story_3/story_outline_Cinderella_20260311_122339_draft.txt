## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Aster's Reach is a near-future coastal town reworking its life around tides it can no longer ignore. The crescent harbor stitches together patched wooden piers, retrofitted floating platforms, and a scattering of corporate steelwork where investors stake claims. The Tide Lab—once a boathouse—sits beside algae-dark water and is lined with mason jars of samples, whiteboards crowded with handwritten contours of the shoreline, and condensation-beaded windows. Greenline Reclamation Hub demonstrates living shorelines with terraced wetlands and reclaimed driftwood scaffolds, while the Old Breakwater stands as a jagged testimony to where concrete failed. Town politics revolve around survival and identity: Mayor Amara Sol balances votes and safety; Lina Park organizes mobilizations from the Community Meadow rooftop garden; Professor Haruto offers archival truth; Tomas Reyes carries the memory of the sea in his weathered hands. Corporate sea-tech, led by Serena Voss, brings sleek plans and polished holo-tablets—promises of engineered salvation—while community-first advocates push for slower, inclusive adaptation. The culture is salt-bleached, stubbornly resourceful, protective of memory, and uneasy with quick fixes. Class lines blur: fishermen, civic designers, volunteers, and executives all share the same storm predictions, but disagree on what to save and how. Atmosphere: painterly coastal realism—wet light, muted palette with neon-tech accents, and weather that acts like a chorus.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Maya Calder, a coastal scientist returned to Aster's Reach after loss. The story begins with careful, meticulous work at the Tide Lab and a town meeting that presents two irreconcilable paths: a fast, corporate-engineered seawall pushed by Serena Voss that promises quick protection but long-term harm, and community-led living shorelines championed by Elias Rowe and Lina Park—slower, morally anchored, and culturally preserving. The narrative follows a Cinderella emotional arc: the initial rise as community cooperation and scientific clarity create hope; a sudden fall when storms, political maneuvering, and ethical compromises shatter trust; then a final rise when contested truths and acts of collective courage produce enduring resilience. Along the way your relationship with Elias is tested by your default to work over vulnerability, and Serena’s pragmatic ruthlessness challenges your ethics. Branching decisions force you to choose collaboration, negotiation, or exposure; each choice leads to distinct outcomes—happy stabilization, bittersweet preservation with loss, a cautionary collapse, or a principled national reckoning. Visuals emphasize the salt-worn details of characters (Maya’s silver hair strand, Elias’s wooden bracelet, Serena’s platinum bob) and places (wet planks, bioluminescent algae glows), keeping the romance and climate stakes interwoven: love is shaped by shared labor, responsibility, and the cost of compromise.
