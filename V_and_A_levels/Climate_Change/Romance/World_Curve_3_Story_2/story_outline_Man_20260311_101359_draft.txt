## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Elara

---

## World View
Elara is a near-future coastal town clinging to a temperate sea reshaped by climate change. Rising tides and violent seasonal storms have remade daily life: sandbag lines, elevated walkways, shuttered storefronts, and experimental green infrastructure define the streets. Political power is split between local committees steeped in tradition and outside developers armed with capital and glossy plans. The culture is maritime and communal — fishers, shopkeepers, volunteer crews, and field scientists share memory of old floodlines and family loss. Religion and civic ritual thread through seasonal memorials for those taken by sudden floods, and a pragmatic, often gritty religious tolerance governs public life. Class hierarchy is subtle: long-standing fishing families and small-business owners command social respect; developers and corporate planners bring wealth and influence but little local trust. Geography alternates low marsh and jagged breakwater, with a small harbor and a converted boathouse-turned-research greenhouse — Tidewatch Lab. Conflict centers on adaptation vs. profit, memory vs. redevelopment, and whether Elara will choose living, nature-led resilience or an engineered seawall and waterfront redevelopment. The atmosphere is nautical melancholy warmed by the community's stubborn resilience: foggy mornings, salt-stained air, sudden gold after storms, and wet wood textures underfoot. Visuals lean semi-realistic painterly with muted coastal palette, cinematic overcast lighting, and character-focused anime-influenced expressions.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You inhabit Maya Soler in first-person as Elara's environmental engineer-activist. The town faces a decisive council vote: Elias Voss, the polished developer, pushes a hard-engineered seawall and profitable waterfront redevelopment; Maya champions living breakwaters, wetlands restoration, and community-led adaptation. The story follows a Man-in-a-Hole arc: early normalcy slides into crisis as political pressure and personal stakes rise; Maya bottoms out amid betrayal, angry storms, and risk to loved ones; then recovery begins through careful strategy, compromise, or revelation. Romance with Aiden Kuro — a steady fisherman — is entwined with policy: each tactical choice reshapes trust, livelihoods, and intimacy. Branching decisions at the town council, during fieldwork, and at moments of moral reckoning create divergent outcomes: a triumphant hybrid solution that heals both town and relationship; a bittersweet compromise requiring relocations; an exposed corruption leading to systemic change but personal sacrifice; or a rushed engineering victory with heavy costs. Every route adheres to the environmental themes: adaptation vs. profit, tradition vs. innovation, and how love and sacrifice mirror communal choices.
