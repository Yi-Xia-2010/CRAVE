## Visual Novel Title
Tide-Line Promises

## World View
Valora Bay is a near-future coastal metropolis folded into the rhythms of sea and storm. Rising tides and a string of violent weather events have pushed the city into a tense, improvisational adaptation economy: experimental living shorelines and rooftop farms sit beside concrete seawalls and construction rigs. The municipal center hums with policy debates and consortium meetings; the Tideward Promenade still smells of fried fish and salt, patched together by floating platforms and hand-sewn canopies. Class lines follow elevation—wealth and political capital cluster toward high-ground towers and engineered defenses, while lower-lying districts keep cultural networks alive through craft, barter, and community labor. Religion and ritual have shifted toward seasonal observances of repair and remembrance; civic culture valorizes resilience, but also rewards top-down, quick solutions that can displace fragile neighborhoods. The Development Consortium, led in part by established families, pushes massive engineering projects; grassroots makers and ecologists push communal, low-tech adaptation. The city’s atmosphere is humid, salt-scented and storm-darkened at times, but threaded with saturated greens from rooftop farms and soft interior lights. Politics is compromise and calculation; the moral conflict is whether to prioritize immediate, large-scale protection or to preserve community autonomy and cultural continuity. Love, loyalty, and the ethics of sacrifice thread the human-scale stories unfolding against sea-slick concrete and reclaimed marsh.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Reyes), an urban ecologist returned to Valora Bay, try to design community-led adaptation to protect vulnerable neighborhoods. A proposal for a large engineered seawall—pushed by Noah Ortega and the Development Consortium—threatens displacement and cultural erasure. Emil Kwon, my tactile maker and love interest, builds low-tech prototypes and insists we center neighbors’ agency. The arc follows a Man-in-a-Hole pattern: I discover systemic compromises and a looming decision that collapses my sense of control (fall). I grapple with guilt over past failures and the cost of asking for help. Multiple forks force me to choose between political compromise, grassroots resistance, and exposing hidden conflicts of interest. My relationships—especially with Emil—and my tactics shift as I negotiate public meetings, flood-damaged markets, and experimental sites. The recovery (rise) comes through hybrid solutions, revelations, or moral victories. Depending on choices, endings range from blended civic-romantic triumph, to bittersweet local wins with personal sacrifice, to sad displacement and fracturing, to a “true” ending where structural change happens but love evolves into a different, matured companionship. The story is a study of love under crisis: intimacy informs policy, and policy reshapes intimacy.

Notes on Structure & Emotional Curve
- The global arc follows Man-in-a-Hole (Fall → Rise): Chapters 1–3 descend into a crisis (Neutral → VeryNegative; arousal rises from VeryLow to Mid), then each route (A, B, C) begins a recovery sequence (Neutral/Positive → Positive/VeryPositive) with steadily increasing arousal culminating in high-intensity climaxes and denouements (A-VeryHigh at splits and endings).
- Branch points: Chapter 3 (major split into Routes A/B/C), Chapter 5 (Route A split), Chapter 8 (Route B split), Chapter 11 (Route C split) — satisfying at least three distinct split points.
- Route Depth: Every path from Chapter 1 to any ending is no more than 6 chapters (e.g., 1–2–3–4–5–6 or 1–2–3–7–8–9, etc.).
- Character visuals and personalities are used consistently: Maya (deep chestnut hair, silver streak, warm hazel eyes, olive jacket, brass pendant), Emil (faded teal fisherman shirt, sea-glass green eyes, tool satchel), Noah (salt-and-pepper hair, steel-gray eyes, tailored coat, signet ring), Asha, Diego, Mayor Chen and locations are referenced visually throughout each chapter.
- POV: All chapter summaries are first-person as Maya, reflecting her internal conflicts, voice, and emotional stance.
