## Visual Novel Title
Tides We Keep

---

## World View
Puerto Alba is a small coastal town in the 2030s where sea-level rise and increasingly violent storms have become part of daily life. The town is a patchwork of renovated fishing cottages, salt-stained concrete municipal buildings, and a few corporate-funded pilot projects. Politically, the town is governed by a compact council weighed by economic precarity: municipal leadership, embodied by Iris Varela, must balance rescue funding and public accountability. Culturally, Puerto Alba is defined by intergenerational knowledge — Abuela Rosa’s tidal lore and fishing atlas — and by a gritty civic solidarity where volunteer crews keep the harbor operational. Religion is local and syncretic, with ritual storm-watch vigils and blessings before dredging seasons. Class divisions are visible: long-time fishers in patched oilskins and volunteers contrast with light-reflective startup overalls and investor emissaries who come on clean flights and leave before the next storm. Geography: a narrow marketboard on raised boards, a mudflat estuary and the Saltmarsh Labs, a cliffside promenade, a compromised promenade-to-lighthouse route, and a contestatory Green Prototype Site where tech meets marsh. The overall atmosphere is saline and intimate: wind-driven flourishes, the tang of brine, the tactile surfaces of tidal gardens, and a painterly palette of seafoam greens, storm grays, rust, and the occasional warm light during private moments.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Mara Serrano, first-person: a marine-informed community organizer determined to secure Puerto Alba’s future. The story follows an Oedipus arc (Fall → Rise → Fall). Act 1 (Fall): the town endures a damaging storm and council splintering; trust erodes as Iris negotiates a fast investor deal and Elias arrives with a promising modular defense. Act 2 (Rise): through collaboration and risk, you and Elias pilot a hybrid plan that appears to stabilize the shoreline; local marsh restoration, sensor arrays, and modular barriers look like a scalable compromise that preserves livelihoods. Act 3 (Fall): the apparent solution exposes structural faults under stress—either investor haste, engineering blind spots, or political backroom compromises—and a catastrophic storm reveals the limits of short-term fixes. The final outcomes differ by which alliances and values you choose along the way: a community-first hybrid that best preserves Puerto Alba (Happy but hard-won); a pragmatic deal that protects most but sacrifices shoreline and trust (Bittersweet); investor-driven fixes that break during a storm causing severe loss and exile (Sad); or your choice to step away from immediate control and empower slow, traditional stewardship that avoids instant victory but seeds durable resilience (True). Along the route, relationship with Elias intensifies or frays depending on your willingness to reconcile technical optimism with emotional truths; Iris’s guilt-driven toughness and Abuela Rosa’s lore anchor moral choices; Mateo and Sofía ground stakes in everyday lives.
