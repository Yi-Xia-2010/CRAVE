## Visual Novel Title
Tidefall: Letters to the Shore

---

## World View
Marisport is a near-future coastal town where sea-level rise and storm surges have become everyday weather, not distant headlines. Its civic life is informal and local: raised wooden platforms and patched netting define markets; ferry hulks become community greenhouses; rooftop orchards and kelp lines knit households together. Power mixes solar film and hand-wired microgrids; signage is hand-painted; municipal authority is thin but law matters when developers and corporate engineers arrive. Politics is pragmatic and personal—community meetings in Reverend Solomon’s oilskin-coat church, market bargains over the floodplain stalls, and mailed notices from corporations promising fast fixes. Class tension maps onto shoreline elevation: wealthier mixed-use blocks lobby for rapid hard infrastructure; poorer neighborhoods rely on living defenses and mutual aid.

Religion and ritual persist in quiet ways—Sol’s oral histories, memorials for fishermen lost to storms—and law is supplemented by customary stewardship networks. The main conflict is blunt: Evelyn Black and Blackbrand propose a modular engineered barrier to defend property quickly; Mara, Kai, Dr. Leila, and the town push for ecological, community-led adaptation. Culture values resilience, thrift, and memory, but rising tides force moral trade-offs: who is protected first, who relocates, and what is the cost of “security”?

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Linh Alvarez. I return to a town I love to lead living shoreline projects—kelp farms, oyster reefs, porous seawalls—meant to protect homes and preserve ways of life after the sea took my father. A corporate plan arrives: Evelyn Black offers a modular engineered seawall that promises quick protection but risks displacing neighborhoods and hollowing out communal stewardship. Kai, my openhearted collaborator, pushes to scale regenerative solutions. Tensions rise at community meetings; Reverend Sol tries to broker compromise. I am forced into choices: trust Evelyn’s efficiency, double down on grassroots resistance, or pursue a risky hybrid that requires concessions.

Across multiple branching choices, my decisions fracture alliances, challenge my relationship with Kai, and expose deep moral compromises. Small tactical errors accumulate—ignored warnings, misread loyalties, a sabotaged concrete pour—that radiate into tragedies: lost homes, scorched trust, and a town forced to accept engineered infrastructure. The global valence declines steadily: early hope becomes hard choices and, eventually, loss. The romance with Kai bends under practical strain—sometimes survives as a fragile partnership; sometimes it breaks. Endings vary in detail—bittersweet compromises, displacement, or a modest regional policy victory—but the arc remains tragic: failure and lessons linger, and Marisport is altered irrevocably.

I step off the ferry hull and inhale the town—wet wood, compost, the iron edge of sea. My sketchbook swings at my hip, brass compass cool against my sternum. The boardwalk still remembers my boots; gulls argue above planks bleached by decades of salt. Nita waves me over between crates of marsh cabbage and fried fish; she hooks my elbow like she did at twelve and announces Blackbrand’s surveyors are already measuring up at the high bank. I find Kai bent over a kelp-frame, sleeves rolled, forearm schematics inked through the cloth of his overalls. His amber eyes lift into a grin that almost hides the tiredness in their crinkles, and I feel steadier for it. We talk shop—porous berms, oyster lines, a nursery seed order at Saltflower Greenhouse—while I trace a tide-line in the sand, thinking about my father’s boat and the storm that took him. There’s comfort here: raised platforms, salvaged solar film, neighbors who barter time and tools. But the murmurs about an engineered seawall thread the air like a cold wind. I tuck my wave-shaped barrette behind my ear and promise—quietly—to myself and to Kai: we’ll fight for something that keeps the town’s life intact.
