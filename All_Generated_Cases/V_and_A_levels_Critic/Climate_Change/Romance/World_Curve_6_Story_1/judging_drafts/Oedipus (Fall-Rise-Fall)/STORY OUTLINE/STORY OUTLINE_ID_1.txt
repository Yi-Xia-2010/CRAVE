## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Nueva Mar is a near-future coastal city-state built from reclaimed wetlands and cliffside neighborhoods. Rising seas, intensifying storms, and persistent heat redefine daily life: hand-stitched sandbags butt up against the sparkling facades of investor-funded seawalls; rooftop nurseries sit beside corporate solar arrays. Politically, power arcs between municipal institutions intent on preserving civic order and multinational infrastructure firms promising quick security through engineering scale. Culture is a patchwork of old shoreline traditions, DIY adaptation, and polished corporate PR — faith in community rituals and science coexist with pragmatic capitalism. Class divides are visible in the material textures: pastel, weathered Low Row homes with painted floral motifs and muraled roofs vs. cool steel construction sites and glass municipal plazas. Religion and ritual are informal — collective ceremonies around the Old Promenade and communal seed exchanges. The city’s geography emphasizes verticality: terraced neighborhoods at sea level, raised municipal halls, and massive seawalls that interrupt tidal ecologies. Tension hums under the humid light: residents improvise resilience while corporate efficiency and municipal compromise threaten to reshape both wetlands and human bonds. The atmosphere is painterly realism — warm coastal light with eco-cyberpunk accents — where engineering ethics and intimacy collide.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Corvin) return to my neighborhood in Nueva Mar to shepherd a community-led resilience plan against a proposed corporate seawall. The initial descent (Fall) exposes bureaucratic limits, corporate pressure, and personal sacrifices: funding dries up, allies fracture, and a catastrophic storm shreds fragile defenses. From that nadir I push for a temporary win (Rise): hybrid wetlands, retooled policy concessions, and a fragile agreement emerge. But the arc bends again (Fall): the city’s political economy, an engineered disaster, or painful compromises undo much of the victory. Across branching choices—whether I trust Elias’s municipal diplomacy, confront Kai’s corporate determinism, or prioritize local autonomy—romantic stakes mirror policy decisions. Love may bloom with Elias, burn with Kai, or fray under loss; every compromise changes neighborhoods, wetlands, and relationships. The ending landscape ranges from a hopeful integration (Happy) to bittersweet accommodation, to outright loss, to a tempered truth of imperfect vigilance. Each route traces ethical dilemmas between scale and care, and every decision tests whether community, love, or ideology will hold against the tide.

### Chapter 3: The Tide’s Ledger [Neutral] (Branch: Common / Split)

* **Entry Condition:** From Chapter 2
* **Summary:** The first public forum is a humid, taut thing. I stand at the microphone beneath the Municipal Hall atrium lights, palms damp, compass on its cord brushing my trefoil tattoo. Dr. Sima Raza lays out worst-case scenarios: without adaptation, frequency of overtopping events could double within five years. Kai Navarro arrives with a crisp presentation and an undeniable gravity: a seawall proposal that guarantees major arteries and the port — but at the cost of displacing low-lying cultural corridors. Elias fields questions with the patience of someone who stitches policy and people together. Rafi reads a neighbors’ testimony that lands in the silence like a pebble. The mayor shifts in her seat; the council asks for a pilot plan. My throat tightens: moving too fast will erase wetlands, moving too slow risks catastrophic loss. The room feels like a see-saw that I could tilt one way and lose gardens, or tilt the other and doom infrastructure. I breathe and phrase the decision plainly: push for an all-out community-designed hybrid plan or accept a scaled corporate breakwater with negotiated green corridors. The choice folds into my chest like a wound and a work plan.

* **Option C:** "Leak an alternative model and build local alliances first — prioritize slow, local resilience." -> **Consequence:** Sidesteps municipal process, leans into grassroots autonomy; may be legally risky and provoke corporate retaliation. -> **Jumps to:** Chapter 4

Notes on Structure and Emotional Logic:
- The global emotional arc is Oedipus (Fall → Rise → Fall). Each route from Chapter 1 to any Ending is six chapters at most (Chapters counted along the player’s path): 1→2→3→(4 or 10)→(5/7/9 or 11/13/15)→(6/8 or 12/14) — depth 6.
- Splits occur at Chapter 3 (Common), Chapter 4 (Route A hub), and Chapter 10 (Route B hub); additional splits are at Chapter 4 and Chapter 11/13 as internal decisions.
- Valence follows the Fall→Rise→Fall shape by depth: Chapter 1 VeryNegative, 2 Negative, 3 Neutral (end of initial fall), 4/10 Positive (rise), 5/7/11/13/15 VeryPositive (peak), final chapters 6/8/12/14 VeryNegative (final fall).
- Arousal increases steadily: Chapter 1 VeryLow → 2 Low → 3 Mid → 4/10 High → 5/7/11/13/15 VeryHigh → 6/8/12/14 VeryHigh (climax and collapse).
- All narrative is first-person Maya, referencing provided character appearances and location visuals. No new main characters are introduced.
