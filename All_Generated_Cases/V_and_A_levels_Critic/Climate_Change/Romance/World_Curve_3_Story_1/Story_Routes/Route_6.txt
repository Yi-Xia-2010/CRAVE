Months have passed and the work continues. The municipal agreement has been signed into law with the community clause intact. Funding continues through line items and civic partnerships. Luca walks beside you, not always saying much, but present in the steady way of someone who has folded his days around another's life. He still leaves little tide-logged notes in your notebook—reminders to rest, to eat, to watch the sunset.

You feel the truth at your core: saving a place was never only about concrete or mangroves. It was about learning to hold one another through the tide.
You let the tide mark you—not with damage, but with an inheritance: a ledger of people who chose to stay, to repair, to remember. You are tired in the best way; your hands know the language of work and of care.
