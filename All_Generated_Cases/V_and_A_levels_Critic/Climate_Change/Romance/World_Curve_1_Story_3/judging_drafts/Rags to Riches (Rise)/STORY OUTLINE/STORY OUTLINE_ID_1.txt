## Visual Novel Title
Maris Bay: Tides of Repair

---

## World View
Maris Bay sits on the edge of the ocean and memory — a near-future coastal town where fishing piers, moss-lined cottages, and retrofit rooftop nurseries coexist with temporary flood barriers and solar textile awnings. Political life pivots between municipal pragmatists, private investors, and grassroots coordinators; mayoral offices and corporate delegations trade grant spreadsheets in the same glass-walled atrium where community elders tell tidal stories. Economics are precarious: seasonal superstorms, displaced families, and job promises from a proposed harbor redevelopment that would level marshland for engineered stability. Culture is participatory and tactile: seed banks, rooftop nurseries, braided friendship bracelets, and maker collectives that cobble desalination kits out of brass and canvas. Religion and ritual center on place-memory — fishermen’s offerings at the Stormcliff Lighthouse, seed-tagged rooftop dedications — while class tensions bend around who benefits from large capital projects and who bears flood damage. Geography compresses politics and intimacy: boardwalks, saltmarsh labyrinths, and a jagged lighthouse frame personal confrontations. The atmosphere is watercolor-washed and urgent — salt stains, rust, teal streaks in hair — intimate romance entangled with civic repair. Every choice about development mirrors a private choice about love and loyalty; the town’s survival and the protagonist’s heart are braided.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I, Maya Alvarez, return to steward Maris Bay through community-led ecological adaptation. Faced with Irene Vale’s aggressively funded Glass Harbor redevelopment promising jobs but erasing marshland, I must rally neighbors, present scientific evidence with Dr. Sora Kim, and work beside Evan Kaito — a quiet inventor who tests modular habitats and desal kits. The narrative arcs upward: from tension and scarcity to increasingly effective community action and moral clarity. Branching choices ask whether I prioritize coalition-building, public testimony and patient ecological repair, or pragmatic bargains and immediate economic relief; each choice shapes relationships with Evan and Irene, and whether the town keeps its memory and habitat. Multiple decision points create routes that end in different balances of community resilience and personal love: outright communal victory and a solidified romance (Happy), compromises with losses and tender partings (Bittersweet), corporate triumph with displacement and exile (Sad), or a complex, pragmatic pivot where Irene is forced to adapt and a cautious truce leads to structural change and a deepened relationship (True). The emotional arc is a rise: each chapter increases hope, energy, and the scope of repair; arousal escalates toward climactic public confrontations at Council and the Stormcliff lighthouse.
