## Visual Novel Title
Tides Between Us

---

## World View
Solhaven is a small, near-future coastal town (year 2035) stitched between weathered fishing-era buildings and glass-front development projects. Rising tides and frequent storms have remade daily life: low-lying streets flood with tidal memory, and residents measure seasons by how high the water steals the boardwalk. Politically, local councils are battlegrounds between grassroots organizers and private planners; Celeste’s corporate-backed development coalition sits opposite Mara’s community reclamation groups. Class and livelihood cleave along the shore: older fishing families prize traditional catch and immediate income while younger activists and scientists push long-term resilience. Religion and ritual are local and embodied—weekly community salt-blessing rituals at the Saltwren Commons—but there’s no dominant church, rather a civic piety toward place. Geography alternates reclaimed wetlands, a scarred promenade slated for redevelopment, Tidewatch Lab in a converted boathouse, Town Hall where votes are noisy, and the rooftop garden refuge above the old mill where people steal quiet conversations. The atmosphere is melancholic but resilient—hopeful activism lives beside grief—and the culture prizes practical solutions, repair, and stubborn attachment to memory. Conflicts center on whether Solhaven’s future will be engineered and monetized or restored and conserved.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I live in Solhaven and have spent years stitching derelict lots into salt-tolerant gardens and buffer wetlands. A new proposal for the promenade—backed by Celeste’s development arm—promises economic stability but threatens natural buffers. Elias, an engineer who believes practical installations can save the town, returns to collaborate; our histories and values clash and connect. I choose among paths: partner with Elias’s cooperative approach, support Celeste’s large-scale plan, or escalate grassroots resistance. Each route accelerates quickly: early wins (grant funding, pilot projects, political endorsements) lift spirits and my relationship with Elias into a bright peak of shared purpose. But the Icarus arc demands a fall—an unforeseen storm, political maneuvering, or a legal compromise shatters the success, forcing loss, displacement, and moral reckoning. Across branches, my decisions determine who I love, who I trust, and how much of Solhaven survives. Romance is intimate and transactional: compromises in planning mirror compromises in affection. The story ends in different falls—some quietly salvageable, some devastating—showing how love and community bend under climate’s pressure.

- Valence follows the Rise → Fall (Icarus) arc: early chapters rise to optimistic peaks around proposed pilots and deals, then each route concludes with a crash (Negative/VeryNegative) after a catastrophic event or severe social fallout.
- Arousal is steadily increasing from VeryLow at the start (intimate, daily rhythms) through High and VeryHigh at the climaxes/falls—the storms, confrontations, and legal/physical escalations are the story's most intense moments.
- Character visuals and clothing (Mara’s choppy chestnut bob, copper bracelet, olive jacket, muddy boots; Elias’s slate/sea-green shirts, navy jacket, deep blue-gray eyes; Celeste’s platinum bob, pale trench, silver lapel pin) are referenced consistently in each chapter. Secondary characters (Tommy, Dr. Amina, Rae) appear and fulfill their roles as described in the world setting.
- All chapters are written in first-person POV as Mara Lin, emphasizing intimate emotional stakes and the interplay between romance and communal responsibility.
