## Visual Novel Title
Saltline Rising

---

## World View
Saltline is a near-future coastal town pinned between shifting tides and stubborn human memory. Once a small fishing village, it now negotiates with rising seas, storm seasons, and competing ideas of survival: grassroots craft and cultural continuity versus centralized, corporate-engineered defenses. Politically, the town sits inside a contested district where municipal councils, regional agencies, and a private infrastructure consortium jostle for control of funding and implementation. Class lines are blurred—boatbuilders and lab technicians live cheek-by-jowl—but cultural authority belongs to those who tend communal rituals, keep songs alive, and claim the shore as public commons.

Culturally, Saltline combines maritime folk practices with emergent neo-ecological experimentation: floating farms, coral nurseries, wind-harnessing cliff gardens, and communal repair co-ops. Religion is informal—ceremonies around tides, harvests, and storms unify the town. Conflict centers on whether to accept large-scale engineered barriers and automated retrofits (promised by external agencies) or to invest in distributed adaptation rooted in local craft. Geography is intimate and visual: salt-silver beaches, reclaimed marsh patchworks, a craggy cliff studded with wind turbines, and a harbor half-mended with salvaged wood. Atmosphere is bittersweet—wind-swept, practical, and hopeful, where warm lamplit interiors and weathered architecture meet blinking sensors and patchwork green tech.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Marin Alvarez: a Saltline native, engineer by training, and keeper of uneasy promises. The story follows a "Man in a Hole" fall-then-rise curve. The opening eases you into town life and mounting weather threats; then a crisis deepens when a controversial mitigation plan—championed by Elena Moro—moves forward and tensions fracture the community and your relationships. At the bottom of this fall you confront the consequences of a past reinvestment that damaged a marsh you once defended. From that nadir, you climb: choosing between centralized control and community-led adaptation, bridging Kaito's craft-based resilience and Elena's systemic tools, mentoring Maya and listening to Luka and Rin. Your romantic thread with Kaito is entangled with ethics: staying to rebuild together, leaving to scale solutions, or splitting over strategy. Branching choices lead to four core outcomes: a cooperative hybrid success, a centralized-but-cultural-loss outcome, a bittersweet compromise, or a "true" federated governance path that restores agency and a mature bond. Each route forces you to reconcile technical expertise, community ritual, and how to love under climate pressure.
