## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Promise

---

## World View
Takase Cove is a mid-21st-century coastal town held between bracing sea and fragile marsh. Warmer oceans, stronger storms and uneven technologies shape daily life: patched fishing boats and community greenhouses sit beside drone fleets, AR overlays and corporate monitoring towers. Political power is divided between local municipal institutions that prize livelihoods and cultural memory, and coastal developers promising engineered certainty. Religion is not dominant; instead, rituals and local stories about storms and lighthouse keepers persist as cultural anchors. Class lines track access to technology: long-time fishers and volunteer restoration crews work side-by-side with younger, tech-enabled engineers contracted by Atlantech. The chief conflict centers on whether to accept a large, corporate seawall and managed infrastructure (fast protection, centralized power) or to pursue community-led ecological restoration of the tidal marshes (slower, place-based resilience). Geography folds the town, marsh, boathouse-lab, seawall construction site and Atlantech Towers into visual contrasts — warm timber and salt-stained glass against sterile concrete and cool white LEDs. Atmosphere alternates between golden fog-lit mornings full of gull calls and storm-lit nights streaked with neon and emergency orange. The tone is intimate, ethically fraught, and elegiac, where environmental stakes are felt as personal losses and decisions about love, trust and sacrifice.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I, Amaya Saito, a field ecologist rooted in Takase Cove, am pushed into the center of a conflict: Maren Voss's corporate plan for a large seawall threatens the tidal marshes I protect; Lucas Herrera, my partner, leads the engineering effort. The story follows a Cinderella arc: a steady rise as community momentum and professional credibility build (I gather evidence, organize volunteers, and Lucas negotiates technical concessions), culminating in a perceived victory when the town council leans my way. A sudden fall follows when a manipulated impact report and a high-stakes incident reverse public opinion, fracturing trust and my relationship with Lucas. The final rise is a hard-won recovery: through strategic choices — leaking data, forging a hybrid design, litigating, or community organizing — I achieve systemic change in different ways depending on player choices. Across routes the romance with Lucas evolves: reconciled partnership, bittersweet distance, rupture, or slow, restored trust aligned with larger policy shifts. Every branch remains within the emotional arc: rise to a peak, sudden collapse, then recovery toward durable victory, while keeping the conflict between large-scale engineered fixes and community-driven ecology central.
