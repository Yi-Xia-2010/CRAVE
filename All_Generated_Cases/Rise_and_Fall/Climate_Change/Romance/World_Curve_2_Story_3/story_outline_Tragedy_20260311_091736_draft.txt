## Visual Novel Title
Tidefall: Greyhaven's Last Weather

---

## World View
Greyhaven is a near-future coastal town (2035) where tides have become a timetable and storms are public events. The town's economy still smells of fish oil and salt flats, but solar panels and community-built breakwaters sit alongside weathered clapboard and rusting piers. Political life gravitates around Town Hall's polished decisions and public promenades — council meetings are as storm-scarred as the harbor. Religious life is modest: small services that bless nets and boats; a cultural religion of pragmatism and mutual aid has replaced formal dogma for many residents. Class is defined by attachment to place versus mobility: longtime fishers and repair folk whose skin bears salt, and outside capital-backed developers promising jobs and hard defenses. Geography compresses drama — narrow streets, a harbor that gapes at the sea, salt flats that mirror the sky. Conflicts are immediate: who adapts and how, whose livelihoods are sacrificed for engineered resilience, and whether outside investors’ promises will drown the town’s heritage. The atmosphere is tactile and weathered: aqueous light, salt-crusted textures, and neon instruments that blink through rain. The personal and political fuse: every policy is a family decision, every shoreline plan an intimacy.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Isla Morgan) return to Greyhaven as a coastal ecologist determined to keep the town whole. Rising seas and aggressive development pitch residents into impossible wagers — preserve tradition and risk inundation, or accept capital-funded redevelopment that promises jobs but erodes community control. I form a fragile partnership with Luca Moreno, a bright, tactile engineer-activist whose optimism clashes with the cold calculus of councilor Aria Chen, whose slate suits and clipped hair hide an urgency to save the town by any route that secures capital. The story is a spiral of missteps: scientific warnings muted by political compromise, a rushed engineering hub that fails in the first storm, an exposed dataset that is quietly buried, and a final evacuation that shreds trust. Love grows in the force of shared labor, then buckles under blame; I shoulder responsibility, make choices that protect some and betray others, and watch the town fracture. Every branch of the story moves downward: moral mistakes, betrayals, and losses multiply. Endings vary by which compromises I make and whom I trust — all of them land in sorrow: ruin, forced departures, fractured love, or a Pyrrhic "truth" that saves the shore at the cost of my personal life.
