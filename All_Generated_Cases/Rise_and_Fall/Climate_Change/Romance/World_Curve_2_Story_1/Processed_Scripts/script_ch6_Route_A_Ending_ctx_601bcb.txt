Maya Rios [You]: "I made a bet because I thought I could steer the keel as it turned. I thought having a seat would buy us time to keep the small things intact."

Cass Adler [quiet, an attempt at intimacy]: "We can adapt the engineering. We can bring in the biologists to rework the compaction schedules."

Cass swallows. Their eyes, cool blue, flash something unguarded—regret, perhaps, or the dawning measurement of a cost their models didn't quantify: the living texture of a town's daily life.
