Dahlia Kestrel [after a beat]: "A legally binding oversight board. Community co-leads. You understand the timelines we're working on—storms don't wait for consensus."

Mayor Lyle Durant [clearing his throat]: "This council needs assurances—jobs, safety, accountability. If this framework delivers both, then I see public support."

Rosa [stepping forward, clipboard in hand]: "And we'll ensure matching labor grants. The town will mobilize—our people want to work, not be sidelined."

Dahlia exhales, a blade of air that somehow softens her features. The edge in her eyes minutes ago seems to blur into something almost collaborative.

Dahlia Kestrel: "Fine. We will pilot a co-management model on the marsh. AquaWorks will provide funding and technical resources. We'll enter a contract that includes the oversight board you asked for. But understand this—benchmarks will be strict. We need measurable recruitment rates, sensor data, clear timelines."

You feel the rush of leverage — the thing you've wanted to turn into reality. Money does not erase the work, but it buys tools and time and, crucially, dignity for local labor.

Maya Reyes [softening]: "We can work with measurable outcomes. We'll pair sensor data with community monitoring. Our definitions of success will include social resilience as well as ecological metrics."

Dahlia Kestrel [a small, sharp nod]: "Then we have an agreement—on the record."

[Sound: Applause rises, tentative then warmer. The crowd leans in; the music lifts a single optimistic phrase.]

Maya Reyes [grinning despite yourself]: "Let it be the first of a thousand."

Elias Kwan [half-smile]: "Don't tempt me. I'll plant a thousand."

Mira [checking a tablet]: "Sensors are syncing. Tide forecast looks stable for the next week—good window for root establishment."

Mira [nodding]: "We can run adaptive cycles. Data comes in, community teams interpret, and we adjust. That's why the co-management agreement matters."

Hands in mud, you explain how to angle plugs against the current, where to press soil to encourage rhizome spread. A child watches and copies your motions with the reverence of a pupil. The textures—cool silt, the slickness of blade grass, the sting of salt on sunburned cheeks—are immediate and intimate.
