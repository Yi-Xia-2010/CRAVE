## Visual Novel Title
Highwater Compass

---

## World View
A near-future coastal town, Highwater Cove, sits partially reclaimed by tides. Streets have become tidal canals; patched stilts and battered vernacular houses rub shoulders with retrofitted modular labs and rooftop farms. The political center presses for rapid, centralized solutions to protect tax bases and infrastructure; developers and city officials frame decisive, top-down projects as salvation. At the same time, a tight-knit maritime culture—fishers, elders, cooperatives—resists displacement and values place-based knowledge. Religion and ritual are local and maritime: offerings to the sea, memorials at the high-tide line. Class tensions are acute: long-time residents with salt-darkened hands struggle against investor promises that favor new luxury pilings. Geography is tactile—salt gloss, reedbeds, fog, and abrupt sun. The atmosphere is melancholic but practical: people convert markets into community farms, fishers retrofit boats with solar and low-tech innovations, and labs crowd into repurposed warehouses. The conflict is ethical as much as physical: preservation of heritage and livelihoods versus rapid infrastructure, extraction, and growth. Romance is woven into this ethical landscape: intimate commitments are tested by policies, storms, and choices about who or what to save.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Armitage, a pragmatic idealist leading a grassroots resilience initiative. A major storm and investor pressure force the town to choose between Evelyn Hart’s large-scale, top-down coastal defense plan and locally-led adaptive measures championed by me, Jonah Reyes, and our neighbors. The story begins with a fall: trust erodes, a scandal or critical failure deepens the crisis, and the town faces displacement. From that nadir, the middle acts climb into a fragile rise where community experiments, rooftop farms, and hybrid technologies seem to stabilize the cove—intimacy with Jonah grows. But every interim success exacts costs: deals are struck, compromises made, and political betrayals reveal deeper structural forces. The final act collapses again: the provisional gains unravel under new storms, funding manipulation, or the moral cost of compromises, forcing a last reckoning. Across branching choices, my relationship with Jonah and my ethical choices shift outcomes among Happy, Bittersweet, Sad, and True endings—each conforming to the Fall→Rise→Fall (Oedipus) arc: an initial downfall, a hopeful interim recovery, and an ultimate collapse or moral reckoning.
