Elder Tomas approaches with his walking stick clicking on the raised stone, and when he sets down his bundle of dune grass, hands that have planted a hundred shorelines tremble just enough with age.

Elder Tomas [softly]: "We plant to remember that land is not only where our feet rest, but where stories take root. Plant with a story in your pocket."

Aria Marin pulls a seed packet from your satchel—the same one that sat pinned on a corkboard months ago—and press it into the soil like a small, private promise.
