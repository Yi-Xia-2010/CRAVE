## Visual Novel Title
Tidal Names

---

## World View
Lowry Bay sits where the sea steadily reclaims memory: a narrow coastal town ringed by boardwalks, raised gardens, and the occasional concrete promise of permanence. Politics here blur with weather forecasts — mayoral offices peddle jobs and contracts while community brigades plant marsh grasses and stitch up ruptured greenhouses. Class lines follow elevation: families on the higher ridge petition for engineered walls; older fishermen and gardeners cling to stilts, nets, and rooted fruit trees. Religion and ritual are practical and seasonal — tidewatcher calendars and harvest offerings to calm storms — but civic faith is split between the technocratic assurance of large-scale infrastructure and the slow science of living shorelines.

Lowry Bay's culture values craft, story, and mutual labor: muralists repaint shuttered storefronts, elders teach tidal lore, teenagers digitize sensor data. There is no omnipotent tech — portable sensors, patched drones, and desal units exist alongside reclaimed greenhouses and community-built berms. Geography is intimate: a drowned orchard, a scarred lighthouse, and a narrow harbor where the water comes closer each year. Atmospherically the town breathes salt and pollen, fog and rust-amber sunset; cinematic storms make decisions urgent. At its heart is a question: do we buy safety with steel and contracts that displace our neighbors, or do we redesign our life with the sea and accept hard trade-offs? Love forms and frays inside that question.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mira Kestrel: coastal ecologist, community builder, and reluctant leader. The story follows one arc of rising hope, a brutal fall, and a final, bittersweet resurgence — the Cinderella curve: we build momentum, taste success, lose ground in a sudden crisis, and claw back toward a more resilient future. In a public moment, I must choose how the town will respond to a consortium-led barrier proposed by Jonah Hale and defended as decisive engineering. My choices branch into pragmatic negotiation, grassroots resistance, or political brokerages. Each path deepens my relationship with Cassian Rhys — whose sandy hair and paint-splattered jacket are as familiar as the boardwalk planks — and tests loyalties with my sister Nadia, Ruben the fisherman, Asha, and Mayor Lin Park.

Across all branches, scenes are shaped by weathered visuals: my dark sea-green hair tucked behind my ear, the brass tidewatcher pendant that keeps time of high water, Cassian’s sun-bleached curls, and Jonah’s polished raincoat. Outcomes vary — a hybrid victory that preserves most homes; a compromise that costs neighborhoods and comforts; a hard infra-led future that secures property at cultural cost; or a lonely moral victory where I choose community safety over personal security. Every route obeys the Rise–Fall–Rise curve: we surge, we crash, we rise again — changed, not whole.
