You walk the harbor one last time before dawn, hands in pockets, listening to the sea that has always been both danger and memory. You let the pendant cool against your throat. You have loved and lost not from a storm but through the currents you set into motion. You fold the notebook closed and slide it into your jacket. You do not leave the town; you cannot — Hearthbend is stitched through your bones. But things have changed irrevocably. You will keep recording. You will teach in smaller circles, you will help the neighbors you can, and you will watch the market do what markets do. The hope you built survives in altered form and so do you, not triumphant but determined.
