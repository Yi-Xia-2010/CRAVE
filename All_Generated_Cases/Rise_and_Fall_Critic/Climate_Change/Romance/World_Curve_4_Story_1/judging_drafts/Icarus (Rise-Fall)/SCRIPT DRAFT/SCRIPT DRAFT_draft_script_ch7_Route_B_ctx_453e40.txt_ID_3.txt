Elias appears at a workbench when the sun is low, grime on his palms and a smear of oil darkening his slate-blue undershirt. For a moment he stands apart, watching how the market has become a living machine of care. His mouth is a line; his eyes search, not for a schematic but for your face.

You register complexity in him—he still carries the architecture of scale in his bones, the habit of patterning systems for the many. But now his hands mirror yours: grease, measured touch, a willingness to learn the grain of timber rather than only the flow of current.

You feel something climb inside your chest: not triumph but a steady ascent—fear unbraiding into agency.
