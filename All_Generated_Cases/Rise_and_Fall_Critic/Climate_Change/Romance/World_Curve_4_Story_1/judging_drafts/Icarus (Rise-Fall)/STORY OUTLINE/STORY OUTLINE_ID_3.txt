## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Hearts

---

## World View
Marenport is a near-future coastal archipelago city reworked by water and will. Sea-level rise has stitched neighborhoods into canals, rooftops into terraces and skyfarms; patched floating platforms and reclaimed promenades coexist with retrofitted labs and corporate towers. Politics is a contested braid of grassroots collectives and powerful climate-tech conglomerates; public governance teeters between local councils and centralized emergency authorities. Religion is sparse formalism; rituals center on place-memory — wakes for lost docks, offerings at flooded shrines — and the city’s social fabric runs on mutual aid networks and trade in salvaged goods and seedbanks. Class divides align with access to technology: modular TideGrid units and sensorized housing are luxuries controlled by corporations, while community carpenters, growers, and Resilience Collectives repurpose salvaged materials and knowledge. Geography alternates sun-bleached terraces and kelp-dark canals; storms carve dramatic outlines into daily life. Culture favors pragmatic collectivism, oral place-memory, and the tactile: mossy railings, rusted signage, and salt-crusted hems. The central conflict is moral and intimate: a large-scale engineered system called the TideGrid promises city-wide stabilization at the cost of centralized control and cultural erasure, while community-led resilience preserves autonomy but risks larger short-term losses. Love and loyalty are pressured by policy, technology, and weather.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I experience Marenport as Maia Soler — scientist-turned-community-leader — pulled between intimacy and ideology. The narrative is an Icarus arc: rapid ascent into hopeful, influential solutions, then a sharp, catastrophic fall. The Common Route introduces the stakes — Elias Kade’s TideGrid, Rin Voss’s urgency, and the neighborhood’s fragile autonomy. At a crucial split, I choose how to engage: negotiate with Rin (Route A), back Elias’s pilot (Route B), or double down on grassroots resilience (Route C). Each route delivers a brief, intoxicating rise: political traction, technical breakthroughs, and emotional reconnection with Elias. But each ascent ends in a crash — a technical cascade, moral compromise, or devastating storm — fracturing trust and reshaping the city. Branches differ in the flavor of the peak (policy success, technological achievement, or mass mobilization) and in the type of fall (authoritarian stabilization, infrastructural cascade, or large-scale loss). Every scene keeps intimacy at the center: how love interprets sacrifice, whether reconciliation survives betrayal, and how shared responsibility can both save and shatter what we are. The endings vary from a reconciled but haunted partnership to separation, mass loss, or a somber truth that reshapes how Marenport rebuilds.
