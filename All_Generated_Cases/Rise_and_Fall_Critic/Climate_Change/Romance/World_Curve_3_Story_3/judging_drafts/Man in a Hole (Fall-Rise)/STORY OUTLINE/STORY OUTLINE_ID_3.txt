## Visual Novel Title
Saltbridge Tides

---

## World View
Saltbridge is a near-future coastal town (circa 2035) stretched along a narrowing bay where tides have grown higher and the winter storms more fickle. Once a modest fishing community, the town is now visually divided: low-lying historic neighborhoods with salt-bleached clapboard and warped boardwalks sit beside gleaming adaptation projects run by private contractors. Local politics are a careful calculus between desperate homeowners, community organizers demanding justice, and corporate-funded engineering firms promising certainty. Class lines follow elevation: those who can afford raised foundations and modular floodproofing live in retrofitted blocks or new developments; long-time residents try to keep homes and livelihoods intact with sandbags and mutual-aid networks. Religion and ritual persist in small rites — dawn blessings on the boardwalks, candle vigils after storms — yet policy inertia and funding bottlenecks make large-scale adaptation painfully slow.

Geography: a jagged coastline, dune belts interrupted by emergency barricades, and a reclaimed rooftop sanctuary atop a converted mall where bees and communities thrive. The culture is one of hands-on repair, rumor, and neighborhood meetings; people trade tools and recipes as readily as protest flyers. The antagonist is both the sea and the political-economic forces that convert public risk into private profit. Visual style: soft-anime character art with watercolor, a muted palette of seafoam greens, slate blues, rust-orange rust, and frequent salt crust textures. Every corner of Saltbridge reflects a town in the process of remaking itself — hopeful and vulnerable at once.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Maya Serrano) am a community resilience coordinator and the first-person narrator. The story is a Man-in-a-Hole arc: I watch the situation deteriorate (fall), hit the bottom — a moment of near-loss that challenges my ethics and relationships — then claw back, rebuilding with new understanding (rise). My romantic choices become lenses for different responses to the climate crisis: Elias channels grassroots direct action and collective sacrificial risk; Noah offers negotiated, technical compromises that preserve livelihoods while asking concessions; and a community-first hybrid route forces me to balance both.

The narrative begins with creeping crises: funding pulled, Mayor Lillian hedging, a corporate firm pushing a top-down solution, and my brother Finn’s livelihood at stake. A town meeting crystallizes my choices. Each primary branch (romantic route) explores a different strategy: grassroots mobilization (Elias), pragmatic collaboration with institutional engineering (Noah), and a hybrid community-policy coalition (Rosa/neutral route—still built around existing characters). Each route drops me deeper before an inflection point: a storm, an eviction push, or a failed negotiation. The rise begins when I accept help, delegate, and reconcile personal love with public purpose. Endings vary: community triumph, bittersweet compromise, corporate loss, or catalytic sacrifice — all grounded in first-person emotional detail and Saltbridge's tactile settings.
