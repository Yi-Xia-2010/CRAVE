Your chest tightens again, but differently: not guilt now, but a grown kind of pride. You remember nights where the only thing you could hold was a printout and a stubborn refusal to quit. You think of Hal's hands, of Miriam shouting plans into a megaphone in the rain, of Rosa ferrying pies to keep people warm and fed.
