## Visual Novel Title
Seabright Rising

---

## World View
Seabright is a mid-sized coastal town in 2035 where the sea is a constant actor in daily life: rising tides lap at foundations, storms leave salt on every window, and adaptation has become civic ritual. Politically, the town sits at the crossroads of grassroots councils, a pragmatic municipal government, and private development interests pushing rapid modernization. Culture blends fisherfolk traditions with a new civic ecology movement; religion and ritual appear in storm memorials and blessing ceremonies for boats. Class lines show in patchworked homes on stilts, newly engineered storefronts, and luxury pilot projects. Conflict centers on whether to preserve the town’s character through community-led restoration (mangroves, microgrids) or to permit large-scale redevelopment that promises infrastructure at the cost of displacement. Geography is compressed: boardwalks and old harbors lead to muddy mangrove flats and the renovated Community Climate Center called the Beacon. Atmosphere is tactile — salt spray, weathered wood, and the hum of sensor arrays — and decisions feel intimate because every policy choice redistributes risk, memory, and livelihood.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Amara Vale, a local scientist and advocate trying to shepherd Seabright from incremental survival to resilient flourishing. The narrative is a Rags-to-Riches arc: I begin with small, community-driven ambitions and face escalating political, financial, and romantic pressures. Across branching choices, my relationship with Eli Navarro — the engineer I am drawn to — and my rivalry/negotiation with Marco Voss — the charismatic developer — shape different pathways to success. Each branch asks: can we secure Seabright with dignity and ingenuity without selling our soul? The stakes rise steadily: small projects become townwide pilots; grudging compromises become robust hybrid governance. Every major choice yields a different kind of success — community-led triumph, negotiated public-private stewardship, or hybrid transformations that keep the heart of Seabright. Romance grows alongside civic accomplishment; intimate decisions about trust and collaboration produce varied but upward outcomes: protected neighborhoods, thriving mangroves, and new systems that make Seabright more livable. Even bittersweet results emphasize resilience and hope. The endings celebrate gains in safety, social equity, and connection — different flavors of hard-won rise.

Notes on Structure and Arc:
- Every chapter is narrated in first-person by Amara Vale and references the provided character designs and locations (hair, clothing, accessories, Beacon, boardwalk, mangrove site, Town Hall, etc.). No new main characters were introduced.
- The narrative strictly follows the Rags-to-Riches [Rise] arc: each chapter moves upward in civic, ecological, or personal terms. Choices lead to different flavors of success rather than success vs. failure.
- Branching occurs at Chapter 3 (major split) and at Chapters 4, 7, and 10 (secondary splits), fulfilling the requirement of at least three distinct split points. Additional alternative branches lead to chapters 13–15 to expand horizontal branches while keeping depth (Chapter 1→ending chapter) to a maximum of six chapters.
- Every chapter except Chapter 1 begins with an explicit Entry Condition linking it to its parent chapter and option.
- All routes end within the six-chapter depth limit (Common 1–3 plus up to three route chapters).
- Endings favor Community Triumph, Hybrid Governance, and Scaled Technical Success — progressive outcomes aligning with the Rise curve and the game's themes of climate change, romance, and community resilience.
