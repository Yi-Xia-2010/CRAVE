## Visual Novel Title
Tideglass Promise

---

## World View
Havenpoint is a near-future coastal town (2035–2040) hemmed by rising tides and human stubbornness. Once a fishing and shipbuilding hub, the town now wears patchwork defenses: living shorelines, solar terraces, community gardens, and a controversial new floodwall. The political landscape is a triad of local councils, developer-investor consortia, and grassroots cooperatives. Religion and ritual live in seaside memory—Elder Mae’s seasonal blessings, harborside funerary songs—while civic life is dominated by urgent debates over seawalls, managed retreat, and funding tradeoffs. Class divides track proximity to the shore: multi-generational fishers and smallholders fight to keep moorings; newer investor-backed developments promise visible, quick fixes. Culture is tactile and artisanal—tarps, patched jackets, handwritten notices—against the clinical glint of outside expertise, embodied by Lydia Voss’s glass-and-steel pavilion. Geography compresses politics: boardwalk markets, Old Shipyard ruins, Greenridge terraces, and a floodwall painted with murals. Atmosphere oscillates between hopeful tinkering and brittle tension: community gardens and prototype marsh-forms exist beside rusting cranes and press briefings. Climate change is not an abstraction here; it is weather, erosion, and the ledger that determines who stays. Intimacy and policy blur—love becomes leverage and solace; every plan touches someone’s dock.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
Tideglass Promise is a first-person romance set in a coastal town negotiating salvation and loss. You are Marin Solace, an engineer-turned-cooperator who leads a grassroots adaptation effort. The story follows an Icarus curve: a rapid ascent as community-led prototypes gain influence and personal relationships deepen, reaching a crest where Marin feels triumphant and in control; then a catastrophic reversal—political backlash, failed infrastructure, or personal sacrifice—crashes into that success. The plot branches at crucial civic and personal choices: whether to publicly ally with Lydia’s resources, to prioritize Jonah’s docks over town consensus, or to accept a pragmatic compromise that saves structures but costs traditions. Each decision steers you toward one of four full routes (A–D), each with its own tonal outcome (Happy / Bittersweet / Sad / True), but all follow the same Rise → Fall curvature. Encounters with Jonah, Lydia, Asha, Raff, Elder Mae, and Councilor Ben inflect both policy and feeling; visual motifs (salt-streaked boots, platinum bob, waxed canvas vest, solar terraces) underwrite emotional beats. Romance is both balm and liability: love amplifies the ascent and complicates the fall. Across short, wide branches you experience community meetings, urgent site work, convivial mornings on the boardwalk, and the bitter aftermath of collapse, told from Marin’s intimate, pragmatic, and heart-heavy first-person voice.
