Asha is already there, sleeves rolled, a stain of peat on her thumb. Her eyes narrow in that quick, practical way she has when she reads a figure out loud.

Asha: "If we bring the terrace edge closer by eight meters here, the attenuation model predicts a ten to twelve percent reduction in near-shore velocities. That's not nothing. But we have to show it—visually and numerically."

Marin Solace: (You tap the chart with a damp fingertip.) "Right. Show the reduction, and then show what that means for the docks—less scour against the pilings. People get something concrete they can picture, not just a line on a graph."

Asha: (She smiles, tired and fierce.) "We make them see the shoal as infrastructure too. Living systems that earn their keep."

You both fall into the rhythm of preparing talking points—phrases clipped and tested until they stand up to scrutiny. You say the technical parts out loud; she recasts them into plain language. You test the handout's first line, then the second; you slide the clipboard toward the lamp to see how the font looks when reflected on wet glass.

Marin Solace: (You feel a steadying confidence—blessing and ballast both—when he speaks for the community you both love.) "If you say it plain, the room listens. Bring specifics—how many moorings, projected maintenance. Numbers land."
