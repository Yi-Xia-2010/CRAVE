[Scene: Experimental Terrace — Night / Storm Approaching]
[Visual: Heavy clouds roll low; the terrace channels darken. Streetlights flicker like hesitant beacons.]
[Music: Low, escalating percussion; distant thunder]
[Sound: Wind increasing, the slap of rain beginning to smear the night]

The models were conservative. The regional forecasts were grim but within the margins you planned for. The storm that arrives is something else—an animal of weather that folds its claws into predictions and sharpens them. You watch the radar on a borrowed phone as G-force lines bend in ways your charts did not expect.

There was one inlet—a narrow, neglected artery at the edge of town—that you'd tabled for an in-depth study. The engineering estimate was low-priority in the cadence of pilots. A contractor's work from before your binding clauses—an easement approved in an earlier permit—held a culvert that everyone assumed would carry. It doesn't.

Sound footage arrives before you do: Raff's phone, breathless, the livestream jittering, water pulsing like a living thing at the terrace's base. The new marsh, the seedlings you've coaxed from mud—pulsed and ripped, not with a slow fade but like someone unpicking cloth. You've seen erosion on a whiteboard; live footage is a different grief. Raff: (on the livestream, voice breaking) "—it's just—look, Marin, it's—"
You (internal): Your name in his mouth is a bell. You have a hundred responses rehearsed in the dark. None of them make the water stand down.

Asha: "They'll try to make you into the story's villain or its martyr. Neither helps the plants."

Jonah: "Whatever happens, you don't have to carry it alone. We can ask for an independent review that includes fishermen, not just engineers. We can push for immediate emergency maintenance on that inlet."
You (internal): Each option is a different kind of loss. Holding to principle may fracture the cooperative's funding beyond repair. Compromising may secure immediate protection but at a cost to community control.

Investors whisper about leadership change as if governance were easily swapped like a gear. Elder Mae gave you a look yesterday that felt like a weighing.

Elder Mae: (soft) "You must not mistake sacrifice for failure. There are different kinds of courage."

Either path is loss and preservation braided together.

You close your fingers around the pin until it leaves a crescent of warmth.
