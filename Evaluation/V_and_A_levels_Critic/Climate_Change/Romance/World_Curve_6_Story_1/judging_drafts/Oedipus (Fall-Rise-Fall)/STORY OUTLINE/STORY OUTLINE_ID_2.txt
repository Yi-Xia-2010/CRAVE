## Visual Novel Title
Tidelines of Nueva Mar

---

## World View
Nueva Mar is a near-future coastal city-state built on reclaimed wetlands and cliffside terraces. The city is governed by a pragmatic municipal apparatus that must balance tourism, investment, and an increasingly frantic need for climate adaptation. Wealth and influence concentrate around corporate infrastructure firms that promise definitive engineering solutions — grand seawalls, channelized outlets, and vertical developments on elevated plateaus. Opposing them is a dense fabric of neighborhood autonomy: cooperatives that run rooftop nurseries, patchwork flood diversions, and informal governance networks. Religion and ritual persist in small shore-side shrines honoring tides; culture prizes community festivals aired across solar-charged networks. Geography is stark — the Low Row’s terraced low-lying homes, the Old Promenade half-submerged and melancholic, Municipal Hall perched on a raised plaza — and politics move between technocratic expediency and grassroots resistance. The atmosphere is humid and painterly, a layered aesthetic of warm pastels in community zones and cool monochrome in corporate sites. Daily life is adaptation: children painting murals on sandbags, municipal drones mapping storm surges, and the ever-present prospect of relocation. The conflict is ethical as much as physical — how much must be sacrificed to survive, and who decides?

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I narrate as Maya Corvin, a coastal systems engineer turned community organizer striving to protect my childhood neighborhood from an imposing corporate seawall. The story follows an Oedipus arc (Fall → Rise → Fall): we first fall into crisis as corporate timelines accelerate and a storm damages crucial defenses; we then achieve a fragile rise when a community-led hybrid plan gains traction and temporarily averts disaster; finally we fall again as the larger political and economic forces — and unavoidable climatic limits — force choices that cause cultural loss and personal rupture. Romance threads are central: Elias, a municipal planner, offers cautious policy routes and a possible shared future; Kai, a corporate lead, offers decisive engineering and a complicated intimacy that tests ideological lines. Secondary characters — Lio, Rafi, Dr. Sima, Mayor Velez — anchor stakes. The player’s choices determine whether the outcome is Happy (a negotiated hybrid succeeds), Bittersweet (partial preservation, personal distance), Sad (corporate solution overrides locals), or True (an uneasy compromise and ongoing vigilance). The narrative emphasizes sensory coastal details, the ethics of engineering, and the cost of compromise on love and community.
