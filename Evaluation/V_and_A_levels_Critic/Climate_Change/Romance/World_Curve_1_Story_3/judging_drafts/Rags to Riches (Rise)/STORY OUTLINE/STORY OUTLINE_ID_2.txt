## Visual Novel Title
Maris Bay: Tides of Salt and Seed

---

## World View
Maris Bay is a near-future coastal town caught between memory and migration. Once-sleepy docks and wooden cottages now shoulder modular flood barriers, rooftop nurseries, and community-built reed beds. Politically, a small municipal government struggles to balance private investment and grassroots stewardship: Mayor Lionel Park is an earnest, exhausted swing-figure between Irene Vale’s high-profile redevelopment lobby and community organizers led by locals like Maya Alvarez. Class and culture reflect a town of working fishers, small-makers, and eco-activists, layered with newcomers seeking refuge from climate-pressed cities. Religion and ritual are informal — sea-blessings, Captain Reyes’s storytelling, and neighborhood seed exchanges stand in for formal congregations.

Geography focuses on the boardwalk, saltmarsh labyrinth, Verdant Rooftop Nursery, Council House Atrium, Glass Harbor development site, and Stormcliff Lighthouse. The atmosphere is salt-stained, moss-mottled, and intimate: watercolor skies, weathered textures, and pragmatic tech like solar textiles and desal units. Conflicts center on adaptation vs. expedience: Irene’s engineered harbor promises jobs and immediate flood control, while Maya and allies press for nature-based, community-led resilience. Romance threads into civic choices: trusting one person mirrors trusting a community to slow the tide. The overall feeling is urgent but tender — storms on the horizon, seeds in pockets, choices that will remake or remember Maris Bay.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Alvarez, a community organizer and practitioner of hands-on ecological design. As sea levels and superstorms press at Maris Bay, I must marshal neighbors, science, and craft to protect both people’s homes and the town’s identity. Early scenes show damaged storefronts, rooftop nurseries, and the political pressure led by Irene Vale’s glossy harbor redevelopment. At the Council House Atrium, a public vote looms. My choices — whether to negotiate, protest, prototype alternate solutions with Evan Kaito, or accept compromises — shape the town’s fate and my heart.

The story branches from a common starting point into three strategic directions: collaborative engineering and civic compromise (Route A), confrontational activism and personal sacrifice (Route B), or quiet coalition-building and hybrid science (Route C). Each route contains further moral forks where I choose trust over expedience or insist on slower, deeper repair. Across every route the emotional arc is Rise: setbacks become leverage; alliances build capacity; love and community strengthen resilience. Endings range from a community-led success and a deepened romance (Happy), to bittersweet protection with sacrifice (Bittersweet), to a harsher displacement (Sad), and a complex institutional pivot where Irene adapts and we form uneasy but effective partnerships (True). The narrative is first-person: tactile descriptions, weathered clothing, and close observation of faces and small gestures drive both civic stakes and intimacy with Evan.
