You hear it as if she has placed that sentence in your hands personally. The word "non-negotiable" tastes like the salt on your tongue and like the iron certainty of a nailed seam.

Silence folds between you, not empty but full of all the small, private reckonings. You think of guilt like a weight you've carried for others; you think of how it has kept you from asking for things you need. The lighthouse wind scrapes clean the edges of those quiet debts.
