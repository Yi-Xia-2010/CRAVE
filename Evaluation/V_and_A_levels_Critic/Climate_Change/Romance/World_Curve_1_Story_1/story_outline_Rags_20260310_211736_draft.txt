## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promise

---

## World View
A mid-sized coastal metropolis ten years hence, reshaped by rising seas and repeated storm surges. Concrete promenades and glass towers are mottled with salt; neighborhoods have either been reinforced with adaptive gardens and pilings or sold to private developers who promise quick, expensive fixes. Political power sits with an uneasy hybrid: City Hall wields emergency powers and private capital, while informal coalitions—kayak couriers, retrofit collectives, and vertical-garden microstudios—patch what policy misses. Religion and class intermingle in pragmatic rituals: flood prayers at stairwells, barter networks for salvaged materials. Geography is a jagged coastline of reclaimed boardwalks, dune reserves, and low terraces on stilts; weathered textures—reclaimed wood, living moss, and rusted metal—permeate the city. The culture values do-it-yourself resilience, community-led adaptation, and a wary optimism; science (represented by climatologists like Dr. Noor) competes with polished political narratives (Irene’s redevelopment) and entrepreneurial salvage (Luca’s firm). The atmosphere is salted and cinematic: soft wet reflections, low-saturation palettes, and pockets of warm green life pushing through the city’s wounds. Everyday life is brittle hope—people building, conserving, arguing, and falling in love when there’s water in the alley and no other place to meet.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Rios: a hydrology technician turned grassroots organizer. The season’s first council hearing forces a choice that will define my neighborhood’s future. I must decide whom to trust: Elliot Chen, whose living seawall prototypes could save homes but require community buy-in; Sami and Dr. Noor, who push for public pressure and urgent radical action; or Irene Voss, the council leader whose private redevelopment offers fast funding but heavy social cost. Each path tests my fear of reliance and my grief over a brother I lost to a storm. Romance with Elliot grows through shared technical labor, intimate late-night problem-solving, and the ethical friction of compromise. Across branching routes, I balance love, responsibility, and systemic leverage: some choices yield a negotiated community-led pilot and a personal partnership; others demand painful trade-offs or moral improvisation leading to complex, quieter victories. The arc is Rise—progress forged by collaboration, hard lessons, and incremental triumphs—culminating in durable infrastructure and a steadier, though imperfect, future for the Harborfront.
