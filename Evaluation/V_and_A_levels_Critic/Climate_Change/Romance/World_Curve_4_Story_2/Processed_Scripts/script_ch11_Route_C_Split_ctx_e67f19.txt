[Background:"ch11_e67f19_1"]
[Sound: Low hum of the prototype, water lapping against the hull, occasional laughter]
[Music: Quick, bright strings — urgent and buoyant]

You still feel the breaker warm under your palm, the lingering electric taste of the moment you flipped it. The LED that marked the tenure names — Mara, Silas, a dozen others — pulses steady and green, a heartbeat you made with your hands. Around you, the Ferryworks smells of wet soil and solder, of algae and lemon oil from Ori's improvised polish. It smells like a small, furious life.

Ava Maren: "All set. Doors open."

Ori Navarro [laughing, voice trembling]: "You're glowing," "In a good way."

You laugh back because laughter pushes the air forward. You wipe your palms on your jacket and fold the notebook into your elbow; the inked tide marks are damp from your thumb. Outside the sliding hatch, families gather under a string of salvaged bulbs—children with rainboots too big for their feet, Old Mara steady as a carved post, neighbors balancing pots on their knees. Someone brings a thermos of sweet tea. Someone else hums an old boat song and the sound hangs like a blessing.

[Background:"ch11_e67f19_2"]
[Sound: Sensors chime — soft, ascending tones as humidity finds balance; a tiny mechanical sigh as a bio-concrete seam settles]
[Music: Tempo quickens; brass stabs with playful warmth]

You move through the Ferryworks like a conductor. Mangrove roots threaded into the bio-concrete have already knit around the first anchor plates; fine white filaments curl up like the beginnings of a promise. You press your palm to one of the living wall panels. It is cool and damp, textured with the infinitesimal grit of reclaimed sand, and it gives under your fingers like a living thing agreeing to be held.

Silas [edges closer, eyes wide, fingers twitching]: "Can I—?" "Can I plant my aunt's seeds here? She says they came from the old market."

You nod before you can think. Nodding tonight feels like the simplest act of governance: permission, trust, continuity. You help him kneel; his hands are quick and sure. Together you press the tiny seed packet into the soil pocket Ori has crafted — a pocket exactly the size of someone’s hope.

Ori Navarro: "Look at them," "they want their hands in it."

Neighbor 1 [soft]: "It's beautiful… how you made the wires look like vines."

Neighbor 2 [laughing]: "Like jewelry for the storm."

You watch Ori's face as praise falls around him. The glow on his cheeks is not just lamplight; it is something that has been building in him since he quit VossCorp and started making things that were allowed to be imperfect and human. His fingers hover over a soldered seam, then tuck a stray fragment of circuitry into a pocket of moss like a secret tucked back into place.

Ava Maren [quiet]: "This is for Saltway. For everyone who sleeps with the tide as a neighbor."

Ori Navarro: "And for anyone who still believes we can make something that holds."

Ori Navarro [soft]: "You see them? The way they touch it like it's already theirs."

Ava Maren: "They are already theirs."

Then the phone buzzes. A neighbor's face lifts as a notification arrives; someone nearby starts chatting about a post that just went up—an influencer with a small but attentive following took a shot through the porthole and wrote about "Harborlight's secret greenhouse." The words ricochet: beautiful, subversive, necessary. Within minutes, the laughter is threaded with a new current: cameras, tags, a spread of light beyond the Ferryworks.

[Sound: Notification chiming sharply; an increase in distant voices; the hum of the prototype brightens]
[Music: Strings and percussion accelerate; a rising, exultant crescendo]

Jun's phone vibrates under his palm. He glances, then looks at you with a grin that strains the edges of something enormous.

Jun: "We wanted this to be seen. Not like this exactly, but—this is traction."

Ava Maren: "Traction is what keeps a community from sliding."

Jun [swift]: "But traction for us is a legal word away from being a target. VossCorp monitors this beat like it's their pulse. I can hear their PR team from a mile off."

Silas [narrowing his eyes]: "They'll try to take it."

Ori Navarro [brisk, joking]: "Then we'll show them how to build it. Brick by mossy brick."

Your chest is a bright piston. The good kind of panic—electric with possibility—threads through your limbs. Everything is moving faster: phones, talk, the green LED that means the living wall's microclimate has stabilized. You feel a ferocious joy, the kind that comes when a plan leaps the gap between blueprint and people.

Jun's face shifts. He steps closer, lowering his voice into something that is both urgent and conspiratorial.

Jun: "Heads-up. I just got an encrypted tip—someone in the civic feed saw the posts and looped in a VossCorp legal contact. They call it an 'emergency assessment' when they want to justify a rapid takeover. They'll file papers to inspect, maybe seize, if they can spin it as a public-safety thing."

Your first thought is not fear. It is a bright, uncompromised clarity: if they come knocking because we are effective, then we are doing something that matters.

Ava Maren: "Then we make them see it's not a threat. We make them see the people."

Ori Navarro: "We could blast the design live—open-source it, force the conversation to be about tools and tenure, not just about spectacle."

Jun: "Or we hold it close enough to build the narrative right: an exposé that ties their loopholes to their decision-making. The legal pressure then becomes a political story."

Silas [hands on his hips]: "Or we keep it here. Let Saltway get stronger and then—then we go big when we're ready."

You can feel the air around you like a membrane about to flex. Every option tastes like brine and sunlight and the copper of adrenaline.

[Background:"ch11_e67f19_3"]
[Sound: The hum resolves into a single, bright chord; then a drumbeat of footsteps as more people edge nearer]
[Music: The strings swell into a triumphant, urgent chorus — very high intensity]

Ori takes your hand. His fingers are warm and steady; his thumb moves in small, nervous circles against the back of your hand. There's so much you could say—promises unfinished, apologies and future plans—but the night itself is saying more: that making, daring, and sharing are acts of love.

Ori Navarro [low, fierce]: "Whatever you decide, I'm with you. Whatever scale, whatever risk—I'm here."

Ava Maren: "Then let's be brave in the way we always are: loud when we must be, careful when we can, honest when it matters."

Jun's recorder blinks red. He looks at the cluster of neighbors, at the images replaying across phones, at the living wall breathing softly. He exhales.

Jun: "You have three clear routes. Each one will make the story different. Each one will change the shape of what we can protect and who gets to help build it."

Your heartbeat matches the LED. The cliff-edge moment arrives with the force of a cresting wave—immediate, inevitable, and luminous. Around you, the Ferryworks is alive with rooted things, with the hands of your neighbors, with Ori's steady presence and Jun's sharp, necessary caution. The city is watching.

You are not afraid. You are fierce, thrilled, and dizzy with possibility.
