You had meant to make the argument in legal language, with margins and clauses that could hold. Instead, the truth is on everyone’s feeds before the first council aide reaches the mic. Nora: "We did the math right. We did the story right. People will—"

Ari: (tilting their head) "I suspected as much. But I need to understand why you chose this way. There were other channels."

You watch the cameras tilt toward Ari, hungry for the spectacle of counterargument. You also watch the faces in the atrium: some already nodding, some stunned, some relieved as if a long secret finally has air to breathe.

The arousal of the world around you remains high, but the feeling under it is sweet and sure: this thing you did is creating a different kind of protection, one that demands patience and presence.
