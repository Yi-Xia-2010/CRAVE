[Scene: Promenade Pilot Site | Early Evening]

[Music: Rhythmic drums and layered vocals; urgency with warmth]
[Sound: Hammers, laughter, shouted measurements; the sea like a steady bassline]

Narration:
You arrive at the promenade and the air changes—there is a new electric current in place, an energy born of doing rather than arguing. Volunteers set down tools, brushes in muddy gloves. She watches without crossing the tape; her expression is immaculate and unreadable, like a polished stone.

Celeste (lifting a hand slightly as if to greet): "Impressive turnout."

Celeste: "Work matters. So does timing. I'm glad to see movement."

Narration:
Her words are both sincere and like a coin placed on a scale. The crowd murmurs—some with supportive claps, others with thin-edged skepticism. Elias: (quiet, close) "She's watching us."

Elias: (searching your face) "You sure you want that?"

Narration:
You move through the volunteer line, kneeling to plant Spartina plugs in the places you mapped. Rae paints a banner: "Keep the Edge Soft" in big, fluted letters.

Tommy (elbowing you, quieter) "You did good getting Elias in the loop. He listens like he actually hears stuff, y'know?"

Elias (coming to stand beside you) "You brought people together. I brought a few sensors and a grant outline. This—" (he gestures at the planted line and the faces around it) "—seems like the place we wanted when we started talking tonight."

Narration:
The two of you plant side by side until your shoulders come close. Your sleeve brushes his—salt damp on fabric—and you hesitate, then use the pause to say what you mean.

You: "If this works, we keep the heart of the Commons here and let the promenade learn to be porous. People stay. Businesses adapt. We don't wall ourselves off from the sea."
Elias: (meeting your eyes) "I want that too. I want to build something that lets people be here for the long haul. With you."

Narration:
That sentence lands like a small landing, a touchdown after a long descent. Around you, people cheer at a planted plug as though it were a christening. Cameras flash. Celeste's lips thin and she steps back, fiddling with her folder. Her expression is complex—appraisal, calculation, and something almost impressed. It cannot be read as anything singular; it's exactly the kind of look that could mean a thousand things later.
