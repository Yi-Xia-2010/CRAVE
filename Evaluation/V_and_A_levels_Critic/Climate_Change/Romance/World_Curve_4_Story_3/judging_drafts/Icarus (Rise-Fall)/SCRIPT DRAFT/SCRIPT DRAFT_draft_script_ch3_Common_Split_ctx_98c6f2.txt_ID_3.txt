Elias Hart: (calling) "Maren, that wall carves the town in two. It might keep the water out in one place and drown us in another — in money and in displacement. There are cheaper, faster ways to build resilience that keep people here."

Maren Voss: (cool) "Mr. Hart, community projects are noble. They are not a replacement for structural engineering."

Elias Hart: "They're not replacements — they're complementary. Salt marshes, modular platforms, distributed jobs: place-based solutions that don't trade one neighborhood's safety for another's survival."

Professor Julian Kim: "If I may. The models I've shared show multiple scenarios. Engineered barriers reduce exposure in high-energy storms but can exacerbate erosion downshore. Restored marsh systems slow wave energy and buy time, but they scale differently and require distributed labor and maintenance. We can phase approaches and test pilot sites. It's not binary."

Narration
