The audit eventually finds its way into public view; Mara's reporting is incisive and relentless. Your decision to demand audits uncovered those words in legal light. On paper—names, clauses, the firm's culpability—are exposed. Councilor Rosa uses the findings to craft new, binding covenants that protect public access and forbid buyouts. In the sterile language of policy, your principles win.

On the ground, the win is brittle.

Rows of houses are gone or condemned. A shoreline community you worked beside for years is dispersed across trailers and borrowed couches. Some neighbors took buyouts earlier than they otherwise would have, others lost everything when the storm outpaced the injunctions. The legal record will stand for the town's future; the immediate reality is loss.

Ari Tanaka (internal): He left, and he returned with tools and receipts; but a person cannot be a bridge unless they stand in the same tide. The bargain felt like a ledger: gains on paper, losses in skin.
