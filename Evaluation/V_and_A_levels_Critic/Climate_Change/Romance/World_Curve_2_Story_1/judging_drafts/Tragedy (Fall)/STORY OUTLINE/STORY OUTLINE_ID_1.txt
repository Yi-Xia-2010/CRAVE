## Visual Novel Title
Tidebridge Tides

---

## World View
Tidebridge is a near-future coastal town pressed between the sea and a memory of what it once sustained. Salt-scarred piers and patched fishing shacks rub shoulders with modular labs and a municipal plaza where protest banners flutter beside investor flags. Geographically, the town sits at the edge of a diminished estuary — peat-stained marshes, braided tidal creeks, and reclaimed plots stitched together with coir logs and hand-built sills. Politically, Tidebridge is contested ground: local restoration collectives, elders and fishermen push for living-shoreline strategies centered on place-keeping, while a sustainability development firm advocates rapid, centralized engineering interventions and managed relocation that promise stability at the cost of privatized waterfronts. Class and cultural lines are drawn along fate and livelihood — longshore families and small fishers, community organizers and returning engineers. Religion or ritual appears as local practice more than institution: tides, seasonal rites of planting and harvest, and Etta’s greenhouse as a sanctuary of stewardship. The atmosphere is painterly and tactile: drizzle, low clouds, sudden shafts of gold, storm-bleached textures, luminous biotech threads that glow against grime. Underneath civic debate loops a private tension: how to love a place enough to save it without breaking the people who live there.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Ari Tanaka, raised in Tidebridge; this is my first-person account of a town at the edge. After decades of erosion and successive storms, our collective is split — Etta’s restoration nursery and my field crews favor living shorelines and marsh revival; Mateo, the engineer I care for, builds buoyed wave-harvesters to power resilient systems; Elio’s firm offers fast, centralized protection that promises safety but demands land and control. The story’s arc is tragic: early optimism and tender moments with Mateo gradually unravel under mistakes, political compromises, and a catastrophic decision that forces painful trade-offs. At a communal council meeting (the first major choice), I must choose where to put my trust — local collective resistance, Mateo’s hybrid tech, or Elio’s top-down plan. Each path leads to different confrontations, moral failures, and losses that culminate in endings ranging from social erosion to a stoic, ongoing struggle. Romance threads through every route: tender touches in Etta’s greenhouse, Mateo’s salt-streaked smile, or quiet nights on a flooded pier. But the climate threat and human choices push us toward fracture; no matter the path, I must face what I sacrificed and what I could not save.
