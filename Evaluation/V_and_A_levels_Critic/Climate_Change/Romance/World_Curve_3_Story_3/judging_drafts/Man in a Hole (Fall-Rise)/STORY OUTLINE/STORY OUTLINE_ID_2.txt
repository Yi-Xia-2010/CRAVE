## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Marisol

---

## World View
Marisol Bay is a near-future coastal town where rising seas and stronger storms have reshaped daily life. Small-scale fisheries, family-run shops, and fraying historic quarters sit beside new scaffolding and corporate staging zones. Political power tilts among an elected mayor balancing petitions, state funds, and developer promises; an old fishing cooperative that guards local traditions; and an incoming climate-tech firm promising large-scale defenses. The culture blends intergenerational memory—boats, boardwalks, and oral histories—with scientific fieldwork and civic activism. Religion and formal ritual are ancillary; communal rituals instead orbit harvests, memorials for storm victims, and town meetings. Class divides are subtle: boat-owners and cooperative members prize autonomy; municipal officials navigate resources and funding; younger residents oscillate between leaving for city opportunities and building resilient futures. Geography is coastal marsh, tidal creeks, a harbor of bobbing boats, and a historic quarter already tasting salt. Atmosphere: salt-thin nostalgia, urgency couched in neighborly hospitality, the constant murmur of policy meetings and community organizing. Visual style is coastal watercolor realism—muted sea-teals, storm-grays, warm sunset oranges—layered with film grain and textured brushstrokes.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Maia Rivera, a returning coastal ecologist tasked with restoring wetlands and protecting Marisol Bay’s livelihoods. The story is a romance-driven moral drama about whether to champion community-led resilience, partner with Elias Wren’s large-scale climate infrastructure, or expose compromised deals and rebuild trust from the ground up. The opening arc traces a fall into crisis: a sudden storm event, political pressure, and a leak about a proposed corporate plan fracture local consensus and strain Maia’s relationships—especially with Jonah Kade, the steadfast fisher. From that nadir, each branch explores different recoveries: Route A pursues community-first hybrid solutions and deepens a romance with Jonah; Route B negotiates with Elias’s firm, gaining infrastructure and economic relief but risking autonomy and shifting feelings toward Elias; Route C chooses principled transparency or risky direct action, generating painful losses or moral victory. The emotional curve is Man in a Hole: fall into crisis (community split, personal guilt), struggle and low point, then recovery through negotiation, sacrifice, or revelation. Every choice shapes Maia’s romantic outcomes and the town’s future.
