## Visual Novel Title
Saltbridge Rising

---

## World View
Saltbridge is a near-future coastal town roughly a decade into escalating sea-level impacts. Once a modest fishing community, its waterfront now alternates between crumbling clapboard cottages and polished corporate pilot projects. Political power is split: a city hall negotiating subsidies, a private firm designing high-tech barriers, and neighborhood networks improvising sandbags, rooftop gardens, and tidal pumps. Religion and ritual persist in small ways—altar-like shrines of driftwood and shells at porches—but civic life centers on community halls, cafés, and occasional emergency shelters. The economy is a mixed knot of small fisheries, artisanal makers, and outside engineering money. Class tensions are visible in who can afford retrofit living and who must relocate.

Culture is pragmatic and neighborly; people still trade tools and recipes. The conflict is structural: short election cycles, municipal risk aversion, and corporate proposals that look secure but displace longtime residents. Geography is salt-battered boardwalks, dunes stitched with sandbags, and a reclaimed rooftop garden where bees hum over salvage lumber. The antagonist is not a single person but a slow, indifferent system—policy inertia and rising waters that force intimate choices. Visual style evokes soft anime character art over neo-realistic watercolor backgrounds: seafoam greens, slate blues, rust, and salt textures. The atmosphere is melancholic hope—warm human rituals against the backdrop of urgent tides.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Serrano, a community resilience coordinator in Saltbridge. The story begins as the tides and politics tighten: funds are delayed, an incoming storm exposes weaknesses, and the town fractures along lines of expediency. The "man in a hole" arc plunges me into a deep crisis—my family and neighbors face imminent flooding, a policy vote is stalled, and an old trauma resurfaces. From that low point I must choose a path to pull Saltbridge back: align with Elias’s grassroots direct action, negotiate a pragmatic settlement with Noah’s firm, or navigate municipal compromise through Mayor Cho and scientific strategy with Dr. Hana. Each romantic route represents a different response to climate crisis—radical mobilization, institutional pragmatism, or negotiated compromise—and every choice reframes what I sacrifice for community and love.

Branches depict different trade-offs: victories with community-led modular barriers, bittersweet pragmatic protections with lost blocks, or painful corporate wins requiring continued resistance. All paths move from collapse toward recovery, but the costs and relationships differ. Romance colors decisions—each love interest's visual presence (Elias’s sandy hair and camera; Noah’s steel-blue eyes and tailored coat) and values influence outcomes. Endings range from Community Triumph (happy) to Compromise and Renewal (bittersweet), Corporate Win (sad), and Catalytic Sacrifice (true). Across every branch I grow: learning to accept help, to balance ethical purity and realism, and to redefine love amid climate choices.
