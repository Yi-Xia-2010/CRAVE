[Scene: Rosa’s Café | Mid-Morning]
[Visual: Warm interior, chipped tiles, steam curling from cups; a map-strewn table under a string of bulbs]
[Music: Acoustic guitar with a hopeful glide]
[Sound: The hiss of the espresso machine; the low murmur of neighbors exchanging pantry tips]
Narration:
Rosa's café is both chapel and command center. The smell of coffee blankets strategy the way tide blankets the shore — familiar, comforting, essential.

Rosa Alvarez: (pulling a tray of cinnamon scones) "You look like you earned this."

Maya Serrano: (you fold your fingers around the cup) "We've got drywall diagrams and water budgets and a model from Dr. Hana that finally shows modular barriers behaving like neighborhoods, not fortresses."  
(You open the notebook, pages crisp with diagrams and hand-scrawled costings.) "We can do this without erasing anyone."

Rosa Alvarez: "Then say that. Make 'without erasing anyone' your headline."  
(She leans in. Her voice softens.) "People will come if they hear hope with a blueprint."

Dr. Hana Park: (arriving with a compact tablet, breathless from the climb) "I've simulated tidal exchange with permeable modules — living shorelines built by communities, scaled in blocks the town can maintain."  
(She taps the screen; a cool model ripples.) "You don't need a million-dollar wall. You need modular cells, community ownership, prioritized relocation funds."

Maya Serrano: "And they'll take that seriously?"  
(You study Hana's face for a moment — the scientist's lines are present when she knows data will stand up in public.)

Dr. Hana Park: "Data plus public capacity equals defensibility at council. We can show cost-effectiveness, we can show deployment timeframes. And you can show you've already done it."  
(She nods at the rooftop toast and the rain barrel system.) "That changes the conversation."

Narration:
For the first time in weeks, the plan has legs that don't tremble. Your throat loosens in a way that feels like exhaling into a room where people are already breathing the same air.

Elias Novak: (taking your hand, thumb warm against your knuckles) "So what do we celebrate?"  
(He teases, but his eyes are wet with relief.)

Maya Serrano: (you look at him, and then at the people around you) "We celebrate that we stayed. We celebrate that we made room for everyone in the plan."  
(You lean into his warmth. Your braid ticks like a small, satisfied clock.)

You let the rooftop be both memorial and future. You realize love here is not an escape from duty but the connective tissue that makes duty possible. You feel the promise in your chest — not a weight but a scaffold.
