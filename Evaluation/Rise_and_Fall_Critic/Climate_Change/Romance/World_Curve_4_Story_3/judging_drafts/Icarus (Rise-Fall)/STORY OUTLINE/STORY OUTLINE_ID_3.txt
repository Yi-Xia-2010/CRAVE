## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promise

---

## World View
Havenpoint, 2035–2040: a narrow coastal town folded between eroding cliffs and a bay mottled with kelp beds. Once a fishing and shipbuilding hub, the town now wears adaptation like layered clothing—patchwork levees, living marsh terraces, solar steps, and stilted houses. Political power is fractured: a regional authority eager for visible infrastructure, local councilors balancing re-election and empathy, and grassroots cooperatives improvising long-term solutions. Class tensions trace the shoreline—boat yards and market stalls shoulder into renovated tech pavilions. Culture blends ritual and retrofit: Elder Mae’s storytelling nights sit beside community solar installations; fishermen insist on tide knowledge while engineers model surge scenarios. Religion and ritual are secularized into place-based devotion—memorials for lost docks and seasonal blessings of the nets. Geography alternates flat salt pans and jagged shipyard ruins; the sea is both livelihood and threat. The atmosphere is painterly, salt-streaked, hopeful-but-tense: warm bulb light on wet boardwalks, fog-softened horizons, modular pavilions glowing like surgical beacons. Conflicts hinge on time preference—short-term investor confidence (and quick concrete) vs. slower, community-led nature-based adaptation. Romance and politics weave together: who we choose to love becomes a political act in a town deciding which memories to preserve and which to let go.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Marin Solace—returnee engineer and cooperative leader—navigating love, leadership, and climate stakes in first person. The story follows an Icarus (Rise-Fall) arc: rapid ascent to a high, celebrated success and then an abrupt, catastrophic collapse. Chapters 1–5 build momentum: Marin’s pilot projects, public credibility, and a fragile romance with Jonah bloom as the cooperative gains traction. At a political inflection (Chapter 3), choices fork into three routes of strategic engagement (local partnership, fisher-centered compromise, or direct confrontation with Lydia’s plan). Each route accelerates toward a visible triumph—grant awards, prototype installs, media praise—reaching a brief, euphoric apex. But decisions made to win quickly (alliances, concessions, compromised messaging, or overreach) precipitate a collapse: funding reversals, a sudden storm that exposes engineering gaps, investor pressure that sidelines community voices, or a divisive public scandal. Endings vary—some contain salvage and bittersweet continuations, some are painful losses, and one reframes the fall as the start of broader grassroots movement—but all obey the arc: the Icarian rise is followed by a fall that forces reckoning with principle, responsibility, and love.
