## Visual Novel Title
Lowry Tides

---

## World View
Lowry Bay is a near-future coastal town pinned between rising tides and the political economy that seeks to tame them. Once a small fishing community, it has become a contested zone where municipal planners, private consortia, and grassroots brigades vie for the town’s future. The municipal government is pragmatic but vulnerable to funding pressures; Mayor Lin Park is the bureaucratic pivot between livelihoods and large-scale investment. Jonah Hale represents the technical-capital axis: polished, efficient, promising hard infrastructure and redevelopment that secure property values but risk displacement. Against him stand community organizers and local scientists—Cassian Rhys and myself—favoring decentralized, nature-based resilience: tidal marshes, living berms, and community desal systems.

The culture is rooted in intergenerational memory, ritual tides, and shared labor—boardwalk fishermen, greenhouse horticulturalists, and muralists who repurpose salvaged materials. Religion is less formal than reverence: lighthouse candle vigils and harvest rites for the drowned orchard. Class tensions map to geography: the southern spit is fortified by early private investments; the old neighborhoods near the boardwalk are vulnerable and culturally dense.

Geography is coastal: fog-laced mornings on the boardwalk, the humid, plant-sweet Tidelab greenhouse, a modernist City Hall with banners, the intimate Drowned Orchard, and the sentinel Old Northpoint Lighthouse. Conflicts center on whether to accept centralized, engineered certainty or embrace distributed ecological strategies that demand sacrifice. The atmosphere is painterly and weathered—sea-glass greens, rust-orange, and storm-gray—where every intimate moment can be scored against the rising tide.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I (Mira Kestrel) return to Lowry Bay to lead nature-based coastal resilience work while carrying the guilt of a failed pilot. The narrative is a Cinderella arc: our community rises from grassroots effort to local recognition; we reach a temporary peak when funding and political attention converge; a sudden fall follows as an engineered mega-barrier threatens to displace neighbors and fracture allegiances; then a final rise brings a hard-won, principled victory—of different flavors across paths (happy hybrid success, bittersweet compromise, sad displacement, or a sacrificial "true" victory).

The branches explore choices between personal desire (a life with Cassian, preserving our community's soul) and communal responsibility (hard decisions about relocation and safety). Jonah’s polished pragmatism challenges my empathy-driven caution; Cassian’s idealistic organizing tests my methodical caution. Routes diverge at a tense City Hall hearing and then split further as I decide whether to collaborate with, resist, or expose elements of the barrier project. Visual romance develops in shared labor—salt-streaked lunches on the boardwalk, paint on Cassian’s jacket, and quiet confessions in the lighthouse—while climatic stakes (failing crops, a storm surge, Ruben’s fate) press urgency. Each branch adheres to the Rise → Fall → Rise curve: the game lifts us, knocks us down, then gives a recovery shaped by the path we chose.
