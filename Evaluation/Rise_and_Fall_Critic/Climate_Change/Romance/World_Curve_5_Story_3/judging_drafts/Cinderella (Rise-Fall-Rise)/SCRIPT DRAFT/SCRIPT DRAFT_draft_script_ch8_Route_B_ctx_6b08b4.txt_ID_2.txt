Jun: "We rigged the new sensor array last night. If the tide's going to test us, we'll know when it's coming and how the roots respond."

You crouch beside them, palms touching the wet earth. The mud smells ancient and immediate — oyster shells, algae, the metallic tang that comes when salt and soil collide. Your diver's watch ticks against your wrist; the barometer needle nudges toward steady.

You (Aria Navarro): "Good. The more we measure, the more we can show that this is working. And the more we can ask for time."

Jun: "Promise me you won't let them rush us into concrete just because the first month has wind and waves." (they jab a muddy finger playfully but their eyes are earnest)

You: "I won't. But I won't let us be reckless either. Technique matters as much as patience."

Narration:
Sora approaches from the boardwalk, an armful of hand-painted stakes and a coil of rope slung over their shoulder. Sora: "I painted names on the stakes. For the families who'll live with these roots when they grow. It's ridiculous and sentimental and important."

They hand you a stake; the paint is still tacky under your fingers. The name is familiar — a neighbor, an old teacher — and the moment is unexpectedly heavy and tender.

You: "Mateo would also say our design lacks measurable failure modes." (you can't keep the smile from your voice)

Sora, eyes warming: "Good. Then we'll make both of them wrong — in the best way."

Sora: "You look like you keep a thousand lists in your head. Does any of them ever let you sleep?"
