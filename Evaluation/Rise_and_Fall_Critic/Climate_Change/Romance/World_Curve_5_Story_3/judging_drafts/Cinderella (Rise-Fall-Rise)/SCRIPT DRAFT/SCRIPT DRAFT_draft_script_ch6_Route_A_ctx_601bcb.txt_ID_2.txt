[Music: Piano motif rising into a hopeful chord]

You stand in the lab where so many late nights have folded into long days. The air smells of coffee, ozone, and a faint trace of solder. Dr. Kavir prepares to deliver the final piece of the puzzle: the formal offer from the foundation, and the legal language it contains.

Dr. Kavir: "They want measurable outcomes—understandable. They want to ensure funds are well used. But some clauses hand control over contracting decisions. They'd expect data dashboards with limited external access."

You read the document—clear, polished, generous. Yet between the lines, there is the tilt of control away from those whose lives are shaped by these decisions.

You: "They can help with equipment and training. But I can't accept anything that bypasses the council."

Dr. Kavir studies you, the familiar crease between his brows softening.

Dr. Kavir: "It's a hard line. You understand the possibilities you'll be forgoing—accelerated infrastructure, broader dissemination of our work. Are you prepared for that?"

You consider the weight of what acceptance would do: faster deployments, but at the expense of local voice. You imagine a community seat at an external board, tokenized. You imagine Elena's hands tied in protocols she won't understand. You imagine Sora's art banned from shorelines because it 'doesn't fit the branding.'

You: "I'm prepared to build slower if it means we build together."

Mateo steps closer, voice low and sure.

Mateo: "And you'll have help. The municipality has committed seed funding, and the council—if we keep to the timeline—will be eligible for cooperative grants that respect local governance. We'll map procurement to local suppliers. We'll make it work."

Mayor Isla: "I'll back the council in municipal budget talks. It won't be easy, but it's the right thing."

There is a pause in the lab, the kind that stores itself in the bones of a place where decisions have weight. Dr. Kavir exhales, then smiles—small, proud.

Dr. Kavir: "Then we decline the foundation's conditions. We accept their offer to collaborate on an open-data platform, but the governance stays here. You keep control."

You feel the knot around your ribs relax, as if something finally recognizes its shape and is allowed to settle. The decision is rooted in more than stubbornness—it is a protection, a letting-go of a quick fix for sustained agency.

Elena: "You chose the town." (She says it as a fact, not a question.)

You: "I did. We did."

Mateo takes your hand—this time not a steadying touch for work, but a claim that is soft and cause enough for a small, startled warmth. He doesn't need to be florid; his words are enough.

Mateo: "Stay. Build this with me."

You turn to him, the lab's monitors blinking the sea's slow heartbeat into the room. There is no grand sweep, only a choice that is also a promise. You think of the nights that spooled past—papers, tide lines, the smell of the quay at dawn—and you find your voice both certain and tender.

You: "I want that."

They lean in. The kiss is gentle like a tide—surprising, inevitable, and steadying. Around you, the lab's soft electronic chorus seems to swell in approval, if machines can ever applaud.

Sora watches from across the room. Their expression is complex—an unreadable tidal face that folds into a smile that is almost mischief. They clap once, sharp and delighted.

Sora: "Finally. About time, engineers."

Mateo laughs—brief, true—and you laugh with him. The laughter is a little like sunlight on aluminum: warm, reflective, and promising.
