Cassian Rhee: "Binding oversight introduces liabilities that will slow implementation. Investors want certainty, Marin. We can offer safeguards—merit-based audits, third-party review—"

You cut him off before "risk" can become a weapon.

You: "Not 'safeguards' that are cosmetic. Not audits that the sponsor chooses. Community oversight isn't a checkbox. It's a seat at the table, vetoes for critical access, public reporting that can't be buried in gray files."

Cassian stiffens; his voice cools to thin diplomacy.

Cassian Rhee: "You understand what that means. Delays. Cost overruns. The model investors signed up for won't tolerate open-ended oversight. We could lose financing and leave the town with—"

Ariana Voss: "Cassian, we both know accountability builds trust. If this is a partnership with Havenport, it has to be with the town in control of the archive, the access rights. We can't let private gates decide who can fish which inlets."

Cassian's jaw works. He leans in, not aggressively but with the kind of precision that has toppled softer arguments before.

Cassian Rhee: "Idealism is noble, Ariana, but I'm here to get results. You're asking me to write a contract that will scare away the money that funds those results."

You: "Then we write protections that don't just sit pretty on paper. Bind them. Enforce them. Make them verifiable. If the investors want certainty, we'll give them a certainty they can see: transparent ledgers, community auditors, publicly accessible access logs."

Cassian's fingers drum the table. For a moment he seems to calculate forgiveness, then rejects it.

Cassian Rhee: "You want clauses that look good on paper and threaten the speed of delivery. That's not my job, Marin. My job is to protect the thing that works."

Lina, who had been quiet until now, lifts her chin. There's a hardness in her voice that cuts the room's stale air.

Lina Park: "And whose job is it to protect the town's rights when 'what works' excludes half the harbor? If this becomes a privatized inlet scheme, people will be locked out of places they've used for generations."

Cassian Rhee: "Those allegations are speculative."
