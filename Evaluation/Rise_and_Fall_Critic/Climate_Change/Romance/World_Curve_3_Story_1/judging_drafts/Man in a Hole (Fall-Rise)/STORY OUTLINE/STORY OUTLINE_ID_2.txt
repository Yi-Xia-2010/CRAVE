## Visual Novel Title
Mariner's Reach: Tides Between Us

---

## World View
Mariner's Reach is a mid-sized coastal town in 2035, a place caught between weathered fishing tradition and urgent climate adaptation. Rising seas and increasingly violent storms have reshaped the town's economy and rhythms: piers broken into memories, boatyards repurposed as community labs, and municipal budgets stretched thin. Politically, the town is governed by a town council that must balance blue-collar livelihoods with regional pressure to modernize infrastructure; municipal planners negotiate funding while residents organize grassroots retrofits. Culture mixes maritime folklore and cooperative ingenuity — rooftop gardens, solar canopies, and salvaged timber workshops sit beside neon-lit fish markets. Class tensions surface when development and short-term revenue threaten communal spaces; religion and formal belief are secondary to civic rituals — the festival pier, the repair-night lanterns, and Etta Bloom's cooperative seedbank. Geographically, narrow cobbled lanes funnel into a battered promenade and an exposed pier; storms turn the scene from golden warmth to cold, neon-lit spray. The atmosphere is melancholic, weathered, and quietly defiant: people patch what the sea takes and refuse to let the horizon be only loss.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
Iris Alvarez returns to Mariner's Reach determined to shepherd community-led adaptation projects that protect the town’s character and people. Soon, a severe storm and a municipal budget shortfall plunge the town into crisis: funding is threatened, critical infrastructure is compromised, and old alliances fracture. The "man in a hole" emotional arc takes hold: Iris falls from steadiness into guilt, helplessness, and political stalemate. At the lowest point, interpersonal rifts with Kai and Marin intensify — each offers competing paths to resilience (radical grassroots tech, pragmatic municipal consolidation, or risky hybrid solutions). Across branching routes, Iris’s choices about who to trust, which compromises to accept, and how to prioritize community versus personal ties dictate different recoveries. Some routes build unexpected coalitions that scale her work regionally; others rescue only the town’s core at the cost of cherished spaces; a few outcomes end in painful losses and fractured relationships. Ultimately the narrative explores love as both catalyst and comfort; whether Iris rises depends on her capacity to balance empathy, scientific rigor, and willingness to accept painful trade-offs.
