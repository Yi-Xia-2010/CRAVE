## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Mariner's Reach is a mid-sized coastal town in 2035 negotiating the strain between memory and survival. Once built on fishing and shipwright trades, the town now reads tides as budgets: streets skirt higher watermarks, and the pier that hosted summer festivals is a fractured silhouette. Municipal Hall sits across from the Greenhouse Commons, where reclaimed shipyard panels warm seedlings beneath solar film. Roof gardens and modular seawalls rub shoulders with rusted crane arms and small boat repair stalls. Politics are lean and pragmatic; councillors bargain between federal grants, local business survival, and grassroots pressure. Community culture favors collective rituals — night market fish fries, seed exchanges at Etta’s garden — that are threatened by erosion, runaway heatwaves, and sharper storms.

Religion and formal institutions take background roles; civic memory and place-attachment are the town's faith. The class hierarchy is pragmatic rather than rigid: boatwrights, municipal staff, cooperative gardeners, and tech-savvy returnees form overlapping networks. Conflicts center on adaptation strategy: open-source, DIY resilience versus formal, engineered infrastructure that asks for trade-offs (land use, displacement of small businesses). Geography is intimate and combative — a narrow harbor, flanked by breakwaters, a promenade, and patched rooftops that catch both sun and salt. The atmosphere is melancholic but stitched with hope: lamp-lit alleys, damp stone, and rooftop chlorophyll resisting the dark.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Iris Alvarez, back in Mariner's Reach to shepherd community-led adaptation. A mounting crisis — a violent coastal storm season and a crucial funding shortfall — triggers a Fall: infrastructural failures, political gridlock, and a public disaster that shoves me into the town's worst hour. "Man in a Hole" guides the emotion: the first chapters spiral down as a familiar shore is lost and personal relationships fray; I confront blame, guilt, and the possibility of leaving. Then, with allies — Kai (the activist-engineer), Marin (the municipal planner), Tomas, Etta, and reluctant partners — recovery begins. Each branching route tests different mixtures of love and strategy: grassroots ingenuity led by Kai, structured municipal compromise with Marin, or pragmatic economic coalition with Tomas and Lina. Choices determine which compromises we accept and which losses we mourn. Across every branch, the arc is the same: I fall into crisis, hit the nadir around the split, then climb back by building trust, adapting tactics, and rekindling connection. Endings vary in hue (happy, bittersweet, sad, or “true”) but all follow the Fall→Rise trajectory: the town — and my heart — are remade through hardship and commitment.

Notes on Navigation and Narrative Logic:
- Chapters 1–3 form the Common Route and the Fall of the Man in a Hole arc; they are all tagged [Fall].
- Chapters 4–15 continue the Rise phase with multiple routes and alternate endings; all are tagged [Rise].
- Major splits: Chapter 3 (initial fork to Route A/Route B/Route C), Chapter 5 (within Route A: decisions that can jump to Chapter 13), Chapter 8 (within Route B: decisions that can jump to Chapter 14), Chapter 11 (within Route C: decisions that can jump to Chapter 15). These ensure at least three distinct split points.
- Route Depth: Any complete path from Chapter 1 to an Ending contains no more than six chapters. Examples: 1→2→3→4→5→6; 1→2→3→7→8→9; 1→2→3→10→11→12; alternate endings are reached as 1→2→3→4→5→13, 1→2→3→7→8→14, and 1→2→3→10→11→15.
- Visual and character traits are referenced consistently: Iris’s chestnut hair, braided bracelet, bent metal ring; Kai’s jet-black hair and ocean-blue eyes; Marin’s steel-blonde hair and slate trench coat; other secondary characters’ visual markers.
- POV: All narrative passages are written in first-person as Iris Alvarez.
- Emotional arc: Global Fall (Chapters 1–3) into crisis, then Rise across all branches (Chapters 4–15), aligning with "Man in a Hole" (Fall→Rise).
