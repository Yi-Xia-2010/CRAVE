## Visual Novel Title
Seabright Rising

---

## World View
Seabright is a mid-sized coastal town in 2035 where tides have become a constant part of daily conversation. The townscape is half retrofit, half ruin: boardwalks raised unevenly beside sagging piers, rooftop gardens contrived atop shuttered storefronts, and a mosaic of community-built seawalls and corporate scaffolds. Political power is fragmented — a wary town council, activist networks, small engineering firms, and private developers who promise quick fixes. Culture is communal and practical: morning coffee in Rosa’s café, town meetings that bleed into late-night repairs, and elders like Old Man Tor telling weather stories that shape local identity. Religion and ritual survive in small shrines to the sea; economic class shows in who can afford stilts and who piles sandbags. Geography is intimate: the bay, the Old Harbor, the mangrove flats, the Beacon (Community Climate Center) and Town Hall form narrative anchors. Atmosphere is painterly coastal realism: salt-scraped wood, rust-orange railings, storm-muted blues, neon-reflected puddles. Conflicts are systemic and personal — scientific urgency versus economic survival versus political expediency — and the central question is whether love and community can steer an ethical, viable path through climate crisis without losing the town’s soul.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I — Amara Vale, a scientist returned to Seabright — aim to shepherd community-led adaptation: mangroves, raised promenades, microgrids. The story is a steady Rise: I gather allies (Eli, Hal, Miriam, Rosa), face Marco Voss’s charismatic development pitch, and navigate ethical trade-offs. Each decision route is a different kind of progress: grassroots ecological resilience, engineered pragmatic protection, or negotiated hybrid stewardship. Branches explore collaboration with Eli and Hal (engineering + ecology), conditional partnerships with Marco that leverage private capital, and pure community mobilization with Miriam’s organizers. Multiple split points push me to balance intimacy with activism — my relationship with Eli grows as we design solutions together or strains as we compromise — but every route moves Seabright toward stronger capacity: ecological restoration, durable infrastructure, or institutional hybridity. Endings vary in texture (preserved character with trade-offs; partial losses with durable gains; hybrid governance) but all represent advancement from the storm-worn stasis we began in. The emotional arc is upward: each choice, even when costly, builds resilience, trust, or institutional muscle that lifts town and relationship forward.
