## Visual Novel Title
Tidelines Between Us

---

## World View
A small temperate coastal town in the near-future (early 2030s) clings to its low cliffs and salt-dulled boardwalks. The sea has already rewritten daily life: seasonal storms arrive stronger and earlier, tidal gardens and living seawalls run alongside weathered wooden storefronts, and municipal signs ask residents to check tide alerts before leaving home. The town’s politics orbit around scarce funding, developer proposals, and the slow, exhausting labor of community adaptation. Class lines are visible in the harboryards and renovated lofts; the council chambers are a battleground between pragmatic development and neighbor-led resilience. Religion is small and local—chapels lit by candlelight after storm watches—while civic rituals center on volunteer tides and repair brigades. Geography mixes a crescent harbor, a rising line of dunes retrofitted with marsh planting, a rooftop observatory above Municipal Hall, and an abandoned resort sagging back into the sea. Atmosphere: briny, windswept, melancholic but alive with urgency—the town’s weathered faces, creaking docks, and patches of eco-engineering speak to a culture struggling to keep its identity while surviving climate pressures. Everyday life is tactile: salt in hair, mud on boots, the hum of instruments in greenhouses, and the constant bleep of alerts.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Maya Ortega: hometown environmental engineer returned to save my town by blending low-tech, humane adaptation with social stewardship. The story unfolds as a compressed political season and a looming storm create a moral crucible. The initial Fall thrust: funding is threatened, Councilwoman Tamsin pushes a rapid, top-down seawall, and old losses—my family home lost in a flood—resurface. The Rise is a brief window of improvisation and hope: Elias and I rally neighbors, prototype living defenses at the harbor and rooftop gardens, temporary relief and economic plans spark genuine community momentum, and a fragile romance grows amid shared labor and late-night planning in my boathouse lab. The final Fall descends when a decision—political compromise, rushed builds, or a withheld concession—angles the town toward catastrophe. Stormfronts test every choice. Across multiple branches I must decide whether to push for community-led defenses, accept quick outside funding that changes the town’s face, or search for an improvised hybrid; each route reaches a temporary success and then a hard downturn, forcing losses, accountability, and reshaped relationships. The Oedipus arc (Fall → Rise → Fall) yields endings from bitter survival to fractured love and realistic persistence: nothing is cleanly victorious; the sea and politics are unforgiving, and growth comes through hard cost.
