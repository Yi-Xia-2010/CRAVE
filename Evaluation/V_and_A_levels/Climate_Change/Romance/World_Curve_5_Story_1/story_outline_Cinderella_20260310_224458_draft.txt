## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Marisol Bay is a small, weathered coastal town in the 2030s where sea levels and storms have become a constant presence. Economies center on fishers, small-scale aquaculture, and crafts; the boardwalk and the marshlands are both livelihoods and cultural memory. Political power is mixed: a cautious municipal council, pressured by regional infrastructure priorities, negotiates with an influential corporate actor — Voss Marine Solutions — which advances large-scale engineering fixes. Social life is braided through family ties, community meetings at the low-ceilinged Community Hall, and TideLab’s messy optimism. Religion is local ritual and respect for the sea; Abuela Rosa keeps oral histories that frame the shoreline as both home and teacher. Class is not stark, but economic strain is visible: patched overalls, jury-rigged solar panels, and homes at risk. Conflict pits grassroots resilience — low-tech marsh restoration, seed-based defenses, cooperative aquaculture — against Corinne Voss’s polished, profit-driven seawall and industrial aquaculture plan. Geography compresses politics: the boardwalk, marshland, TideLab shed, and Voss’s quay sit within sight of each other, so every intervention changes neighbors’ lives. The visual style is semi-realistic anime-influenced: muted coastal palettes, textured brushwork for water and weather, practical field clothing, salt-stained boots and sun-faded denim patched with care.

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
You are Amaya Reyes — chestnut bob, sea-glass green eyes rimmed with tiredness, wearing an olive field jacket and patched overalls — first-person narrator. The story follows a Cinderella arc: a rise (the town rallies and a fragile peak of hope is reached), a sudden fall (a crisis or betrayal undermines trust and places homes at risk), and a final rise (a strategic, costly victory toward community-centered resilience). The core conflict is between TideLab’s community-led adaptation approach (you and Luka, Mateo, Abuela Rosa, Jules) and Corinne Voss’s corporate-scale seawall and aquaculture development. Romantic tension with Luka — inventive, amber-eyed, with a battered drone controller at his hip — threads through strategic choices. At critical meetings you must choose rhetoric, alliances, and ethics: protest, legal strategy, compromise, or risky disclosure. Each major decision branches to outcomes that range from negotiated hybrid protections to painful relocations, or to systemic policy changes achieved through legal, social, and technological pressure. Visual and tactile details (salt stains, weather-beaten hands, waterlogged tide maps) underscore the stakes. The player experiences Amaya’s internal guilt and stubborn resilience as both a strength and a liability — decisions force delegation, trust, and sacrifice. Across branches the emotional spine remains: a rise to hope, a fall into crisis, then a rise toward justice and resilience.
