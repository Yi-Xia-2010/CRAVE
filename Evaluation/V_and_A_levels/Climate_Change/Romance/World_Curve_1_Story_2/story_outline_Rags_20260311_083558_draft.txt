## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Seabreak Hollow

---

## World View
Seabreak Hollow is a small coastal town in 2035 where the ritual of daily tides collides with the blunt logic of municipal planning. Once a clustered hub of Victorian storefronts and family-run fish sheds, the town now wears modern retrofit tech—sensor buoys, patched dikes, solar strips—like provisional armor. The marshes that braided the inlet have thinned under rising seas and stronger storms; boardwalks warp, docks lean, and the smell of brine is threaded with diesel. Politically, local councils juggle legacy livelihoods against regional investment deals: Councilor Tomas Hale presses for quick capital and jobs; regional planners promise stability through engineered defenses; NGOs like Priya Anand’s coordinate grants and community pilots. Class feels small-town intimate: third-generation fishers with patched pea coats, scientific returnees with battered notebooks, volunteers painting protest banners on the community rooftop garden. Religion and ritual survive as cultural memory—Old Man Reyes tells marsh myths over tea—but decision-making now lives in fluorescent offices at Vernon & Crow and late-night rooftop meetings under fairy lights. Atmosphere: melancholic resilience—fog-bleached paint, damp wool, and the glint of reclaimed marsh pools at dawn. The core conflict: engineered seawall vs. nature-led restoration, and whether love between two people who share this place can hold through the ethical compromises that choice demands.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I return to Seabreak Hollow as Maya Kwon—marine ecologist, field gear scuffed, a leather notebook with tide charts at my hip—determined to restore the marshes that once protected my childhood home. The town faces a decisive political vote. The story rises: each choice we make—mobilize volunteers, negotiate compromises, or accept engineered certainty—builds community momentum, personal connection, and technical complexity. As I navigate councilrooms under fluorescent glare and rain-slashed nights at the lighthouse lab, my growing bond with Aiden Reyes—his salt-dark thermos, braided rope bracelet—mirrors the town’s capacity to balance tradition and adaptation. Dr. Elara Voss, in her sleek trench and bright municipal badge, pushes large-scale engineering, forcing moral tradeoffs. Branch points force me to reconcile grief over the storm that took my parents’ home with the urgent need to act. The arc is rise: incremental victories, expanding coalitions, and learned humility culminate in one of several endings where Seabreak Hollow either embraces hybrid solutions, settles for partial engineering, grimly accepts a seawall, or begins a long, generational restoration—while my relationship with Aiden grows, strains, or reorients into committed partnership shaped by choices, loss, and hope.
