## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Hearts

---

## World View
A near-future coastal town stitched from reclaimed land, piers, and sinking marsh. Once a bustling shipyard and market, it now cycles through semi-permanent floods and seasonal superstorms. Municipal politics are a fragile negotiation between long-time residents who cling to place and culture, a charismatic cooperative that runs the market, and a polished development firm promising engineered security. The firm’s Voss Coastal Spire—gleaming, guarded, hydraulics visible—casts cold light over patched boardwalks and rooftop gardens. Community radios hum; drones measure tides; pumps thrum day and night. Religion and ritual are informal: morning fishing blessings, memorials for storm victims, and Aunt Pilar’s oral histories that bind neighbors. Class stratification is spatial and cultural—luxury redevelopment sits elevated and sterile while low-lying neighborhoods breathe salt and moss, repaired again and again. Climate urgency drives everything: contested land-use hearings at Town Hall Plaza, clandestine data leaks, and grassroots marsh restoration projects. Atmospherically, the world favors muted coastal palettes with neon accents: wet reflections, watercolor fog, moss-slick metal, and cinematic golden-hour light that turns to storm-lit neon at night. Romance and politics interweave—love is not an escape but a lever that forces hard choices about who we save and what we become.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mara Solano: returned coastal ecologist, practical and fiercely loyal. The story begins with a crisis: Elena Voss’s high-tech coastal barrier and luxury redevelopment plan gains political traction, promising stability but threatening displacement. My relationship with Jonah Reyes—charismatic, community-minded—becomes the engine of moral choice. Early chapters are a descent: meetings collapse, a storm exposes vulnerabilities, and legal and social pressure push me toward untenable compromises. At the bottom, I must choose among three strategies: mobilize the community with Jonah, negotiate with technocratic power for partial protections, or expose Elena’s proprietary risks with Dr. Claire Hsu’s help. Each path leads through a confrontation—public action, council votes, or data leaks—and then a recovery phase of rebuilding and reckoning. Across branches, the overall emotional curve is "Man in a Hole": a fall into crisis followed by a climb toward recovery. Endings diverge into four tonal outcomes—Happy (community-led restoration and love deepened), Bittersweet (compromise with losses), Sad (Elena’s redevelopment predominates; town fractured), and True (exposure forces systemic change but personal cost as I leave to lead elsewhere). All outcomes keep the fall-then-rise arc: even loss includes renewal, and victory carries emotional cost.
