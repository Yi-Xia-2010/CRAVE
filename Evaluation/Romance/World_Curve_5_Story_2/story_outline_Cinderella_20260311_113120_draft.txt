## Visual Novel Title
Tides of Brinehaven

---

## World View
Brinehaven, 2035: a narrow coastal town pushed to the margin by rising seas and fiercer storms. Once a patchwork of family docks and fishmongers’ porches, the town now wears sandbags, patched murals, and municipal notices like armor. Governance is municipal and tactile—town meetings beneath the Watchhouse beams, decisions argued over tea and tide charts rather than anonymous boardrooms. The culture prizes mutual aid and craft knowledge; religion is diffuse, leaning toward ritualized sea-memories and memorial wakes, and class tensions flicker between longshore families and new investors. Conflict centers on adaptation: grassroots living-shorelines and small retrofits championed by community activists versus engineered, capital-driven seawalls and relocation developments pushed by corporate Urbanworks. Geography is intimate—marsh fringes, a scarred dunehouse, a weathered pier, and Valence Urbanworks’ gleaming tower on the horizon. The atmosphere is wind-swept, melancholic-hopeful: salty air, golden-hour light, and the sense that every sunset reframes what must be saved. Technology is uneven—drones and AR for surveys sit beside hand-stitched patches and enamel compasses. Storytellers here measure value in lineage, livelihoods, and whether a design keeps people tied to place, or pulls them apart.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Lena Maris: pragmatic, empathetic, and anchored to Brinehaven by loss and duty. The municipal climate office is my battleground. Jonah Reyes, with his sand-streaked animator of living shorelines, and Iris Valence, in her clinical steel-blue suits and corporate AR cuff, press opposing visions. The arc is Cinderella: an initial rise as Lena’s community-led pilots win momentum; a sudden, crushing fall when a storm and political pressure reveal costs and fractures; and a final rise where Lena forges a durable victory—different in flavor across branches: community-first success, negotiated compromise, or systemic change via a coalition. The branches explore how love, ambition, and ethics bend under climate pressure: aligning with Jonah deepens intimacy and grassroots wins; aligning with Iris offers scale and moral compromise; brokering a hybrid path tests stamina and creativity. Every choice is tethered to place—wet mud, salt-stained jackets, enamel compass pendants—and every victory is hard-won, layered with trade-offs. The visual novel keeps everything in first-person, tactile and immediate, as I reckon with love, duty, and what stewardship truly costs.

Notes on Structure and Emotional Curve:
- Global arc (Cinderella: Rise → Fall → Rise) is enforced on every path: Chapters 1–4/7/10 are the rising phase; Chapters 5/8/11 are the fall/crisis nodes; Chapters 6/9/12/13/14/15 are restorative rises (different flavors). Every chapter header contains [Rise] or [Fall], with Valence and Arousal declared.
- Branching: Major split at Chapter 3, then additional splits at Chapters 5, 8, and 11 (three distinct split points beyond the common split).
- Depth: Any path from Chapter 1 to an ending is exactly six chapters (1→2→3→branch start→split→ending), satisfying the Estimated Depth constraint.
- All chapters remain strictly first-person, include visual traits (hair, eyes, clothing, accessories), and use only provided characters and locations.
- No new main characters introduced; secondary characters appear only in their specified roles.
