## Visual Novel Title
Tidebound Promises

---

## World View
Solace Harbor is a near-future, mid-latitude coastal city reshaped by decades of sea-level rise and intensifying storms. Once-distinct neighborhoods now stitch themselves together across stilts, reclaimed rooftops, and raised causeways. Political power centers around a coalition of municipal agencies, emergent community collectives, and multinational resilience firms such as Aegis Corporation. Class and place are tightly bound: the economic core — bank towers and executive terraces — sits behind hardened infrastructure proposals; older dockside districts house multi-generational residents who claim place-based knowledge and informal economies. Religion and ritual persist in neighborhood assemblies and seaside offerings, while a transactional civic religiosity attends grand infrastructure unveilings.

Culture favors pragmatic repair, barter, and adaptive design: rooftop gardens, rain-harvesting markets, and "guerrilla greening." Conflict fractures around questions of scale and equity: do you build monolithic engineered defenses to protect the economic hub quickly, or do you invest in slower, community-led, ecologically integrated adaptations that risk short-term exposure but preserve social fabric? Geography blends salt and concrete — reed beds greening a sea wall, neon reflected on flooded pavements, cranes and tide-logged boats. Atmosphere is melancholic but not defeated: damp salt air, watercolor fog, and the daily rituals of care that promise renewal.

---

## General Plot Overview - Plot Outline
I am Mira Solace, a municipal resilience scientist rooted in the Old Pier Neighborhood. In a rising-arc story of steady progress, I navigate technical plans, community grief, and a rekindled romance with Arin Voss as Solace Harbor must choose how to survive and who will survive with it. The pressure point is Aegis Corporation’s proposal — fast, engineered floodwalls and relocations — championed by Liora Kade. My work and my heart are on the line: I convene assemblies in the Tidehouse, sketch tidal gradients into my worn leather notebook, and argue for community-led, ecologically integrated adaptations with craft and evidence. Arin, with his patched sailcloth jacket and battered camera, galvanizes grassroots energy. Samir mediates technical nuance; Etta grounds us in memory; Noah anchors the personal stakes.

Branches of choices let me pursue different kinds of success: scaling community-led solutions, negotiating hybrid policy wins that protect neighborhoods, or steering municipal processes to enable equitable relocation with restitution. Every branch advances upward: relationships deepen or transform into mature partnerships, community capacity grows, and Solace Harbor gains resilience in different forms. Even when compromises are costly, each route ends with strengthened institutions, repaired relationships, or new beginnings — small triumphs accumulated into real progress.
